# Three-Pass Revision Checklist

## How to Use
Run each pass SEPARATELY. Do not try to fix everything at once. Read your writing three times, each time looking for ONE thing.

---

## Pass 1: Unity
**Question:** Does every sentence serve my claim?

Check each sentence:
- [ ] Does this sentence support my thesis or a sub-claim? If not, CUT or MOVE it.
- [ ] Is this summary disguised as analysis? Apply the S-F-E test — does it reach Function or Effect?
- [ ] Does every body paragraph have a clear topic sentence (claim), not just a transition?
- [ ] Does my conclusion do more than restate my intro? Does it answer "So what?"

**If you find a drifting sentence:** Ask — does it belong in a different paragraph, or does it not belong at all?

---

## Pass 2: Coherence
**Question:** Does each sentence connect logically to the next?

Check the flow:
- [ ] Does each paragraph begin with a clear topic sentence that connects to the thesis?
- [ ] Is there a transition between every body paragraph? (Use the Transition Reference)
- [ ] Within each paragraph, does each sentence follow logically from the previous one?
- [ ] Are my S-F-E chains complete? (Structure named → Function explained → Effect connected to thesis)
- [ ] Can a reader follow my argument WITHOUT having to re-read?

**If you find a gap:** Add a transition word/phrase, or add a sentence that bridges the two ideas.

---

## Pass 3: Concision
**Question:** Can I say this in fewer words without losing meaning?

Hunt for these patterns:
- [ ] **"There is/There are" openers** — Rewrite with a real subject. ("There are many reasons" → "Three reasons explain...")
- [ ] **Unnecessary modifiers** — Cut "very," "really," "quite," "rather," "somewhat" unless they change meaning.
- [ ] **Deadwood phrases** — Replace wordy phrases with shorter ones. ("Due to the fact that" → "Because." "In order to" → "To." "At this point in time" → "Now.")
- [ ] **Passive voice** (when unjustified) — "The argument is made by Douglass" → "Douglass argues." Keep passive only when the receiver matters more than the actor.
- [ ] **Target: cut 15-20% of word count.** If your paragraph is 120 words, aim for 100.
