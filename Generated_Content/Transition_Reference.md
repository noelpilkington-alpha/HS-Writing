# Transition Reference -- Analytical & Argumentative Writing

Use this sheet as a quick-reference when drafting and revising analytical or argumentative essays. Choose transitions that match the **rhetorical move** you are making — not just to "sound formal."

---

## Transitions by Rhetorical Function

| Category | Transitions | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Adding Evidence** | Furthermore · In addition · Moreover · Similarly · Equally important · Along the same lines | Stacking a second or third piece of evidence that supports the same claim |
| **Cause & Effect** | Because · Therefore · Consequently · As a result · Thus · This suggests that · It follows that | Showing that one thing leads to or produces another; connecting reasoning to a claim |
| **Contrast & Concession** | However · Nevertheless · Although · While · On the other hand · Despite · Conversely · Granted that · Yet | Acknowledging a counterargument, introducing a pivot, or qualifying your claim |
| **Emphasis & Intensification** | Indeed · In fact · Most importantly · Above all · Crucially · Especially | Signaling that this point carries the most weight in your argument |
| **Example & Illustration** | For instance · For example · Specifically · To illustrate · As demonstrated by · In particular | Introducing a concrete piece of evidence, quotation, or detail |
| **Sequence & Structure** | First / Second / Third · Initially · Subsequently · Finally · In the first place · Building on this | Organizing a multi-step argument or walking through evidence in order |
| **Conclusion & Synthesis** | Ultimately · In conclusion · Taken together · This reveals · The cumulative effect | Wrapping up a paragraph or essay; pulling multiple points into one insight |

---

## S-F-E Move Signals

Use these phrases to guide your reader through the **Structure → Function → Effect** analytical chain.

| Move | Signal Phrases |
|---|---|
| **S → F** (Structure to Function) | "This serves to..." · "By doing so..." · "The function of this is..." · "This works by..." |
| **F → E** (Function to Effect) | "Which suggests..." · "The effect is..." · "This ultimately..." · "As a result, the reader..." |
| **Direct to E** (Straight to significance) | "This matters because..." · "The significance is..." · "This reveals..." |

**Tip:** If your analysis paragraph feels like summary, check whether you have at least one F→E or Direct-to-E signal. That is where interpretation lives.
