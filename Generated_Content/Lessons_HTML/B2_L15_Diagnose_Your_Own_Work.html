Diagnose Your Own Work

Self-Diagnosis Protocol for Pattern Recognition

⏱ ~25 min 📍 KCs: C.HS.05 (Self-Diagnose at 80%+ Accuracy) 🧠 Metacognition
Self-Diagnosis = Expert-Level Metacognition. AP tutors don't just score your essay and say "that's a 4." They identify the SPECIFIC problem causing the 4 and prescribe targeted practice. Now you can do this yourself. The 5-step protocol transforms scoring into diagnosis: not just "what's my score?" but "what exactly is broken, and how do I fix it?"

Phase 1: Teach (3 min)

The Self-Diagnosis Protocol

Scoring your own essay tells you where you are. Diagnosing your own essay tells you how to improve. There's a crucial difference.

Scoring: "My Speed Run 2 essay scored 4/6. Row A = 1, Row B = 2, Row C = 1."

Diagnosis: "My Speed Run 2 essay scored 4/6 because my second body paragraph feature-spots without reaching Effect—I identify strategies but don't explain their impact on the audience. Prescription: drill S-F-E commentary, focusing on completing the Effect step."

The 5-Step Self-Diagnosis Protocol:

  1. Score your essay on Rows A, B, C (use calibrated judgment from L14)
  2. Identify the weakest row (where did you lose the most points?)
  3. Identify the SPECIFIC problem within that row:
    • Row A weakness: No line of reasoning? Vague thesis? Mismatch between thesis and body paragraphs?
    • Row B weakness: Feature-spotting without analysis? No S-F-E Effect step? Vague evidence? Summary instead of analysis?
    • Row C weakness: No sophistication move attempted? Sophistication tacked on (not organic)? Historical fact mistaken for broader context?
  4. Write a 2-sentence diagnosis: "My essay scored [X] because [specific problem in specific location]. This prevents me from earning [next score point] because [what the rubric requires]."
  5. Prescribe ONE practice target: "To fix this, I need to practice [specific skill]."

Example diagnosis (strong): "My essay scored 4 because my thesis previews strategies but doesn't preview reasoning—it lists 'irony and contrast' without explaining how these strategies achieve the writer's purpose. This prevents me from earning full Row A credit because the AP rubric requires a line of reasoning, not just a strategy list. To fix this, I need to practice thesis revision: for each strategy, add 'to [purpose] by [how it works].'"

Example diagnosis (weak): "My essay scored 4 because Row B is weak. I need to practice analysis." ← Too vague. What SPECIFICALLY is weak in Row B? Where in the essay? What type of practice?

Key insight: A single essay's diagnosis might be unreliable (maybe you had a bad day). A pattern diagnosis across THREE essays reveals your consistent weakness—that's what you practice.

Pattern Check

Compare Speed Run 1 and Speed Run 2 diagnoses. Is your weakest row the same in both essays?

  1. Yes — the same row is weak in both essays (pattern detected)
  2. No — different rows are weak (weakness is essay-type-specific or inconsistent)

If yes: You've identified your practice priority. That row is your consistent weakness across essay types.

If no: You need the 3-essay pattern diagnosis (next phase) to see if a pattern emerges.

Pattern Questions:

  1. Which ROW is consistently weakest across all 3 essays? (If 2 or 3 essays have the same weakest row, that's your pattern)
  2. Which ESSAY TYPE scored lowest? (If one essay type is significantly weaker, that's format-specific weakness)
  3. Did you improve from Speed Run 1 to Speed Run 3? (If scores increased, you're already self-correcting)
Write Your 3-Sentence Pattern Diagnosis

Synthesize across all 3 Speed Runs:

"Across my three Speed Run essays, my consistent weakness is [Row A/B/C] because [pattern you observed]. This appears as [specific manifestation] in rhetorical analysis, [manifestation] in argument, and [manifestation] in synthesis. My practice priority is [the ONE thing that would fix this pattern]."

Common Patterns & Prescriptions:

  • Pattern: Row A consistently 0-1, Row B strong → Practice thesis writing with line of reasoning. Your analysis is good but your thesis doesn't preview it.
  • Pattern: Row B consistently 1-2 → Practice S-F-E commentary. You're feature-spotting without reaching Effect. Drill: for every strategy, write 2 sentences explaining impact on audience.
  • Pattern: Row C consistently 0 → Practice sophistication moves. Plan one sophistication move (broader context, alternative reading, or tension) during your 5-minute planning phase.
  • Pattern: Synthesis scores 2+ points lower than other types → Practice source integration and synthesis planning. You may be summarizing sources instead of synthesizing them.
  • Pattern: Quality drops in third body paragraph across all essays → Practice timing allocation. You're spending too long on early paragraphs. Set 11-minute timers for each body paragraph.

Phase 4: Transfer (2 min)

Your pattern diagnosis is your practice plan. You now know exactly what to practice between now and the B2 gate. Not "get better at writing"—that's too vague. Practice the specific skill that fixes your pattern weakness.

  • If Row A is your pattern weakness: Write 10 thesis statements this week, each with a clear line of reasoning. Score each one. Revise until you consistently earn Row A = 1.
  • If Row B is your pattern weakness: Write 10 body paragraphs with S-F-E analysis. For each, check: did I reach Effect? Did I explain impact on audience, not just identify strategy?
  • If Row C is your pattern weakness: Read 5 essays (from L14 calibration or online sample essays). Identify the sophistication move in each. Then write 5 sophistication moves for passages you've analyzed before.
  • If one essay TYPE is consistently weak: Write 3 additional timed essays in that format before the gate.

Key insight: The pattern diagnosis you wrote today is more valuable than any single essay score. It identifies what you need to practice—and that knowledge is what allows independent improvement.

Gate Preparation

How will you use your pattern diagnosis to prepare for the B2 gate assessment? Write a specific practice plan: "Between now and the gate, I will practice [specific skill] by [specific method] to fix my [pattern weakness]."
