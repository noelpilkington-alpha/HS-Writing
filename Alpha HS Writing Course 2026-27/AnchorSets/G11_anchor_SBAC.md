### 2. What the released form contains — VERIFIED (Grade 11 practice-test PT scoring guide + adjusted blueprint)
**Two components.**

**(A) The Performance Task (PT) — the full-write essay anchor.** Verified structure of the Grade-11 practice PT:
- **A single set of 4 source texts** (labeled Source #1–#4) on one research topic (released practice topic: financial-literacy education — named as a label only). *Consortium spec: PTs carry **2–5 sources depending on grade**; the released G11 practice form uses **4**.*
- **A "classroom activity"** (teacher-facilitated, ~30 min, non-scored) precedes the task to build shared context/vocabulary before students read the sources and write.
- **3 research (Claim 4) short-response items** on the sources, then **1 full-write essay** on the same source set. Verified the three research SR items test, in order: (1) **evaluate the relevance** of information across sources (0–2), (2) **integrate/paraphrase** information while avoiding plagiarism — explain how one source refutes another (0–2), (3) **cite evidence** by matching claims to sources in a table (0–1).
- **The full-write** = one essay, scored on three traits (below). Prompt fixes purpose, audience, and form ("write an argument…"). At Grade 11 the purpose is **argument OR informational/explanatory only — NO narrative** (confirmed: "students in grade 11 will not receive a narrative PT"). Argument at G11 requires **claims AND counterclaims**.
- No fixed word count is published; the response is an on-demand multi-paragraph essay in an untimed-within-session online box. (SBAC does not print a word-count target — the length band is inferred from the anchor essays, roughly 400–900 words for on-grade G11 argument.)

**(B) The CAT — Claim 2 Writing items (the editing/revising SR anchor).** Verified from the Grade-11 blueprint: the whole Grade-11 ELA CAT + PT ≈ **~40–41 items total (~76 score points)** across 4 claims. **Claim 2 (Writing) = 4 CAT items + the PT full-write.** The 4 CAT writing items are:
| CAT writing target | Reporting category | Items | DOK | What it tests |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1a/3a/6a: Write Brief Texts** & **1b/3b/6b: Revise Brief Texts** | Organization/Purpose | 1 | 2–3 | draft/revise a brief passage for organization + purpose |
| **1a/3a/6a: Write Brief Texts** & **1b/3b/6b: Revise Brief Texts** | Evidence/Elaboration | 1 | 2 | draft/revise a brief passage for evidence + elaboration (of the two Write/Revise items, exactly one is a *Write* Brief Text) |
| **8: Language and Vocabulary Use** | Evidence/Elaboration | 1 | 1–2 | precise word choice / register (contributes one item to Evidence/Elaboration) |
| **9: Edit/Clarify** | Conventions | 1 | 1–2 | fix conventions errors (grammar/usage/mechanics) in a short given passage |
- **Note on the task's "targets 8/9" framing:** verified target numbering = **Target 8 = Language and Vocabulary Use** and **Target 9 = Edit/Clarify**. "Revise Brief Texts" is **Target 1b/3b/6b** (not 8/9). All three are short, machine-scored CAT items; item shells are edit/clarify (hot-text/inline-choice correction) and short-answer "brief write/revise."

### 3. Exact rubric (as applied to the PT full-write) — VERIFIED (byte-read, both modes)
**Analytic, three traits, applied to one essay response; argument and explanatory share the same trait skeleton (mode-specific descriptors).** Grade band on the official rubric: **"Grades 6–High School"** for Organization/Purpose and Evidence/Elaboration; **"Grades 3–11"** for Conventions.
| Trait | Scale | Notes (verbatim-cited to the rubric) |
|---|---|---|
| **Organization/Purpose** | **4–1** (+ NS) | 4 = "clear and effective organizational structure… fully sustained… consistently and purposefully focused." Argument descriptor scores claim clarity, transitions, intro/conclusion, logical progression, and **"alternate and opposing argument(s) are… acknowledged or addressed"** (footnote: *"Acknowledging and/or addressing the opposing point of view begins at grade 7."*) |
| **Evidence/Elaboration** | **4–1** (+ NS) | 4 = "thorough and convincing elaboration… reasoned, in-depth analysis and the effective use of source material… precise language." Scores integration/relevance/specificity of source evidence, citation/attribution, elaborative techniques, and vocabulary. |
| **Conventions** | **2–0** (+ NS) | 2 = "adequate command of conventions" (sentence formation, punctuation, capitalization, grammar usage, spelling). Scored **holistically** on Variety × Severity × Density of errors. |
- **Max full-write raw = 10** (4 + 4 + 2). **NS** = insufficient/copied text, non-English, off-topic, or off-purpose.
- The two open-ended writing traits carry the weight (8 of 10); Conventions is a light 0–2 band — the design **weights ideas/organization/evidence over mechanics**.

### 5. Modeling note (replicate WITHOUT copying)
- **Source-set shape our G11 synthesis stimuli MUST match:** a **single 4-source set** on one debatable research question, mixed genres (informational articles + at least one that supplies data/examples), preceded by a shared-context "classroom activity." Model our G11 argument/synthesis prompts as **4-source, single-topic** sets — NOT single-passage (that's the G10 STAAR/MCAS shape).
- **Purpose set:** author **argument (claim + counterclaim) and informational/explanatory** only at G11. **Do NOT author a G11 narrative PT** — SBAC explicitly excludes it at 11.
- **Prompt phrasing pattern:** motivating scenario → 4 sources → 3 research warm-up items (relevance / paraphrase-integrate / cite-evidence) → one full-write with fixed purpose, audience, form, and the rubric shown to the student. Mirror this "research-then-write" scaffold.
- **Rubric to score against:** **Organization/Purpose 4–1 · Evidence/Elaboration 4–1 · Conventions 2–0 (max 10).** This is the finer 4-point idea scale (like Ohio/PARCC) rather than STAAR's 0–3; counterargument credit starts inside Organization/Purpose. Mirror the 8:2 ideas-to-mechanics weighting.
- **CAT editing/revising template to replicate:** short, discrete, machine-scored items — **Edit/Clarify** (fix conventions in a given passage), **Language and Vocabulary Use** (precise word choice), and **Write/Revise Brief Texts** (improve a short passage's organization or evidence). Unlike STAAR's dedicated 2-revise + 2-edit passage section, SBAC delivers these as **~4 adaptive standalone items**, not a passage set.
- **Difficulty markers:** a top (4/4) G11 argument shows sustained cross-source synthesis, counterclaim handling, precise citation/attribution, and controlled style; the drop to 2 is "uneven/cursory… simplistic language… source summary rather than analysis." Use the Full-Writes baseline anchors to calibrate our "on-grade" vs "approaching" G11 exemplars.
