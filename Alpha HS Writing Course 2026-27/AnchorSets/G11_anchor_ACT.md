## SYSTEM — ACT Writing (optional essay) ✅ FULL PUBLIC ANCHOR (structure + rubric + annotated samples all public)

Governance: ACT (nonprofit, now ACT/Nurturing Learning). The Writing test is an **optional add-on** to the ACT; it does not affect the multiple-choice subject scores or the Composite. Typically taken in **Grade 11**. ACT publishes the task description, the analytic scoring rubric, and a full set of scored sample essays with scorer commentary as open web pages.

### 2. What the task IS — VERIFIED (Writing description page)
- **One prompt, 40 minutes.** Verbatim: the section is "a 40-minute essay test." A single writing prompt; the student produces one unified essay.
- **Structure = an ISSUE + THREE GIVEN PERSPECTIVES.** The prompt presents a complex contemporary issue and, verbatim, "provides three different perspectives on the issue." These are short given viewpoints written into the prompt.
- **NO source passage / NO reading text to cite.** Unlike every G10 state anchor (all source-based), ACT Writing supplies only the issue framing + the three perspective statements. There is no article, data set, or passage set to quote — evidence comes from the student's own knowledge, reasoning, and examples. This is the defining structural difference from our source-bound G9/G10 anchors.
- **What the student must do:** (a) develop and clearly state their OWN perspective on the issue; (b) **analyze the relationship between their own perspective and at least one of the given perspectives** (verbatim: the essay "must analyze the relationship between your own perspective and one or more other perspectives"); (c) support ideas with reasoning and examples; (d) organize logically; (e) communicate in standard written English. The student may fully agree with a given perspective, partially agree, or take a wholly different position — **the stance chosen does not affect the score.**

### 3. Exact rubric (as officially published) — VERIFIED (understanding-your-scores page + description page)
**Analytic, FOUR domains.** Each essay is scored on each domain **1–6 by two trained readers**; the two readers' scores are **summed to a 2–12 domain score**; the reported **subject-level Writing score (2–12) is the rounded average of the four domain scores.** If the two readers disagree by more than one point on a domain, a **third reader resolves** it. (Online essays are scored by an automated engine, with human review of low-confidence essays and QA samples; state/district/international administrations may score differently.)

| Domain (exact ACT name) | Per-reader scale | Combined domain scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Ideas and Analysis** | 1–6 | 2–12 |
| **Development and Support** | 1–6 | 2–12 |
| **Organization** | 1–6 | 2–12 |
| **Language Use and Conventions** | 1–6 | 2–12 |

- Reported score = **subject-level Writing 2–12** = rounded average of the four 2–12 domain scores (not a sum). ACT notes the average writing score is roughly 6–7.
- Domain focus (own words): **Ideas and Analysis** = quality of the thesis + critical engagement with the multiple perspectives; **Development and Support** = reasoning, rationale, and examples that reinforce the argument; **Organization** = clear, purposeful arrangement of ideas; **Language Use and Conventions** = grammar, syntax, usage, mechanics, and appropriate style/tone.

### 4. Annotated / scored sample essays — YES, at EVERY score point 1–6, with per-domain commentary
Verified (sample-essays page, via reader proxy): ACT publishes, for one official prompt ("Intelligent Machines"), **six scored sample essays — one at each score point from 1 to 6** — each followed by a **"Score Explanation"** that addresses **all four scoring domains** (Ideas and Analysis, Development and Support, Organization, Language Use and Conventions). Headings run "Score Explanation – Score 1" through "Score 6." This is a **full per-score-point annotated anchor set**, functionally equivalent to the state anchor-paper sets in the G10 file — the key difference is that ACT publishes **one prompt's worth** of samples (a single canonical form) rather than a new released form each year.
- ⚠ Caveat: the six sample essays illustrate the **holistic single-rater 1–6 band** per domain (they are teaching exemplars); they are not a fresh multi-form released bank. For a commercial bank this is sufficient calibration for the 1–6 band; it will not give year-over-year prompt variety.

### 5. Modeling note (replicate WITHOUT copying)
- **Task shape to match:** an **issue + exactly THREE short given perspectives**, then a directive to (1) stake the student's own position and (2) **analyze the relationship between that position and at least one given perspective.** Model our G11 argument prompts on this "given-perspectives" shell — NOT on a source-passage shell. The intellectual move being tested is *evaluating and positioning among competing viewpoints*, not *citing a text*.
- **NO source passage.** Our G11 multi-perspective items must supply the perspectives inside the prompt and require the student to argue from their own knowledge + reasoning. Do not attach a reading selection to items authored against this anchor (that would make them Regents/STAAR-shaped, not ACT-shaped).
- **Rubric to mirror:** four analytic domains — **Ideas and Analysis · Development and Support · Organization · Language Use and Conventions** — each on a **1–6** scale (report 2–12 subject-level as the rounded 4-domain average). Weight **Ideas and Analysis** heavily: engagement with the *multiple perspectives* (not just the student's own claim) is the ACT-distinctive scoring driver. Build our top-band descriptors around "generates a nuanced own-perspective AND analyzes its relationship to the given perspectives (tensions, implications, underlying assumptions)."
- **Difficulty markers (from the 1–6 samples):** the climb from a mid-band (3) to top-band (5–6) essay is defined less by more examples and more by **depth of engagement between perspectives** — precise analysis of tension/implication/assumption across viewpoints, plus purposeful organization and controlled, varied language. Use ACT's per-score-point Score Explanations to calibrate our "on-grade / approaching / exemplary" G11 exemplars.
- **Timing to mirror:** a **40-minute single-essay** budget — model our G11 timed-argument practice on a ~40-minute planning-to-draft window for one multi-perspective essay.

## Enhanced-ACT flag (current test format) — §4 context

The ACT was redesigned ("enhanced ACT") with a shorter core and Science moved to optional. **Byte-verified this session** from the live `act.org` test page: current sections are **English 35 min / 50 q, Math 50 min / 45 q, Reading 40 min / 36 q, Science 40 min / 40 q (Optional Add-On), Writing 40 min / 1 prompt (Optional Add-On).** These shortened counts + Science-optional status ARE the enhanced format, so the enhanced ACT is confirmed live.
- **Effect on Writing:** none material. Writing **remains a 40-minute, single-prompt, optional essay** with the same three-perspective task and the same four-domain 2–12 rubric. The enhancement changed the multiple-choice core (and made Science optional), not the Writing task structure or its rubric.
- ⚠ **Not byte-verified this session:** the specific rollout DATES of the enhanced ACT (the official enhancement/newsroom announcement URLs all returned HTTP 404). Do not cite a rollout date without re-verifying from a live ACT announcement page; the *current live format* above is what is verified.
