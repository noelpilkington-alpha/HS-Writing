# G9-12 Sentence-Skill Progression Map (VERIFIED)

**Status:** VERIFIED (source-triangulated, cell-by-cell). Supersedes the provisional draft cut in the spec (`docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-08-cross-grade-sentence-progression-and-course-arc.md` §B).
**Date:** 2026-07-08
**Basis:** Every cell is justified against at least one of four sources: (1) the state-test crosswalk (`skills_by_grade_crosswalk.html` + `TestDesign_Reference.md`), (2) TWR's documented skill sequence (`personas/01-twr-hochman-wexler.md`), (3) CCSS/ACC grade-band standards (`05_AlphaCommonCore_Writing_Spine.md` + `01_ccss_adherence_map.md`), and (4) the Council of Writing Instruction, convened in-session 2026-07-08, only where the first three conflicted. Full evidence trail: `_evidence/sentence_skill_sources.md`.

## How to read this map

- **NEW @ Grade** = the grade that OWNS the skill. It gets a full lesson (Teach through Transfer) here, taught to automaticity.
- **INHERITED at** = every later grade that assumes the skill. Inherited skills get a fast retrieval CHECK at entry, NOT a re-teaching lesson. Pass and proceed; fail and the platform routes remediation to the owning grade. This is the expertise-reversal guard: re-teaching an automated skill to a more-expert student depresses learning (d approximately -0.428, ExpertiseReversal2025).
- **Two layers.** Layer 1 = genre-GENERAL mechanics (transfer to every strand, taught once front-loaded). Layer 2 = genre-SPECIFIC sentence moves (taught just-in-time, immediately before that genre's paragraph phase).
- G9 is the course entry floor. Its Layer-1 substrate is taught outright (nothing below it to route remediation to), so G9 has no inherited Layer-1 mechanics by design.

**Layer-1 finding confirmed:** G10 has **no genre-general mechanics that are novel in kind**. Its Layer-1 news (appositives, parallelism, modifier placement, basic precision) are refinements/extensions of the G9 substrate, several of them conventions-level editing targets. A G10 course that re-taught combining/expansion/because-but-so would be redundant. G9 and G11 carry the thick Layer-1 phases; G10's Layer-1 phase is deliberately thin.

## What changed from the spec's provisional draft

The spec's §B table was a triangulated hypothesis. Verification moved these cells:

**Layer 1:**
1. **Appositives: G9 to G10.** The draft folded appositives into the G9 substrate. TWR sequences appositives AFTER combining (an intermediate skill), and the crosswalk first names them (under "modifiers") at G10. Owning grade corrected to G10.
2. **Precision split from concision, precision landing at G10.** The draft lumped "concision, precision" together at G11. The council split them: basic precision is a G10 scaffolded-editing skill (2-3 elements); full stylistic concision is a G11 open-revision skill (5-7 elements). Precision moved down a grade; concision confirmed at G11.
3. **Controlling-idea / topic sentence added to Layer 1.** The draft placed it in Layer 2. It is genre-general (every paragraph has a controlling idea), so it moves to Layer 1 as a G9-owned skill. Its genre-specific realizations (claim, thesis, analytical assertion) remain as separate Layer-2 rows.
4. **"Complex combining" is not a separate G10 Layer-1 row.** The draft listed "complex combining" as G10-new. Combining is G9-NEW; the escalating complexity the G10 crosswalk shows is retrieval + application of the G9 skill to harder material, not a new mechanic. Folded into G9 combining (inherited/applied at G10), not a new row.
5. **because/but/so delivery bound to CER.** The draft did not specify delivery. Per Noel (2026-07-08), it is taught inside the claim-evidence-reasoning context, not as a standalone connective drill.

**Layer 2:**
6. **Controlling-idea sentence moved OUT to Layer 1** (see #3).
7. **"Nuanced/counterclaim-aware claim" split into two grades.** The draft treated it as ONE G10 skill. The council split it: counterclaim-aware claim (binary acknowledgment) is G10-NEW; fully nuanced claim (multi-dimensional qualification) is G11-NEW.

Everything else in the draft was confirmed by the sources.

## Internal consistency check (verified by hand)

- **No skill is NEW at two grades.** Each row has exactly one owning grade. PASS.
- **Every INHERITED entry points to a grade that OWNS the skill earlier.** All inherited grades are strictly greater than the owning grade. PASS.
- **G9 inherits no Layer-1 mechanics.** All six G9 Layer-1 rows are NEW (entry-grade substrate taught outright). PASS.
- **Split skills are non-overlapping.** Precision (G10) and concision (G11) are distinct skills at distinct grades; counterclaim-aware (G10) and nuanced (G11) are distinct skills at distinct grades. PASS.

**Row totals:** 13 Layer-1 skills + 8 Layer-2 skills = 21 verified sentence-skill placements.

**Ownership by grade:**
- **G9 owns:** expansion, combining, subordinating conjunctions (basic), fragment/run-on/comma-splice, because/but/so, controlling-idea sentence (Layer 1); claim sentence, attributed-evidence sentence (Layer 2). = 8 skills.
- **G10 owns:** appositives, parallelism, modifier placement, basic precision (Layer 1); counterclaim-aware claim, device-to-effect-to-warrant sentence (Layer 2). = 6 skills.
- **G11 owns:** concision + stylistic precision, rhetorical syntax (Layer 1); nuanced claim, cross-source/synthesis sentence, rhetorical-analysis sentence (Layer 2). = 5 skills.
- **G12 owns:** AP sophistication syntax (Layer 1); AP-level sophistication moves (Layer 2). = 2 skills.

This is the intended shape: thick at G9 (foundational substrate), thick-ish at G11 (the rhetorical/synthesis turn), thin at G10 (genre-specific news, not mechanics) and G12 (AP depth refinement atop G11).
