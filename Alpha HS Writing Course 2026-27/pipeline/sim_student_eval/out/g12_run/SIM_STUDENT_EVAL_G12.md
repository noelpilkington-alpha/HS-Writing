## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Do the lessons make sense?

Verdict: the individual concepts are consistently clear to both personas; the confusion points are almost all structural (broken steps, missing scaffolds, contradictions, sequencing), not conceptual. Per lesson:

- **l01_situate_context: clear for both walks.** Both personas could execute the move and the 3-question check. average_fable left with a real, legitimate worry the lesson never resolves: "would an actual grader agree my invented frames are genuinely larger... self-checking with the 3 questions can fool you and there's no one to verify" (conf 0.4 on grader judgment). The lesson also plants a claim l04 later contradicts (see below).

- **l02_genuine_complexity: concept clear, one step broken (CORROBORATED, both fable personas).** Step 5 asked a multiple-choice question with NO answer options rendered. achiever_fable: "the prompt asked which sentence shows genuine complexity but no answer options were provided, so I couldn't actually answer it." average_fable: "the multiple choice had no answer options shown." This is a defect, not a difficulty. Also, Step 8 runs the check FOR the student before asking them to run it (achiever_fable: "like being handed the answer key first"), a pattern that recurs in l03 Step 7, l12 Step 8, and l15 Step 8.

- **l03_hold_tension: clear.** Both personas executed the pivot and the landing rule. Confusion is limited to assessment design: distractors containing the exact trap words "weak" and "sentimental" from Step 3 (achiever_fable Step 4: "I didn't have to think"; average_fable: "2-for-2 doesn't tell me much").

- **l04_sophisticated_argument: clear, and it creates a cross-lesson contradiction (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable).** average_fable: "Lesson 1 sort of implied the thesis move was the point-earner, but this lesson says sophistication has to run through the whole piece, which version should I trust?" The course never reconciles this.

- **l05_sophisticated_analysis: clear.** Genre transfer landed for both. average_fable found the SITUATION-first planning order genuinely new but was unsure it could override the device-first habit (conf 0.5).

- **l06_sophisticated_synthesis: the single worst clarity failure in the course (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable).** The two moves the lesson exists to teach, "synthesize" and "weight," "get one sentence each and were never actually taught; I don't know what weighting looks like on the page (say 'Source 2 matters most'? give it more space?)," conf 0.2 on both. Additionally, Step 3 says "3 sources" but the material "reads like one continuous article with three section headings, I had to guess my own mapping with no way to verify." The definition of weighting only arrives in l11 ("relief, because this lesson finally answers something I flagged after lesson 6"), five lessons late. achiever_fable did not report this confusion, so it is persona-specific, but it is exactly the persona a G12 course must serve.

- **l07_calibrate_sophistication: concept clear, mechanism broken (CORROBORATED, both personas).** Step 2 instructs "SUBMIT to the grader" and "READ THE REVEAL" in a solo self-paced course with no grader. achiever_fable: "the whole calibration routine hinges on it." average_fable conf 0.3 on "knowing whether an actual grader will verify my prediction." Flagged again at l11, l14 (where Step 10 "quietly admits the second opinion is my own reread," achiever_fable), never fixed.

- **l08_sustain_full_essay: clear idea, two internal contradictions.** (a) "Practice without a clock" vs Step 2's "keep a phone timer in view" (SINGLE-MODEL, achiever_fable; average_fable independently found no-clock practice "backwards" and added its own timer, so the underlying clock ambiguity is CORROBORATED). (b) Step 1 claims the student "already owns" budgeting and fast-planning; average_fable checked its notes and disagreed: "lessons 1-7 never taught either" (PERSONA-SPECIFIC).

- **l09_timed_argument: clear but underspecified (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable).** Step 2 gives no actual minute numbers for a 40-minute FRQ: "I had to guess what a realistic AP timing is." This gap persists through l14 ("roughly the fourth lesson managing budget pressure with my own invented 7/27/6 split and no way to know if it's right"). Credit: Step 2's "Delivery here has no clock; the budget is your discipline" resolved l08's clock contradiction for achiever_fable.

- **l10_timed_analysis, l11_timed_synthesis: clear.** l11's weighting definition is the payoff for l06's gap. Both walks note the teach steps are mostly recap (see Q2). average_fable's l11 narration cut off mid-Step 5, so the back half of that lesson is unverified for that persona.

- **l12_which_frq_type: content trivially clear, placement makes no sense (CORROBORATED, both personas).** achiever_fable: "this lesson teaches how to tell the three FRQ types apart AFTER lessons 9-11 already had me write full timed essays in all three types, it should have come before lesson 9." average_fable: "the sequencing feels backwards." Also Step 7 re-pastes the full source set already read in Step 2 (achiever_fable), and Steps 7-9 repeat the same planning task three times at shrinking scaffold with no added difficulty (average_fable).

- **l13_interleaved_pair: the new idea (move bleed) is clear; half of Step 1 re-teaches l12 and even re-defines what a thesis is (achiever_fable: "I learned what a thesis is years ago"). Step 1 promises "rehearse two different prompts" and achiever_fable questioned whether the lesson actually delivers two prompts (SINGLE-MODEL, unresolved).**

- **l14_calibrate_timed_write: clear except the persistent grader fiction (CORROBORATED)** and a missing prerequisite (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable): "No lesson ever taught the difference between Evidence 2 and Evidence 3 on the real rubric, so I'll be guessing at the boundaries."

- **l15_voice_syntax: the move (subordinate, land the point last) is clear and both personas executed it cold. Gaps: the lesson never addresses when a shaped sentence becomes over-shaped/cluttered, or whether splitting into two sentences is valid (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable, conf 0.4 on over-shaping). average_fable also asked why a sentence-level lesson comes after all the timed full-essay lessons (placement question, SINGLE-MODEL). Step 8 prints its own check and sample fix, then asks the student to produce a variant (achiever_fable: "what is the step actually assessing when it does its own work?").**

- **l16_gate_frq_section: framing undercuts itself (CORROBORATED in spirit).** Step 1 says "run the checklist from memory" then prints the type-tell checklist above the test. achiever_fable: "like taping the formula sheet to the exam and calling it closed-book." average_fable: "a budget with no clock is a pretend budget," and there is no lesson anywhere that trains against a real timer. Credit where due: the gate is honest that repetition is intentional and that the untimed choice is deliberate, which achiever_fable explicitly praised as "the transparency I've been asking for since lesson 7."

## 3. Do the lessons prepare them for the full compositions?

## Q3: Do the lessons prepare students for the full compositions?

The composition probes on record are l04, l05, l06, l07, l08, l09, l10, l11, l13, l14, and l16 (the walks did not surface separate probes labeled l18/l23/l24/l26/l27; the evidence covers the composition tasks embedded in these lessons). Findings per probe, traced to prior lessons:

### g12_l04_sophisticated_argument (full argument essay)
- **In place (CORROBORATED, both personas, fable):** situating (l01), naming the cost (l02), holding tension and landing on a rule (l03) were all available; both students planned sophistication first and assembled the moves. achiever_fable converted percentages to absolute bases (l01 skill) as real evidence; average_fable reused the 33.5%-of-246k reading and its own "winds down / phases up" rule from l02.
- **Not in place (CORROBORATED):** the ability to write a genuinely original essay was compromised by design, not by missing skills. The Step 3 source was the same prepare-vs-protect passage from l01 and l03, and Step 4's worked example was a pre-written answer key on the same source. achiever_fable "had to deliberately steer away from its training-first rule to avoid cloning it"; average_fable was "only about 0.5 confident a real grader would see my flipped rule as genuinely mine." The composition tested recall on a memorized source, not transfer.

### g12_l05_sophisticated_analysis (full rhetorical analysis)
- **In place (CORROBORATED):** situating and tension moves transferred from l01/l03 to analyzing Douglass; both walks identified the speaker's bind. achiever_fable inferred the bind before the lesson named it.
- **Not in place (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable):** sustaining the move past the opening. average_fable: "my real risk isn't picking the device-hunt option; it's writing an essay that opens like the sophisticated answer and then decays into device-hunting by body paragraph two (my 0.4 confidence on not abandoning the pivot)." Nothing before l05 trained sustaining analysis across paragraphs; that skill only arrives at l08 (sustain_full_essay), after this composition demanded it.
- **Not in place (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable):** situation-first planning conflicted with an untrained habit (device-first), self-rated 0.5 going in.

### g12_l06_sophisticated_synthesis (full synthesis essay)
- **In place:** situate, tension, base-rate statistics (l01-l03).
- **Not in place (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable, severe):** the two moves the composition actually turns on, weaving and weighting, "get one sentence each and were never actually taught; I don't know what weighting looks like." Self-rated confidence 0.2 on both synthesis mechanics and weighting at the moment of the composition. average_fable also could not confirm the source mapping ("Step 3 says 3 sources but it reads like one article with three headings"). The clarification only arrives in l11. achiever_fable handled it (conf 0.8 on weaving), so this is a persona gap: the curriculum's one-sentence treatment of synthesis mechanics is enough for a high achiever and not for an average student.

### g12_l07_calibrate_sophistication (essay plus self-prediction)
- **In place (CORROBORATED):** the three assembled moves (l01, l02, l03, l06) and the Row C criterion.
- **Not in place (CORROBORATED, both personas):** the calibration loop itself cannot be completed. The lesson's SUBMIT/READ THE REVEAL step assumes a grader. achiever_fable: "I'm doing this course alone, there's no grader reveal for me to read, and the whole calibration routine hinges on it." average_fable: "I don't know if there's an actual grader that returns anything" (conf 0.3 on getting verification). The composition's core skill, comparing prediction to result, is structurally unexecutable.

### g12_l08_sustain_full_essay (timed full essay)
- **Prerequisite dispute (CORROBORATED in opposite directions, which is itself a finding):** Step 1 claims budgeting and fast-planning are "two moves you already own." achiever_fable partially agreed (mapping to l07 plan-first work); average_fable flatly disagreed: "lessons 1-7 never taught either, all three moves were actually new to me, and Step 2 had to teach them from scratch." The curriculum's prerequisite claim is wrong for the average persona.
- **Not in place (CORROBORATED):** clock discipline. The lesson says "practice without a clock" and also "keep a phone timer in view" (achiever_fable flagged the contradiction; average_fable added its own phone timer "so it wouldn't be pretend").

### g12_l09/l10/l11 (timed argument, analysis, synthesis compositions)
- **In place:** assembly of moves, pacing skeleton (l08).
- **Not in place (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable):** realistic timing numbers. l09 Step 2 "gives no actual numbers for the time budget, I had to guess what a realistic AP timing is (7/27/6)" and this stays an open question through l14 and l16.
- **Not in place (SINGLE-MODEL, achiever_fable, l11):** the composition's one skill (capping the reading) could not be practiced honestly because "the source set is the same AI-workforce material for the fifth time; I could reconstruct the data from memory."
- **Not in place (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable, l10):** analyzing a choice the worked example already used ("fear itself") without copying, conf 0.4; the worked example on the same text as the composition created the problem.

### g12_l13/l14 (interleaved pair, calibrated timed write)
- **In place:** type-naming (l12), switch check, pacing, fade patterns.
- **Not in place (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable, l14):** rubric knowledge needed for the prediction composition: "No lesson ever taught the difference between Evidence 2 and Evidence 3 on the real rubric, so I'll be guessing at the boundaries" (conf 0.3 on that row).
- **Not in place (CORROBORATED):** originality on the recycled water source. average_fable l13: "My Step 6 thesis is the same floor-rule from lesson 3, not even flipped; I can't tell if I'm transferring the skill or reciting my old answer." achiever_fable l14 "had to deliberately steer away from my own Lesson 3 grid-trigger rule."

### g12_l16_gate_frq_section (final gate, three FRQs)
- **In place (CORROBORATED):** type identification on fresh sources, section budgeting on paper, base-rate trap catching on new grid data, weighting plan (average_fable executed a visible weighting plan; achiever_fable at 0.8-0.9 across gate skills).
- **Not in place (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable):** execution under a real clock: "A budget with no clock is a pretend budget... no lesson has ever made me practice against a real ticking timer," plus persistent doubt (0.6) whether a grader would recognize the on-page weighting. The gate itself was also compromised: the checklist that was supposed to be run "from memory" was printed above the test (achiever_fable), and average_fable's narration cut off mid-Step 4 with FRQs 2 and 3 not shown, so the gate never actually verified three complete essays for that persona.

### Bottom line
The three core sophistication moves (situate, cost, tension) are reliably in place by l04 for both personas. What is NOT reliably in place at composition time: synthesis/weighting mechanics before l06 (average), sustained-move endurance before l05 (average), grader verification for every calibration composition (both), realistic timing numbers (average), and, most damaging, the ability to demonstrate transfer at all, because nearly every composition reuses the same two memorized sources with an answer-key worked example attached (both).
