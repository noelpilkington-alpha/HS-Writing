## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Do the lessons make sense?

Overall, both walks (achiever_fable, average_fable) found the underlying skill sequence intelligible: situate (l01) → complexity (l02) → tension (l03) → assembly (l04) → analysis (l05) → synthesis (l06) → calibration (l07) → pacing (l08-l11) → type discipline (l12-l13) → timed calibration (l14) → syntax (l15) → gate (l16). But both walks logged specific breakages:

**Broken or self-contradicting steps**
- **l02 Step 5 is broken (CORROBORATED, both personas, one model).** Both walks report the multiple-choice step displayed no answer options. achiever_fable: "the prompt asked which sentence shows genuine complexity but no answer options were provided, so I couldn't actually answer it." average_fable additionally notes this cost them the harder test they wanted ("names the cost but ignores it").
- **l07 Step 2 SUBMIT/REVEAL loop references a grader that does not exist (CORROBORATED).** achiever_fable: "there's no grader reveal for me to read, and the whole calibration routine hinges on it." average_fable: "I'm working alone in a self-paced course and don't know if there's an actual grader that returns anything." The contradiction persists into l14, where achiever_fable notes Step 1 tells them to SUBMIT while Step 10 "quietly admits" the second opinion is their own reread. A calibration lesson whose feedback loop cannot close does not make sense as designed.
- **l08 contradicts itself on clocks (CORROBORATED in substance).** achiever_fable: "it says 'practice without a clock' but then Step 2 says keep a phone timer in view." average_fable independently had to add their own phone timer "so it wouldn't be pretend." The contradiction is only partially resolved in l09/l13, and average_fable flags at l16 that the course never resolved it structurally (no lesson practices against an enforced timer).
- **l08 Step 1 makes a false prerequisite claim for the average persona (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable).** The lesson says budgeting and fast-planning are "two moves you already own," but average_fable checked their notes: "lessons 1-7 never taught either, all three moves were actually new to me." For achiever_fable the claim roughly held. The lesson's model of prior knowledge fits one persona and not the other.
- **l06 under-teaches its own new content (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable).** The two genuinely new moves, synthesize and weight, "get one sentence each and were never actually taught; I don't know what weighting looks like (say 'Source 2 matters most'? give it more space?)." Confidence self-ratings of 0.2 on both. average_fable was also confused whether the "3 sources" were three sources or one article with section headings, with no way to verify. l11 partially repairs the weighting gap ("this lesson finally answers something I flagged after lesson 6"), but that means l06's core skill was unlearnable at the point it was assessed.
- **l09 Step 2 gives no actual timing numbers (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable).** "I had to guess what a realistic AP timing is... because no lesson ever gave me real numbers for a 40-minute FRQ." This open question is still unanswered at l14 ("roughly the fourth lesson managing 'budget pressure' with my own invented 7/27/6 split and no way to know if it's right").

**Sequencing that does not make sense**
- **l12 (which FRQ type) comes after l09-l11 (CORROBORATED, both personas).** average_fable: "like being taught to tie my shoes after running the race." achiever_fable: "it should have come before lesson 9." Both note l09-l11 already required knowing which type was which.
- **l15 (sentence syntax) comes after the timed full-essay lessons (SINGLE-MODEL, average_fable).** Open question logged at l15: "Why does a sentence-level syntax lesson come after the timed full-essay lessons instead of before?"
- **l01 vs l04 send mixed messages about what earns Row C (SINGLE-MODEL, average_fable).** l04 open question: "Lesson 1 sort of implied the thesis move was the point-earner, but this lesson says sophistication has to run through the whole piece, which version should I trust?"

**Assessment steps that don't assess (CORROBORATED, pervasive)**
- Multiple-choice distractors contain the exact trap words the teach step just named, so answers are keyword-matching, not judgment. achiever_fable flags this at l03 Steps 4-5, l04 Steps 5-6, l05 Steps 5-6, l07 Steps 5-6, l08 Steps 5-6, l09 Steps 5-6, l11 Steps 5-6, l13 Steps 4-5, l14 Steps 5-6, l15 Step 6. average_fable independently flags the same at l02, l03, l04, l05, l07, l08, l09, l10, l12 ("Step 6 was the easiest check in the entire course"), l13, l14, l15. average_fable repeatedly names the missing near-miss distractor they would actually fail on (e.g., l03: "grants the other truth in one clause and quietly ignores it"; l10: "budgeting reading correctly but drifting into summary in the body paragraphs").
- The lesson runs its own check before asking the student to run it. achiever_fable at l02 Step 8, l03 Step 7, l07 Step 7, l12 Step 8, l15 Step 8 ("what is the step actually assessing when it does its own work?"). average_fable at l15 Step 8 ("my job shrank to just rewriting the sentence").
- Worked examples sit on the same source as the student's writing task, so the AFTER is a pre-written answer key. achiever_fable at l04 Step 4, l09 Step 4, l10 Step 4, l11 Step 4 ("a draft to copy, not a model to transfer"), l15. average_fable at l05, l10 ("the exact choice used in the worked example"), l11.

**What did land as clear teaching (per the walks)**
- l05's genre transfer to rhetorical analysis was recognized as genuinely new by both walks. l08's "a faded ending is a pacing failure, not a thinking failure" reframe landed hard for both. l14's three fade patterns were called "the best paragraph in the lesson" (achiever_fable). l16's honesty about repetition and its untimed choice was explicitly appreciated (achiever_fable: "the kind of transparency I've been asking for since lesson 7").

## 3. Do the lessons prepare them for the full compositions?

## Q3: Readiness for the full compositions (probes at l04, l05, l06, l07, l08 and onward)

**l04 (sophisticated argument): mostly ready, but the assembly was scaffolded by recycled content.**
- Skills in place: situate (l01), cost/complexity (l02), hold tension (l03), stats bases (l01/l02). Both walks produced full plans and openings; achiever_fable deliberately inverted the worked example's rule, average_fable extended their own "winds down / phases up" move from l02.
- Not in place: independent generation on fresh material. average_fable: "this lesson still doesn't test whether I can generate a tension myself on fresh material, the tension was handed to me again" (0.4 confidence). achiever_fable had the numbers memorized and "skimmed it in thirty seconds," so the composition partially tested memory, not transfer. average_fable also entered the lesson with an unresolved conflict from l01 about whether sophistication is a thesis move or a whole-essay property; l04 answered it, but the confusion existed at the point of composition.

**l05 (sophisticated analysis): the one genuinely new transfer, and the walks diverge by persona.**
- In place for achiever_fable: they inferred Douglass's bind unprompted before the lesson named it and planned around different rhetorical choices to avoid cloning.
- Not in place for average_fable (PERSONA-SPECIFIC): situation-first planning ran against their device-first habit (0.5 confidence "unsure if I can actually do it"), and their self-identified real failure mode, "opening like the sophisticated answer and then decaying into device-hunting by body paragraph two" (0.4 confidence), was never taught against or tested by the lesson's soft MC options.

**l06 (sophisticated synthesis): NOT ready, and this is the clearest readiness failure in the evidence (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, average_fable; partially CORROBORATED).**
- average_fable hit the composition with the two load-bearing new skills untaught: "synthesize and weight get one sentence each and were never actually taught" (0.2 confidence on both), plus confusion about whether the three sources were even three sources. The old moves (situate, tension, stats bases) were in place from l01-l03.
- achiever_fable managed the weave and weighting but confirms the teaching was thin (Steps 1-2 "maybe 20% new content") and that no lesson had "formally taught weighting before"; their success came from prior skill, not from l06's instruction.
- The gap propagates: at l11 average_fable was still "only about 60% sure I could execute weighting in a way a grader would recognize," and at the l16 gate it remained their "flagged weakest spot."

**l07 (calibrate + full essay): the writing skills were in place, the calibration skill could not be exercised (CORROBORATED).**
- Both walks assembled situate + cost + tension into a full essay fine (skills from l01-l03, assembled in l04/l06). But the lesson's actual target, comparing a prediction to a grade, was impossible: no grader exists (both walks, l07 Step 2). So the composition tested the old skills and left the new one unverifiable. average_fable's confidence in "knowing whether an actual grader will verify my prediction" was 0.3.

**l08 onward (sustained/timed full essays, l08-l11, l13-l14): pacing skills arrived on time for one persona, late or absent for the other.**
- achiever_fable had plan-first (l04/l05) and assembly (l04/l06) in place; budgeting was new-ish and workable; their complaint is the opposite of unreadiness (the reused water source meant the composition re-tested memorized content).
- average_fable (PERSONA-SPECIFIC): l08 claimed prerequisites they did not have ("nobody ever taught me a time budget or a fast-plan format"), l09 gave no timing numbers so the budget was invented, and no lesson enforced a clock, so every "timed" composition (l09, l10, l13, l14) ran on a self-supplied phone timer. Their l16 verdict: "a budget with no clock is a pretend budget."
- CORROBORATED gap across all composition probes: neither walk was ever required to find a tension in a source that does not announce its own tension. average_fable logs this at l03, l07, l08, l09, l12, l13, l14 (confidence stuck at 0.3-0.4 throughout); achiever_fable's parallel complaint is that every source hands over the tension in its final paragraph (l02, l03, l07). Since "hold the tension" is the spine of every composition, the compositions never verified the entry skill of tension-finding.
- CORROBORATED gap: "is my work mine?" Both walks had to invent anti-cloning rules (achiever_fable banning "fear itself" at l10 and inverting rules at l04/l09; average_fable picking the opposite side at l09/l14) because worked examples sat on the same source as the composition task. The compositions therefore cannot distinguish transfer from recitation, and average_fable says so explicitly at l13: "I can't tell if I'm transferring the skill or reciting my old lesson-3 answer."
