## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Do the lessons make sense?

**Overall: yes, students followed the content. Neither walk reports failing to understand a lesson's core idea. The problems are pacing, scaffolding that leaks answers, and confusion at specific steps, not incomprehensible teaching.**

### Lessons that landed cleanly
- **g10_l01_counterclaim_claim**: both walks completed all skills; average_fable had a productive struggle (its first daylight saving reason "disrupts sleep" restated its side instead of answering the dark-mornings objection, then it self-corrected). That is the lesson working as designed.
- **g10_l04 through g10_l09 (analysis strand)**: average_fable shows genuine learning progression here, e.g., in l06 it says "Before Step 5 I had never separated 'effect on the character' from 'effect on the reader'." The traps in l04 (theme-claim-without-choice, option C) and l06 (Step 4 option B, a "reader"-word decoy) actually tempted the average student, meaning the difficulty was correctly aimed for that persona.
- **g10_l21 / g10_l25 (fresh Chopin sources)**: achiever_fable explicitly celebrated that l21 finally used sources it had never seen ("WAIT. These are actually new sources??"), and l25 Step 3 was read carefully "because it's new to me." Fresh material makes the lessons make sense as tests of skill.

### Specific confusion points, with lesson and step
1. **g10_l02_concede_hold, checklist question 3 (average_fable)**: the lesson weakened the third checklist question from "does the reason answer the objection?" (lesson 1's version) to merely "is there a reason?" The student's exact complaint: "the lesson lowered the bar instead of helping with my actual struggle," and reason_answers_the_objection was the student's weakest skill (0.65 after l01). A later lesson lowering the standard on the one thing the student struggles with is a design error.
2. **g10_l02, Step 6 (average_fable)**: feedback confused the student "about which side is conceding what — the fix sentence seemed to concede the arguer's own point depending on which side they're on." This open question (whose point is being conceded when positions flip) was never resolved.
3. **g10_l07 (average_fable)**: the term "warrant" collided with the student's prior claim-evidence-warrant knowledge from argument contexts. Then in **g10_l09** the same student had to ask "Is 'warrant' actually the same thing as what lesson 7 called 'significance'?" The course uses two names for one concept and never says so; achiever_fable also noted "'warrant' is just a new word for Lesson 7's 'significance'" (l09 redundancy flag).
4. **g10_l04 and g10_l05 (average_fable)**: the Chopin excerpt "stops suddenly at 'Free! Body and soul free!'" and the student only learned the twist (the husband is not dead) from a spoiler inside a wrong answer choice in l05. The open question "what happens in the rest of the story" persisted through l06 and l08 and was never answered. A wrong-answer option should not be the delivery vehicle for the text's ending.
5. **g10_l13_error_autopsy, Step 9 (both walks)**: the "first No names the type" rule breaks on drafts with two tangled error types. average_fable: "the rule got murky because 'radar' IS a specific — I had to make a judgment call and wished there were a teacher to ask." achiever_fable self-rated 0.7 on "diagnose_drafts_with_tangled_error_types" and asked the same open question. The lesson teaches a decision procedure that fails on exactly the realistic case.
6. **g10_l13, Step 8 (average_fable)**: diagnosing an error the student planted itself "felt weird — like hiding my own Easter eggs, since I obviously knew the answer." The step's design defeats its purpose.
7. **g10_l15_evidence_pool (average_fable)**: the daylight saving "con" source is not a clean con (it partly agrees the switching is the harm, proposes regional choice). The lesson never warned that a con source might partly agree; the student's confidence on label_source_job_when_con_partly_agrees ended at 0.4, the lowest confidence in either walk. The teaching does not cover the case its own source set contains.
8. **g10_l14, Step 5 (achiever_fable)**: the "reorder" diagnosis "felt half-invented — the draft was already in causal order, so I had to get the answer partly by elimination." The exercise asks students to find a flaw that is not there.
9. **g10_l11_logical_sequence (average_fable)**: the whole lesson "felt like a big step DOWN in difficulty — like a middle-school skill after ten lessons of counterclaims and craft analysis." Sequencing after craft analysis is a difficulty inversion in the ordering.
10. **Reprinted sources as padding (achiever_fable, corroborated within-walk across lessons)**: l21 Step 8 reprints the entire highways article from Step 4; l24 Step 3 reprints the full source set from Step 2 word for word; l25 Step 7 reprints the whole Chopin story from Step 3; l26 Step 7 reprints the wetlands article from Step 2. The student's standing open question: "Why does the course keep reprinting full source texts word for word right after I just read them?"
11. **g10_l26, Step 9 (achiever_fable)**: the step "says 'no checklist' but then restates the checklist in the prompt — that's not cold, that's the checklist in a trench coat." Cold steps that are not cold undermine the lesson's own claim about what it measures.

### Structural pattern that damages sense-making
The worked-example-spoils-the-exercise problem recurs across the whole course and both personas: average_fable logged it in l03, l06 (Step 7), l08 (Step 4 answer copied from Step 3), l10 (Step 7 reuses Step 3's draft, Step 8 answers its own diagnosis question), l11 (Steps 4/7/8), l13 (Step 7), l14 (Steps 4 and 7), l15 (Step 4), l16 (Step 7), l20, l25, l26; achiever_fable logged it in l16 (Step 7 "basically forced me to reproduce the Step 4 worked example"), l18 (AFTER sentence was "the answer key for Steps 8–10"), l19 (Step 4 wove the same source pair as Step 7), l21, l22 (Step 4's AFTER "handed me the weave move like an answer key"), l23, l26 (Step 7's paragraph is almost word for word Step 3's BEFORE). When the model answer is visible on the same material, the write step measures memory, not skill. Both personas ended up deliberately banning themselves from copying, which is the students doing the instructional design themselves.

### Summary of the redundancy structure
Three distinct redundancy mechanisms show up: (a) **concept relabeling** (significance→warrant, analysis-vs-summary→craft-vs-content, weaving as a name for l03's move, synthesis claim as concede-and-hold plus a provenance rule), (b) **source-set exhaustion** (congestion pricing across l03/l09-10/l15/l18/l20/l22/l23/l24; daylight saving across l07/l15/l16/l20/l22; school year across l02/l03/l14/l15/l16/l17), and (c) **within-lesson triple-testing** (three MC checks on one distinction, three near-identical write steps: flagged in l15, l16, l17, l18, l21, l22, l23, l26 by achiever_fable and in l03, l08, l09, l11, l13, l15 by average_fable). The corroborated l01→l02→l03 opening run and the l04→l05→l06 analysis run are the most costly, since both personas lost early lessons to re-teaching.
