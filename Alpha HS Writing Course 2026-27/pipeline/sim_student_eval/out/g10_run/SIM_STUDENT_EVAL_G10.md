## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Do the lessons make sense?

Overall verdict: the lessons are individually clear. Neither walk reported failing to understand a lesson's core teaching. But both walks reported specific confusion points, sequencing problems, and lesson-design defects that undermine sense-making.

### Lessons that were clear and worked as intended
- g10_l01_counterclaim_claim: clear for both personas. The average_fable student had a productive struggle (their Step 7-9 daylight saving reason "disrupts sleep" failed question 3 of the checklist, and Step 5 option A briefly tricked them with a real concession whose reason did not answer the objection). This is the lesson working: the confusion was instructional, not structural.
- g10_l04, g10_l05, g10_l06 (analysis strand): clear for both. average_fable had real learning moments (nearly picking the theme-claim-without-a-choice trap in l04 Step 5; discovering the character-effect vs. reader-effect distinction in l06 Step 5 as genuinely new).
- g10_l21 and g10_l25 (fresh Chopin/highways sources): both walks explicitly credited these as sensible capstone builds, even while flagging Steps 1-2 as re-teaching.

### Specific confusion points (student-reported, by lesson and step)
1. g10_l02_concede_hold, Step 6 (average_fable): the feedback confused the student about "which side is conceding what," and they left the lesson with an open question about keeping straight whose point is conceded when positions flip. The lesson never resolved it.
2. g10_l02_concede_hold, checklist design (average_fable): the student noticed the third checklist question was weakened from lesson 1's "does the reason answer the objection?" to merely "is there a reason?" and asked, verbatim, why the lesson "lowered the bar instead of helping with my actual struggle." That is a coherence defect: lesson 2's tool is weaker than lesson 1's tool on exactly the skill the student self-identified as their weakest (conf 0.65 after l01).
3. g10_l07_significance and g10_l09_analysis_check, terminology (average_fable): "warrant" confused the student twice. In l07 they flagged that "warrant" collides with the claim-evidence-warrant argument usage; in l09 they logged an open question, "Is 'warrant' actually the same thing as what lesson 7 called 'significance', or is it secretly different?" The curriculum introduces two labels for one move and never reconciles them. achiever_fable independently noted "'warrant' is just a new word for Lesson 7's 'significance'" (l09 redundancy flag).
4. g10_l13_error_autopsy, Step 9 (average_fable): the "first No names the type" diagnostic rule broke down on a draft with two simultaneous problems, since "radar" is a specific term inside an otherwise general draft. The student made a judgment call and explicitly wished for a teacher. The lesson never answers whether a draft can have two error types at once, and the student self-rated the tangled-diagnosis skill at 0.6. achiever_fable logged the same gap (conf 0.7 on tangled error types, open question about which to fix first).
5. g10_l15_evidence_pool, source labeling (average_fable): the daylight saving "con" source is not a clean con, it partly agrees and proposes regional choice. The lesson's label-each-source's-job framework gave no guidance, and the student's confidence on "label_source_job_when_con_partly_agrees" cratered to 0.4, the lowest confidence in either walk. The lesson's own tool does not cover the lesson's own material.
6. g10_l04/l05/l06/l08, the Chopin excerpt (average_fable): the excerpt cuts off at "Free! Body and soul free!" across four lessons, and the student only learned the twist (the husband is not dead) from a spoiler in a wrong answer choice in l05. They asked repeatedly, across three lessons, what happens at the end. Using a truncated text with the resolution leaked via a distractor is a content-sequencing defect.
7. g10_l11_logical_sequence (average_fable): the whole lesson "felt like a big step DOWN in difficulty, like a middle-school skill after ten lessons of counterclaims and craft analysis." Placement after craft-analysis lessons makes the sequence feel incoherent.
8. g10_l26_precision_pass, Step 9 (achiever_fable): a step labeled "no checklist" restates the checklist inside the prompt, "the checklist in a trench coat." The step contradicts its own stated purpose.

### Structural defects that recur across many lessons (both walks)
- Worked examples that pre-answer the write steps: average_fable logged this in lessons 3, 8, 11 (Step 8's fixed version was written in the instructions), 13, 14 (Step 4 answered by Step 3), 15, 16, 20, 25, 26; achiever_fable logged it in lessons 16 (Step 7), 18 (AFTER sentence was "basically the answer key for Steps 8-10"), 19 (Step 4 handed the answer to Step 7), 21, 22, 23, 26 (Step 7 nearly word-for-word the Step 3 BEFORE example). CORROBORATED across personas and lessons.
- Full-source reprints as padding: achiever_fable flagged l21 Step 8 (entire highways article reprinted from Step 4), l24 Step 3 (source set reprinted word for word from Step 2), l25 Step 7 (entire story reprinted from Step 3), l26 Step 7 (wetlands article reprinted from Step 2).
- Hollow self-checks: achiever_fable flagged in lessons 10, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 23, 26 that checking a paragraph you just wrote to pass the check "almost guarantees a pass, which tells you nothing." In l26 the self-check caught exactly one word on the student's own fresh draft.
- Single-flaw, self-announcing multiple choice: achiever_fable flagged l19 (list options "literally announced themselves as lists in their last clauses"), l21 Step 6, l22, l25 Step 6, l26; average_fable flagged too-easy MC in l12 Steps 4-5, l16 Step 5 ("the source says" giveaway openers), l20 Step 5.

## 2. Are there redundancies?

## Q2: Are there redundancies?

Yes, extensively. The deterministic table plus walk evidence shows redundancy is the curriculum's dominant defect. Findings below use the students' own felt_repeated flags, labeled per the rules. Note: both walks come from one model family (Fable), so nothing here is cross-model corroborated; the strongest available label is PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model).

### Corroborated across both personas (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, cross-persona, one model)
1. g10_l02 repeats g10_l01 (achiever_fable, average_fable): near-total overlap. Same 3-question checklist (actually weakened, per average_fable), same fake-concession trap, same diagnose exercise, same daylight saving topic. average_fable: "I wrote nearly the same sentence three times and didn't learn anything new from those steps" (Steps 2-9). achiever_fable: Steps 7-9 were "three versions of the same sentence."
2. g10_l03 repeats g10_l02 (both): first half of teach re-explains concede-and-answer; POSITION/CONCEDE/ANSWER is "the old 3-question checklist with new labels"; the longer-school-year topic was literally lesson 2's transfer topic. average_fable: Steps 3, 4, 5, and 7 made the same "naming isn't answering" diagnosis four times.
3. g10_l05 repeats g10_l04 (both): technique-plus-effect and the "choice without a purpose" diagnosis are straight from l04; "craft vs. content" is analysis-vs-summary relabeled. Plus three MC checks in a row on one distinction and a Step 8 diagnosis the lesson pre-answered.
4. g10_l06 repeats g10_l05 (both): "name the choice + say what it does to the reader" is a rebrand of l05's work (and l04's); the device-to-effect test reuses the checklist template from lessons 1-3.
5. g10_l14 repeats g10_l10 (both): l14's pitch is explicitly combining l10 (add/delete) and l11 (ordering); the 3-question check is those lessons' checks stacked; Step 5 mirrors l10's diagnose format step for step; Step 9 rewrites nearly the same draft.
6. g10_l23 repeats g10_l19 (both): l19's woven/list check plus the concede-and-answer move (l15-17, back to l03) "stapled together and called revision"; congestion pricing sources reused from l20/l22; Steps 4-6 repeated one distinction three times.

### Cross-lesson source reuse (compounds the redundancy)
- Congestion pricing articles: used in l03 (transfer), l15, l18, l20, l22 (probably), l23, l24 (the gate). achiever_fable by l20: "I've basically memorized (FHWA, BLS, Census stats, timing-gap objection), so I skimmed instead of really reading."
- Daylight saving: l01/l02 (topic), l07, l15, l16, l20, l22 (fourth use per achiever_fable: "the course answered by handing me daylight saving a fourth time").
- Longer school year: l02 transfer, l03, l13-14, l15, l16, l17.
Both walks flagged that this reuse means transfer and gate tasks never test cold reading. CORROBORATED in effect (both personas raise it repeatedly).

### Interpretation caveat
The achiever persona flags structural-skeleton repetition even when content is new (e.g., l04); those flags are about pacing for a high achiever, not necessarily curriculum defects for all students. But the l01-l03, l05-l06, l14, and l23 overlaps were felt by BOTH personas, including the on-grade student, and should be treated as real.
