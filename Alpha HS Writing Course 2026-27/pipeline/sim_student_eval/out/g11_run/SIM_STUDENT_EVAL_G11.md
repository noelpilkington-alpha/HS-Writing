## Q1: Do the lessons make sense?

Overall verdict: the core skill sequence (claim shapes → reasoning → rhetorical analysis basics → sourcing → synthesis → source-free → multi-perspective → exam craft → gates) is intelligible to both personas, and the individual "one idea per lesson" teaches land quickly. The problems are almost all in the scaffolding mechanics, which are so systematically broken that both students learned to route around them. Findings by lesson, with steps:

### Lessons that landed cleanly
- **g11_l01_scope_claim**: both walks report high confidence on the WHOM/WHICH/WHEN move. But achiever_fable, Step 8: "the fill-in frame nearly forced me to copy the lesson's own example sentence." average_fable was left unsure whether "most" counts as scoped or hedged (Step 10) and whether an exception "counts" against a claim, "working alone with no one to ask made this hard." The lesson never resolves these boundary cases.
- **g11_l23_time_budget, g11_l24_plan_fast, g11_l26_concision_pass**: the ideas are clear, arguably too clear. achiever_fable on l23: the idea is "almost insultingly simple" but genuinely new, "I admit I've never actually done it before." On l24: "a two-minute idea stretched across nine steps." On l26: "a skill teachable in one paragraph... takes eleven steps and two full-source repastes."

### Systematic confusion and design flaws, with specific citations
1. **"Run the check" steps that pre-print all the answers.** achiever_fable counted this from l01 Step 9 ("the prompt's instructions literally run the test for me") through l03 Step 8, l04 Step 7, l05 Step 8, l06 Step 8, l07 Step 7, l08 Step 7, l09 Step 8, l10 Step 8, l11 Step 8, l12 Step 8, l17 Step 8, l20 Step 8, l21 Step 7, l25 Step 8, l26 Step 8: "sixth lesson in a row where the 'run the check' step runs the check for me." l26 provided the proof this matters: in Step 8 the achiever wrote "very high" with "very" still in it and only caught it because for once they actually ran the check themselves.
2. **The worked example pre-writes the student's answer (the "funnel").** achiever_fable used a deliberate build-a-different-answer workaround in l02 Step 7, l03 Step 10, l04 Steps 6/8/10, l05 Step 7, l06 Step 7, l09 Step 7, l10 Step 7, l11 Step 7, l12 Step 7, l13 Step 7, l17 Step 3→7, l18 Steps 6-8, l22 Step 4, calling it "the twelfth lesson running with this problem." Note: this workaround is persona-specific; average_fable, without the same self-imposed discipline, repeatedly could not tell whether they had learned anything: l01 "Couldn't tell if my Step 9 rewrite showed real learning or just copied the example's pattern with words swapped"; l02 "am I doing the move or copying the sentence?"; l03 "unsure whether I'm learning the move or just getting good at one debate." For the average student the funnel does not just waste time, it destroys their ability to self-assess.
3. **Next task's answer leaked inside the current task's feedback.** achiever_fable flagged this in l08 Step 5 (reveal did the trimming for Step 6), l10, l11, l12 Step 6, l13 Step 6 (reveal hands "scarcity" and "tradeoff" before the write), l16, l17 Step 6, l21 Step 5 (reveal hands the exact limit Step 6 asks for), l22 Step 6.
4. **Full-source double-pastes.** Flagged by achiever_fable in l04 (Steps 2/6), l06 (Steps 2/7, 10/11), l07, l08, l09, l10 (four pastes in one lesson), l11, l12, l13, l14, l16 Step 3 ("a couple thousand words of pure scroll padding"), l17, l19, l20, l21, l22, l23, l24, l25, l26, l29, l30, l31; "seventh lesson in a row" by l29. Steps 8/9 in l19, l24, l29 prove the authors know how to use a one-line reminder instead.
5. **Broken or contradictory steps.**
   - **g11_l11_bias_limits Step 5 is broken**: "it asked a multiple-choice question... but no answer options were printed at all, I had to guess what the choices would have looked like and skip it" (achiever_fable). This is a publishing defect, not a design choice.
   - **g11_l16_midgate_synthesis Step 3 vs Step 4**: Step 3 ends "Then write the full synthesis from this plan," but writing the full synthesis is Step 4's entire job; "followed literally I'd write the same essay twice back to back" (achiever_fable). The identical defect recurs at **g11_l30_gate_capstone Steps 3/4**: "the instructions seem to ask for the identical essay twice; I had to guess the intent."
   - **g11_l30_gate_capstone Step 4 pre-answers the gate's one decision**: "Step 4 tells me to name the task type myself, then the very next sentence names it for me ('it is a source-free argument'), the one decision the gate is supposed to test was handed to me in the prompt" (achiever_fable).
   - **g11_l31_rhetorical_analysis_essay Step 1 assumes lessons that do not exist in the track**: "Step 1 claims I have 'practiced the choice-effect-purpose move at the sentence and the paragraph', I checked my lesson list and there is no rhetorical-analysis lesson in it" (achiever_fable). Note: the achiever's walk DID include l06/l07 (choice, effect, purpose), so this appears to be a track-configuration inconsistency between what l31 assumes and what a given walk delivers; average_fable's evidence for this lesson is not present. Either way the lesson's own framing ("a move you already own") did not match the student's experienced sequence.
   - **g11_l15_calibrate_own_synthesis Step 2 describes a loop that cannot run**: PREDICT → SUBMIT → READ THE REVEAL assumes a grader; "there is no grader, nothing is submitted or revealed; Steps 9 and 10 quietly swap in re-scoring myself... same blind spots both times, and the lesson never acknowledges this" (achiever_fable).
6. **Sequencing questions raised by students.**
   - **l13 before l12**: "this point-not-source lesson should have come before Lesson 12's full synthesis, not after... did Lesson 12 teach this implicitly (making this a re-teach) or did it let me write a source tour without telling me?" (achiever_fable, l13 Step 1).
   - **Rubric shown too late**: "Why were the rubric rows only shown now, I've been writing syntheses since Lesson 12 with no idea what 'good' was being measured against" (achiever_fable, l15).
   - **Missed connections the lessons never draw**: l13 tour-vs-weave is l04 list-vs-line "one level up"; l18 develop-and-tie "connects to Lesson 4's list-vs-line... I'd have gotten it in half the time if it had" (achiever_fable). The course leaves these to the student.
7. **The "stake" gap.** g11_l10 Step 1 mentions "expertise or stake" and then never uses stake again; achiever_fable re-raised it in l14: "every practice set since Lesson 10... has been stake-free federal agencies, third time on the easy case." An open conceptual question the course introduces and abandons.
8. **average_fable's unresolved conceptual confusions** (persona-specific, and worrying because no one answers them): l02 "is a good claim supposed to be BOTH scoped and nuanced? The lesson doesn't say"; l02 Step 9 "is the trailing clause nuance or accidental hedging?, no one to ask"; l01 "does one exception... sink my claim?" These are exactly the judgment calls a course should teach, and the average student finishes lessons still holding them.

### Cross-persona, one model (labeled PERSONA-SPECIFIC in the table; raised by both achiever_fable and average_fable)
1. **g11_l02_nuanced_claim repeats g11_l01_scope_claim.** The not-X-but-Y skill is new, but the lesson recycles l01's grid/clean-power topic and the "fund retraining instead of control jobs" example without acknowledgment, and l02 Step 8 repeats l01 Step 9's flaw of running the check for the student.
2. **g11_l03_qualified_claim repeats g11_l01_scope_claim.** "Two-thirds of the lesson repeats earlier ones": the QUALIFIER move is l01's WHOM/WHICH/WHEN scoping, "hedging is not the same as bounding" is l01's hedging-vs-scoping distinction with hedging renamed "waffle," the source is l02's exact AI-workforce debate, and Step 3's AFTER is l01's scoped retraining claim "with a 'because' stapled on."
3. **g11_l16_midgate_synthesis repeats g11_l12_full_synthesis.** Step 1's four-bullet checklist is literally l12 + l13 + l14 combined. Both students accepted recap-then-test as legitimate for a gate; the real repetition complaints were the Step 3 full-source double-paste and the Step 3/Step 4 write-it-twice contradiction.
4. **g11_l30_gate_capstone repeats g11_l29_which_task_type.** Step 1's type-naming checklist is "word-for-word the skill from Lesson 29," accepted as "an honest reminder card before a test, not fake new teaching"; the residual complaints are the Step 2/3 double-paste ("flagged for six lessons running") and Step 4 pre-answering the task type.
