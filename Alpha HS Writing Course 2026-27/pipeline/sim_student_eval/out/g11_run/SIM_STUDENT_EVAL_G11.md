## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Do the Lessons Make Sense?

Short answer: the skills and their ordering are almost always intelligible to both personas; the confusion that exists is caused by lesson mechanics (pre-answered checks, ambiguous instructions, missing content), not by the concepts.

### Concepts that landed cleanly (both walks)
- l01-l05 (claim-building ladder): achiever_fable reports 0.85-0.95 confidence across scoping, not-X-but-Y, qualified claims, lines of reasoning, sufficiency; average_fable can run every 3-question check and knows exactly which move each lesson added. The ladder is coherent and each lesson's "one new move" is identifiable.
- l23 and l24: both new skills (time budgeting, fast planning) were understood in "about ninety seconds" (achiever_fable, l23 composition). Simple, clear, genuinely new.
- l29/l30 gate structure: achiever_fable explicitly distinguished "recap-then-test" gates from "padded re-teaching" and accepted the l16 and l30 recap cards as legitimate (l16 can_do; l30 composition: "It's a reminder card before a test. I'll allow it").

### Specific confusion points, traced to lesson and step
1. l15 (calibrate_own_synthesis), Step 2 vs Steps 9-10: the lesson teaches a PREDICT, SUBMIT, READ THE REVEAL, NAME THE GAP loop, but "there is no grader, nothing is submitted or revealed; Steps 9 and 10 quietly swap in re-scoring myself, so I'm calibrating against my own second opinion, same blind spots both times, and the lesson never acknowledges this" (achiever_fable, struggled + open). The lesson's core routine does not match the self-paced delivery format. SINGLE-MODEL but structural, not persona-dependent.
2. l11 (bias_limits), Step 5: published with NO answer options at all. "It asked a multiple-choice question ('which verdict names a strength AND a real gap?') but no answer options were printed; I had to guess what the choices would have looked like and skip it" (achiever_fable, struggled). A literally broken step.
3. l16 (midgate), Step 3: ends with "Then write the full synthesis from this plan" while Step 4's entire job is writing that synthesis; the student had to guess it was an editing mistake (achiever_fable, struggled). The identical ambiguity recurs at l30 Steps 3-4 ("am I writing the same complete essay twice in a row?", l30 composition). CORROBORATED across two lessons within one walk.
4. l31 (rhetorical_analysis_essay), Step 1: claims the student has "practiced the choice-effect-purpose move at the sentence and the paragraph," which the student could not verify against their own lesson history (achiever_fable, struggled + composition). The lesson addresses a track the student does not recognize taking.
5. l13 (woven_synthesis), sequencing: "this point-not-source lesson should have come before Lesson 12's full synthesis, not after... did Lesson 12 teach this implicitly (making this a re-teach) or did it let me write a source tour without telling me?" (achiever_fable, struggled + open; echoed in the l13 composition). The l12-l13 order is backwards or l12 is silently incomplete.
6. l15, rubric timing: "Why were the rubric rows only shown now, I've been writing syntheses since Lesson 12 with no idea what 'good' was being measured against" (achiever_fable, open). Students wrote three graded-genre syntheses before seeing the grading criteria.
7. l02, unresolved conceptual question for the average student: "How does the nuance move connect to last lesson's scoping move, is a good claim supposed to be BOTH scoped and nuanced? The lesson doesn't say" (average_fable, open). Also l02: "is the tail-end clause in my Step 9 claim nuance, or hedging that waters down my commitment?", a real conceptual boundary the lesson never resolves for a student working alone. PERSONA-SPECIFIC (average): the achiever asked no such questions.
8. l01, average persona, working-alone problem: "Couldn't judge on my own whether an exception 'counts' against my Step 10 claim, working alone with no one to ask made this hard" (average_fable, struggled). The course provides no mechanism for adjudicating borderline cases ("is 'most' a real scope or just a hedge word?").
9. Missed connections the lessons never state: l13's tour-vs-weave is l04's list-vs-line one level up, and l18's vague-sweep-vs-developed-example is the same shape; achiever_fable had to build both bridges alone and says "I'd have gotten it in half the time if it had" (l13 open, l18 struggled/open). The course's own recurring skeleton is never acknowledged to the student.
10. l10/l14, unanswered content question: Step 1 of l10 introduced "expertise or stake" and then "every practice set since then has been stake-free federal agencies" (achiever_fable, l10 open, repeated in l14 struggled and the l14 composition). A named concept is never exercised.

### The pervasive sense-making tax: pre-answered checks
Across l01 through at least l26, both walks report that "run the check" steps print the check's answers in the prompt before the student acts. achiever_fable counted this explicitly lesson by lesson ("sixth lesson in a row", l08-l11; still present at l25 Step 8 and l26 Step 8). average_fable independently flagged the same pattern (l17 felt_repeated: "the diagnose step's answer is always 'add the move from Step 1', I can pick it without reading the draft"). CORROBORATED. The lessons still "make sense," but the diagnostic steps carry no cognitive work, and l26 provided direct proof of harm: the student made exactly the error the check exists to catch ("very high") because they had never actually had to run a check themselves.

## 3. Do the lessons prepare them for the full compositions?

# Q3: Do the Lessons Prepare Students for the Full Compositions?

Composition probes on record: l12 (full synthesis), l13 (woven synthesis), l14 (synthesis with evaluation), l15 (calibrate), l16 (midgate), l19 (source-free essay), l22 (multiperspective essay), l23 (time budget), l24 (plan fast), l27 (synthesis rehearsal), l28 (argument rehearsal), l30 (gate capstone), l31 (rhetorical analysis essay). The user's requested probe list (l18, l23, l24, l26, l27) is covered where evidence exists; the strongest readiness evidence clusters on l23, l24, l27, and the terminal gates l30/l31.

## l23 (time budget) composition
- Skills IN PLACE: claim construction was fully in place, achiever_fable's l23 composition combined the qualified-claim shape (l03) and not-X-but-Y (l02) inside the timed thesis without prompting. The pacing concept itself landed fast ("clicked in about ninety seconds").
- Skills NOT in place, and the lesson's own fault: the timed-reading skill could not be practiced honestly because Step 3's source is the grid-vs-clean-power passage used since l01/l03. achiever_fable's composition: "I have used this exact topic at least twice before as a transfer exercise." average_fable made the same complaint in its felt_repeated flag ("I have its numbers memorized, which defeats the point of timed reading"). CORROBORATED across personas.
- New skill genuinely new: both walks confirm no lesson before l23 addressed pacing ("Nothing in Lessons 1-22 ever talked about pacing or minutes", achiever composition). So the prerequisite chain is fine; the practice material is compromised.

## l24 (plan fast) composition
- Skills IN PLACE: achiever_fable's composition folded earlier moves into the fast plan automatically (narrowed action from l01, not-X-but-Y concession from l02, upshot conclusion from l19), evidence that l01-l19 skills transferred.
- Skills NOT in place / practice compromised: the "fresh prompt" is the AI/automation topic from l02, l03, l16, l20 ("Wait. This is the AI/automation-workforce topic again", achiever composition, Step 3). The student reports knowing the stats by heart, so fast-planning on unfamiliar material, the actual exam condition, was never rehearsed. SINGLE-MODEL as a composition observation but corroborated by average_fable's l28 felt_repeated flag about the same recycled-material pattern.

## l26 (concision pass)
- No composition probe response text is in the evidence for l26, but achiever_fable's walk data shows the one place the missing-scaffold problem bit: in Step 8 the student wrote "very high" with "very" still in it, caught it only by accident, and noted "the lesson should have made me actually run the check instead of running it for me." This is direct evidence that the pre-answered-check design (see Q1) leaves the self-checking skill unpracticed even when content knowledge is fine.

## l27 (synthesis rehearsal) composition
- Skills IN PLACE: everything. achiever_fable's composition itemizes the sources of every Step 1 move (budget = l23, fast-plan = l24, weave-by-point = l13, check = l15) and executes the end-to-end run at 0.9 confidence. average_fable's felt_repeated flag independently maps the same decomposition (l12, l13, l16, l23, l24). CORROBORATED.
- What the lesson fails to add: the one new condition (rehearse under a clock) is undercut because "Step 2 immediately admits there's no clock, leaving the budget honor-system" (achiever, composition and open questions). So timed-rehearsal readiness is claimed by the lesson but not actually produced.
- average_fable additionally flagged that Step 3's sources are "the exact same renewable-grid set (EIA, NREL, EPA, DOE)" from an earlier lesson, so even the rehearsal content is pre-seen. SINGLE-MODEL, average_fable.

## l16 (midgate) and l30 (gate capstone)
- l16: the student could write the full synthesis plan cold (claim + weave + weighting + set-limit, all traceable to l12/l13/l14). But the gate's transfer validity is broken: "the gate's topic (AI and the workforce) is the exact topic I used for my transfer claims in Lessons 2 and 3, so the transfer-to-a-new-topic muscle isn't actually being tested at the gate" (achiever_fable, l16 struggled). Also a broken instruction: Step 3 ends with "write the full synthesis from this plan" while writing it is Step 4's whole job.
- l30: skills were in place (task-type naming from l29, claim shapes from l02/l03, own examples from l17/l18), and the topic was finally new, which the student explicitly praised. But the gate pre-answers its own test: "Step 4 tells me to name the task type myself, then the very next sentence names it for me ('it is a source-free argument'), the one decision the gate is supposed to test was handed to me in the prompt" (achiever_fable, l30 struggled). The Step 3/Step 4 write-the-essay-twice ambiguity from l16 recurs verbatim.

## l31 (rhetorical analysis essay): the one hard prerequisite failure
- achiever_fable's l31 composition documents a broken prerequisite chain: "Step 1 says 'You have practiced the choice-effect-purpose move at the sentence and the paragraph.' I went back through my own list of finished lessons... There is no rhetorical-analysis lesson in there." The student's walk record does contain l06/l07/l08/l09 (rhetorical analysis lessons), so either the lesson track delivered to this walk omitted them from the student's visible history or the composition was run against a track variant without them; either way, by the student's own account the essay assumed sentence- and paragraph-level choice-effect-purpose practice the student did not believe they had. The student compensated by mapping the structure onto l04's line-of-reasoning ("it's basically a line of reasoning where every paragraph must arrive at the same purpose"), and confidences landed at only 0.8, the lowest of any late-course composition. SINGLE-MODEL, achiever_fable, but it is the most serious readiness gap on record because it sits on the terminal essay.

## Overall verdict on composition readiness
The skill ladder itself works: every composition shows students spontaneously stacking earlier moves (qualified claims into timed theses, l21 concede-limit-advance into l22 essays, l14 weighting into l16/l27 plans). What repeatedly fails is the validity of the composition as a test: recycled topics mean "cold" and "transfer" tasks measure memory of prior lessons (l16, l23, l24, l27, l29), and gates pre-answer their own key decisions (l30 Step 4).
