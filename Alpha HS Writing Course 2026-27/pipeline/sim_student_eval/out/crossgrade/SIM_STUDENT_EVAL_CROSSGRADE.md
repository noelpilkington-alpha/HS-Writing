## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Does the grade-to-grade progression make sense?

**Overall verdict: The macro-sequence is coherent but badly over-flattened. Each grade's core ladder is logical (claims -> evidence -> reasoning -> paragraphs -> essays -> gates), and each grade's opening genuinely extends the prior grade at least once. But the student hit essentially zero conceptual gaps and massive conceptual overlap, which means the progression "makes sense" mostly because later grades re-teach earlier grades' content rather than assuming it.** All findings below are SINGLE-MODEL (one walk, achiever_fable) unless noted.

### What builds coherently
- **g9 internal ladder is sound.** g9_l01 (arguable claim) -> g9_l05/l06/l07 (evidence integration/selection) -> g9_l08/l09 (hinges, warrant) -> g9_l10/l17 (P-E-W paragraph) -> g9_l20/l21/l22 (planning, coherence, intro/conclusion) -> g9_l23/l24 (full essays) -> g9_l26/l27 (gates). The student explicitly called g9_l09 (warrant) "the natural next rung" after g9_l07/l08. (SINGLE-MODEL)
- **g10 opens with a genuinely new move.** The student flagged g10_l01 (counterclaim claim) as "genuinely NEW (first non-one-sided claim since lesson 1)." The analysis strand (g10_l04-l09, craft claims, device-to-effect, significance) and the cross-text synthesis strand (g10_l15-l24) are new territory relative to g9. (SINGLE-MODEL)
- **g11's new contributions are real but thin:** scope/qualification of claims (g11_l01-l03), sufficiency (g11_l05), source credibility/bias (g11_l10/l11), timed-writing skills (g11_l23/l24), and task-type triage (g11_l29). (SINGLE-MODEL)
- **g12's new contribution is thinner still:** "sophistication" framing (g12_l02-l07), per-paragraph pacing shares (g12_l08), and the FRQ-section gate. The student repeatedly noted lessons admitting "You already own the two moves this needs" (g12_l06) and "no new teaching" (g12_l16). (SINGLE-MODEL)

### Jumps or gaps where a grade assumes an untaught skill
- **Almost none were reported.** Across four grades of journal entries, there is no case where the student says "I was asked to do X and no earlier lesson taught X." The failure mode is the inverse: later grades assume the student does NOT retain earlier skills and re-teach them (see Q2).
- **One genuine unfilled gap, carried from g9 onward: summarizing.** g9_l05 defined summarize as one of three source-integration modes and never had the student practice it. The student re-raised this open question in g9_l10, g9_l14, g9_l15 ("When will I get summarizing practice, since lesson 5 defined it but no lesson has asked for it yet?"). No later journal entry records it being taught. This matters because g10 and g11 lean hard on "analysis is not summary" and "don't tour the sources," i.e., the curriculum spends three grades warning against a move it never taught as a controlled skill. (SINGLE-MODEL, traceable to g9_l05 struggled/open items)
- **One structural confusion, not a skill gap: two g9 gates.** The student was confused at g9_l27: "my memory says I already did g9_l26_gate_essay, yet this lesson calls itself 'the argument gate' and 'the terminal task the whole course led to' -- either my notes got ahead of me or the course has two gates." The course never explains that l26 is the informational gate and l27 the argument gate. (SINGLE-MODEL, g9_l27 open question)

### Specific per-lesson confusion points (all SINGLE-MODEL, all traced)
- **g9_l02 Step 6, g9_l03 Step 6, g9_l04 Step 5, g9_l08 Step 5: missing multiple-choice options bug.** The student could "only answer in principle" four separate times; the bug "wanders around" between step numbers. It disappeared around g9_l05-l06 and the student repeatedly asked whether it was fixed on purpose or by luck.
- **Answer-key-inside-the-question flaw, curriculum-wide.** First flagged at g9_l04 Step 10, then g9_l05 Step 8, g9_l06 Step 8, g9_l07 Step 8, g9_l08 Step 8, g9_l09 Step 8, g9_l10 Step 7, g9_l11 Step 7, g9_l12 Step 9, g9_l13 Step 7, g9_l14 Step 8, g9_l15 Step 7, g9_l16 Step 8, g9_l17 Step 7, g9_l18 Step 8, g9_l22 Step 7, g9_l25 Steps 7-8, and continuing into g10 (l02 Step 8, l04 Step 8, l05 Step 8), g11 (l04 Step 7), and g12 lessons. The student's own words: "If the 'diagnose' step is always going to hand me the check fully answered... why not rename it 'copy the rewrite'?" This is the single most persistent instructional defect in the curriculum and it makes the diagnosis skill untested at every grade.
- **Correct-MC-option restates the lesson's teaching (giveaway streak):** g9_l09 Step 6, g9_l14 Step 6, g9_l15 Step 6, g9_l19 Step 7, g9_l23 Step 5, g9_l24 Steps 4 AND 5, g10_l01 Step 6, g10_l02 Step 4, continuing through g11_l01 Step 5 ("ninth-ish lesson running").
- **Worked example pre-answers the writing task:** g9_l13 Step 7 -> Step 8, g9_l17 Step 5 -> Steps 6/8, g9_l20 Step 3, g9_l23 Step 4 (student had to switch sides "to avoid transcribing"), g10_l01 Step 3, g10_l05 Step 3 -> Step 7, g10_l20 Step 4, g11_l01, and onward.
- **Difficulty inversion at g9_l13:** the student called transitions "a step DOWN in difficulty after twelve lessons of claims, warrants, and calibrating reasoning -- the transition words themselves are fifth-grade material." Similar inversion at g10_l11 (arranging three pre-written sentences after having written full multi-paragraph essays in g9_l23/l24/l26/l27): "looping back to a much easier version." Sequencing lessons that operate below the student's already-demonstrated level is the clearest ordering defect in the vertical progression.
- **Dangling instructions:** g9_l15 and g9_l19 both told the student to pick a side in the source and then no step ever collected the claim/thesis; the student pre-registered and confirmed the prediction at g9_l19.

### Caveat
All of this is one simulated walk by one persona (achiever_fable), a fast, high-retention student. Some "everything was easy, nothing new" reactions are PERSONA-SPECIFIC to a learner who retains at ~0.85-0.95 confidence; a weaker student might need some of the re-teaching. But the structural bugs (missing options, pre-answered diagnostics, giveaway MC options, reused sources) are observable lesson properties, not persona judgments.

## 2. Are there redundancies?

## Q2: Cross-grade redundancies

**Every item below is SINGLE-MODEL (one walk, achiever_fable, felt_repeated flags plus journal corroboration within that same walk). No item is corroborated by a second model or persona. Do not treat these as multi-source confirmed; treat them as one consistent witness with detailed receipts.**

### Tier 1: Later grade re-teaches an earlier grade's skill nearly wholesale

1. **g11_l13_woven_synthesis repeats g10_l19_woven_check.** Student: "the same lesson... almost the same wording ('organize by point, not by source')," counted as the fifth time doing the move (after g10_l20, g10_l24, g11_l12). Step 2's plan order repeats g10_l20; Steps 5-6 repeat exact question types from g10_l19/l20. This is the most direct cross-grade duplicate in the record.
2. **g11_l12_full_synthesis repeats g10_l20_plan_cross_text / g10_l24_gate_cross_text.** "Mostly review" of the g10 cross-text unit; "don't tour the sources" = g10_l19's "don't survey" renamed. Only new bit: "a conclusion no single source states on its own."
3. **g12_l12_which_frq_type repeats g11_l29_which_task_type.** "The entire core skill... is exactly what g11_l29 taught," with g11_l27/l28/l31 having already rehearsed each type. g12_l13_interleaved_pair is "literally g11_l29 again" plus g11_l27+l28 stapled; only ~10% new (the "bleed" idea).
4. **g12_l09/l10/l11 (timed argument/analysis/synthesis) repeat g11_l23/l24/l27/l28/l31.** g12_l09 restates g11_l23 (time_budget) and g11_l24 (plan_fast) "including the same 'don't polish the intro forever' trap warning." g12_l10 "is basically stapling l23 + l24 + l31 together." g12_l11 re-taught "the exact cap-the-reading, plan-fast, reserve-time-to-land move from g11_l27." g12_l08 recycles the 40-minute split from g11_l23 (the lesson itself admits it).
5. **g12_l02/l03 (genuine complexity, hold tension) repeat g10_l02/l03 concede-then-hold.** "Formula vs. genuine complexity is basically G10's fake vs. real concession with new vocabulary"; g12_l03's Moves 1-2 are "heavily recycled from G10's concede-then-hold lessons," and its weighing repeats g11_l20/l21. g12_l04 adds a full-essay assembly that g11_l22 already did.
6. **g11_l01_scope_claim repeats the g9 sharpen-claim lessons (g9_l02, plus the vague-vs-specific MC work).** "~70% the G9 'sharpen a claim by making the side specific' skill wearing a new name." Likewise g10_l12_strong_weak_twin repeats the same g9 vague-vs-specific move ("only the label 'circular'... was new"), and g10_l26_precision_pass is "mostly a rebrand" of the same g9 claims-unit material. The specificity skill is thus re-taught in g9, g10 (twice), and g11.
7. **g11_l17_source_free_position repeats g9 claim lessons + g10 concede-hold.** "~70% repackaged": side-with-because and 3-question checklist (g9_l01/l02), specific-not-vague (g9), fence-sitter/collapse spotting (g10_l02), fact-vs-claim (g9_l01).
8. **g10_l11_logical_sequence repeats g9_l21_multi_para_coherence** (and the g9 full-essay lessons g9_l23/l24/l26/l27) at a lower difficulty: "arranging three pre-written sentences feels like looping back to a much easier version."
9. **g10_l10_add_delete repeats g9_l25_essay_revision plus the g9 relevance test (g9_l06):** "the relevance test... wearing a revision hat."
10. **g11_l16_midgate / g11_l27 / g11_l28 vs. their own inputs and vs. g10_l24:** g11 runs a midgate (l16), then two full rehearsals (l27, l28), then a task-type lesson (l29), then a gate (l30). The student counted g11_l27 as "at least my second full rehearsal" and g11_l29 as "~10% new / 90% review." Combined with g10_l24 and g12_l16, the curriculum runs at least six near-identical full-performance gates/rehearsals across grades. g12_l16 "is the same as g11_l30... the lesson itself admits 'no new teaching.'"

- **g12_l05_sophisticated_analysis** traces to g9_l02's "which matters because" so-what logic; g12_l06 repeats g11_l21/l22/l27 (the lesson admits it).

### Cross-grade SOURCE/topic recycling (distinct from skill recycling)
- **AI workforce retraining source (BLS 33.5% / 3.1% / NSF 22%->24%)** appears in g9_l11 (the 33.5-vs-3.1 pair), g11's not-X-but-Y transfer, and then g12_l02, g12_l04, g12_l07, g12_l09, g12_l15 ("I've read it about three times now" -- an undercount by their own record).
- **Congestion pricing source set** used in g10_l15 through g10_l24 ("a fifth-plus lesson" by g10_l20) and echoed in g10_l18's topic territory.
- **Water cycle:** g9_l03 transfer -> g9_l10 -> g9_l12 -> g9_l20. **Volcanoes:** g9_l03 -> g9_l12 -> g9_l14 -> g9_l25 -> g9_l26 gate topic (a "warm topic for a supposedly cold gate"). **Photosynthesis:** g9_l03 -> g9_l13 -> g9_l19 (exact same MC question) -> g9_l22. **Electricity mix (4,178 billion kWh):** g11 synthesis lessons -> g11_l25. **NCES phone stats (76.9/90.9/65.8):** g9_l05 -> g9_l07 -> g9_l09 -> g9_l17. **Community service source:** g9_l22 -> g9_l23 (reused "as if it were new") -> g9_l18. **Free public transit:** g9_l02 -> g9_l16 -> g10_l05.
- Topic recycling directly weakens gates: g9_l26's "unseen" volcano topic repeats the controlling-idea transfer lesson, and g12's sophistication/timed lessons keep resurfacing the same AI-retraining source, so "cold" performance is warm.

- **g9:** dense front-loading redundancy: l02~l01, l03~l02, l04~l01+l03, l07~l05, l08~l07, l10~l09, l11~l09, l18~l17/l10/l09, l19~l03, l23~everything prior, l24~l23, l26~l24, l27~l26/l23. The warrant definition appears "word-for-word" in l09, l18, and l23.

- **g12:** l07~l02-l06, l14~l27/l28 pacing, l16 gate admits "no new teaching."

### Legitimate repeats (do not fix)
The student consistently ACCEPTED gate/rehearsal repetition when framed honestly: g9_l26 ("a gate is supposed to be a cold repeat of the terminal task"), g10_l24, g11_l16, g11_l30, g12_l16. The problem is not that gates recap; it is that (a) non-gate lessons also recap, and (b) gate sources are not actually cold.
