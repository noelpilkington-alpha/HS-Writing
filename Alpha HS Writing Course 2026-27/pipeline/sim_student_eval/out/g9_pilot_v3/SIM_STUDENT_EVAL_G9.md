## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Do the lessons make sense?

Overall verdict: the content of individual lessons is understandable, often too easily so. Both walks report skills "clicking" in seconds across most of the arc. The confusion that does exist is almost never about the writing concepts; it is about lesson mechanics, naming conventions, and one genuinely missing teach (counterclaim). Specific issues:

### Conceptual clarity, lesson by lesson
- **g9_l01 (arguable_claim)**: Clear for both walks. average_fable left with a real, articulated gap: the lesson only checks whether a reason EXISTS, not whether it is a GOOD reason ("Not sure how good my reasons actually are"). This question ("How do I tell a GOOD/strong reason from a weak one?") stays open in average_fable's memory through l01, l02, l03, and is only partially addressed by l09 (conf on judging reason quality never rises above 0.6).
- **g9_l02 (sharpen_stakes)**: Content clear, but average_fable was confused that two different tests were both called "the 3 questions" (lesson 1's claim test vs lesson 2's strength test) and "had to reread and invent my own distinction." Also unsure whether naming a "kind" (tutoring) counts as specific the same way an amount ($50) does; the lesson never resolved this.
- **g9_l03 (controlling_idea)**: average_fable reported "whiplash of being told to take NO side after two lessons drilling 'take a side'," resolved only by the read-the-verb rule. The "3 questions" naming collision hit a third time; the student invented the name "explain test" to cope.
- **g9_l04 (interleave)**: average_fable had a genuine unresolved task-scoping question at Step 13: when the task asks to explain WHY animals migrate but the source lists three parts, does the controlling idea preview all three or just the "why"? Student answered at ~70% certainty and the lesson never confirmed. achiever_fable raised the same question in its own l03 Step 12 and l04 open questions (photosynthesis inputs, migration parts). This is a real ambiguity the lessons never close.
- **g9_l05 (integrate_quote)**: Two unresolved mechanics questions from average_fable that no later lesson answers: (a) who goes in the attributive tag when the article states a fact without naming its origin, and (b) can an exact number like "17.7 billion" be copied into a paraphrase without quotation marks. The number question resurfaces in l10 ("only about 80% sure it's allowed since no lesson has ever said so") and is never answered by the course.
- **g9_l06 (select_relevant)**: average_fable was left unsure whether "the passage" is a specific enough source name (conf 0.5) and whether a debate side's argument inside a source counts as evidence (conf 0.4). Neither is resolved later.
- **g9_l08 (reasoning_vehicle)**: Clear for both, but average_fable could not tell what Step 5 was even asking because the MC options never rendered (see structural bugs below), and asked whether combining "because" and "so" in one sentence is allowed; never answered.
- **g9_l09 (warrant)**: Concept clear, but average_fable flagged a real transfer worry: "having 'because' in a sentence FEELS like reasoning even when it's just restating — I'm afraid I'll write a secret restate and not catch it without the checklist in front of me" (conf 0.5 on that skill).
- **g9_l11 (two_evidence)**: average_fable hit two taxonomy gaps the lesson never covers: a fact that is neither a repeat nor a baseline ("this felt like a third category it never addressed"), and whether a before/after pair from one source sentence is one fact or two. Both left open.
- **g9_l12 (calibrate_reasoning)**: average_fable was confused about scope of allowed knowledge: Step 8 asked what ash actually does, but the Step 2 source had no ash facts, "so I had to go outside the source and nobody said whether that's okay." Never resolved.
- **g9_l13 (transitions)**: average_fable reports cause vs sequence genuinely blur (conf 0.5): "Taking in CO2 happens before making sugar (sequence) but the sugar also depends on it (cause)." Worse, achiever_fable found Step 8's instructions contradicting the lesson's own rule: the prompt said to use sequence signposts where the real relationship is cause, forcing the student to disobey the instructions to follow the lesson's principle. Both walks independently hit this cause/sequence seam at l13 Step 8.
- **g9_l27 (gate_argument_essay)**: The single worst sense-making failure in the course. Step 1 claims "no new teaching," but counterclaim was never taught anywhere; achiever_fable: "the one-sentence bullet definition is the only instruction I've ever gotten on naming and answering a counterclaim, so I had to attempt it on a best guess" (conf 0.5, the lowest skill confidence in the entire achiever walk after lesson 8).

### Structural defects that break sense-making (both walks)
- **Missing multiple-choice options (CORROBORATED, cross-persona, one model)**: MC options failed to render in g9_l02 Step 6, g9_l03 Step 6 (achiever) / Step 6 (average), g9_l04 Step 5, g9_l08 Step 5. achiever_fable counted "FOURTH lesson in a row with a busted multiple-choice step"; average_fable "literally could not answer and had to skip... the THIRD time this bug happened." Students were forced to guess what the question was even testing.
- **Recognition, not application (achiever_fable, systematic)**: Step 4's correct MC answer is the worked example's AFTER sentence, verbatim or near-verbatim, in l02, l03, l04, l06, l07, l09, l10, l11, l12, l13, l16, l17, l18, l20, l21, l22. achiever_fable counted this explicitly seven-plus times ("seventh lesson with this exact flaw"). average_fable independently flagged it at l13 Step 4 and l18 Step 4.
- **Checks that run themselves (CORROBORATED)**: The prompt answers its own check questions before asking the student to check, in l02 Step 9, l03 Step 9, l04 Step 10, l05 Step 8, l06 Step 8, l07 Step 8, l08 Step 8, l09 Step 8, l10 Step 7, l11 Step 7, l12 Step 9, l13 Step 7, l14 Step 8, l16 Step 8, l17 Step 7, l18 Step 8, l19 Steps 8-9, l20 Step 7, l21 Step 7, l22 Step 7, l25 Steps 6/8. Both walks flagged this repeatedly and independently; average_fable: "that diagnosing is the whole skill" (l08).
- **"Which ONE question did your rewrite fix?" when the draft fails multiple checks (average_fable, recurring)**: l01 Step 8, l02 Step 8, l03 Step 9, l04 Step 10, l07 Step 8, l09 Step 8, l11 Step 7. The student repeatedly could not give the demanded single answer honestly.
- **Unnamed proliferating checklists (average_fable, recurring)**: by l13 the student counted five different tools all informally called "the 3 questions" or similar, and had to invent private names ("explain test," "hinge test," "relationship check") to keep them straight. Raised as an open question to the course at l03, l05, l07, l08, l10, l13; never addressed.
- **Self-confessing distractors**: MC options that explain their own wrongness inside the option text, flagged by achiever_fable at l16 Step 6, l20 Step 4 option B, l21 Step 5 option B, l23 Step 5.
- **Teach step pre-announcing the tested trap**: l17 Step 1 names "the partial paragraph" that Step 5 then tests; l22 Step 1 names the trap Step 5 tests; l26 source and l27 source pre-announce the ideal answer (achiever_fable, all four instances).

## Q2: Redundancies

Redundancy is the dominant complaint of both walks. The felt_repeated flags cluster into six blocks. Labels follow the deterministic table.

### CORROBORATED-strength cluster (cross-persona, one model: both fable personas flagged)
1. **The claim arc repeats itself three times (l01 → l02 → l03 → l04)**. PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model; achiever_fable + average_fable). l02 recycled l01's 3-question checklist "presented as a brand-new tool," the same fact-instead-of-claim diagnose task, and the same pay-for-grades topic across five straight steps. l03 re-taught fact vs claim and argue-vs-explain from l01 in Step 1. l04's Step 1 "even admits 'You already know both products'" and its Step 4 was "the exact same multiple choice as Lesson 3's, with the same 'previews parts but takes a side' trap."
2. **l07 is l05 again, nearly wholesale**. PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model). "The entire lesson repeats g9_l05 almost exactly — dropped vs. integrated quotes, the attributive tag vocabulary, the 3-question check, even the same 76.9% phone source and numbers. Nothing new was taught." achiever_fable's struggled list for l07 confirms: "No real struggles with the skill itself — nothing new was taught."
3. **The reasoning chain re-teaches the same connective move four times (l08 → l09 → l12, plus l10's re-teach)**. PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model) for l08-vs-l07, l09-vs-l08, l12-vs-l09. Students estimate only ~30% of l08 is new over l07 (the because/but/so job distinction), l09 is l08 "with new packaging" (the word "warrant"), and l12's core skill "is exactly what g9_l09 taught," with only predict-the-score arguably new. SINGLE-MODEL corroboration on top: achiever_fable flagged l10 Step 1 re-defining warrant from scratch and Step 6 duplicating l09's lone-warrant drill; average_fable flagged l11 Step 5's diagnosis as "a repeat of lesson 9."
4. **Paragraph assembly is taught three separate times (l10, l17, l18)**. PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model) for l17-vs-l10: "The lesson openly admits no new moves are taught... Lesson 10 already did [the assembly]." SINGLE-MODEL (average_fable) for l18-vs-l10/l17: the paragraph half of l18 "is basically what I've been doing since lesson 10 and lesson 17," with the observable self-check as the only new idea. achiever_fable's l18 record agrees: "the only new idea was the observable self-check."
5. **l19 is l03 with the word 'thesis'**. PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model). "Even the exact MC traps (sneaky-side, bare-opinion) were recycled from g9_l03... The only new thing was the vocabulary word 'thesis.'" achiever_fable's l19 record: the new element "took about ten seconds in Step 1."
6. **Cohesion at paragraph level rehashes cohesion at sentence level (l21 vs l13/l14; l15 vs l13+l14)**. l21-vs-l13 is PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model): the LINK half is l13/l14 relocated; "Only the ORDER half was genuinely new." l15-vs-l13 is SINGLE-MODEL (achiever_fable): l15's teach is "l13 plus l14 stapled together; the only new thing is doing both at once."
7. **The capstone/gate block does the same terminal task four times (l23, l24, l26, l27)**. l24-vs-l23 and l26-vs-l24 are PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model): l24 is l23's structure with the thesis swapped for a controlling idea; l26 is "same terminal task as g9_l24... just relabeled a 'gate,' and on volcanoes again — the third volcano lesson in a row, so the magma → eruption → monitoring structure was already memorized before reading the source." l27-vs-l26 is SINGLE-MODEL (achiever_fable) and l27-vs-l23 SINGLE-MODEL (average_fable): "I've now done the same plan → draft → check full argument essay at least three times." Also l23's Steps 1-2 re-taught five earlier lessons word-for-word (PERSONA-SPECIFIC, cross-persona, one model).
8. **l16 recycles the course's structural template and even its transfer topic**. PERSONA-SPECIFIC (cross-persona, one model): the 3-question checklist format is "the ~sixth reuse," and l16's "new" free-transit transfer topic is the same one used in l02.
9. **l25 is a checklist of l05/l07/l09/l13/l14/l15/l18**. SINGLE-MODEL each way, but flagged by BOTH personas against different anchors (achiever_fable vs l18: "Step 8 IS Lesson 18 redone with a volcano topic"; average_fable vs l09: "the BEFORE/AFTER warrant example was 'kind of a repeat'"; only "predict the score" was new). Treat as effectively corroborated repetition of l25's content, even though the anchor lessons differ.

### SINGLE-MODEL redundancies
- **l06 vs l05** (average_fable): the source-named question of the relevance test "is just lesson 5 again"; Step 4 reused Step 3's exact three facts.
- **l10 vs l03** (average_fable): Step 2 reused the exact same water cycle article ("96.5 percent" and watersheds), "so I skimmed it instead of rereading."
- **l20 vs l03** (average_fable): same water cycle passage a third time; "felt like a rerun."
- **l20 vs l17** (achiever_fable): Step 3's BEFORE was "literally" l17's paragraph-outline format stretched over an essay.
- **l22 vs l02** (achiever_fable): the conclusion "upshot" is the l02 stakes move relocated; the intro half was genuinely new.

### Within-lesson padding (both walks, pervasive)
Steps 6-8 (sometimes 6-8-plus-11) are the same exercise done three times in one lesson in l15, l16 (four times), l17, l18, l20, l21, l22, l25 (achiever_fable counts, average_fable independently at l13 "Steps 6, 8, and 10... by the third one I was on autopilot" and l18/l20). l26 pasted the full source twice (Steps 2 and 3); l27 Step 3 reprinted the source verbatim after Step 2 (achiever_fable).

### Source-material reuse degrading transfer
Bird migration used in l06, l07, l09/l10, l14, and l18's "cold transfer" (achiever_fable count); water cycle article reused in l03, l10, l20 (average_fable); the 76.9% NCES phone stat in l05, l07, l17 (both walks); volcano topic in l19, l21, l24-adjacent steps, l25, l26, l27 (achiever_fable: "the third volcano lesson in a row"). Reuse turned intended transfer tasks into memory tasks.
