## 1. Do the lessons make sense?

## Q1: Do the lessons make sense?

Individually, yes: no walk reported failing to understand a lesson's core concept. The through-line (claim → stakes → controlling idea → evidence → warrant → paragraph → cohesion → tone → plan → essay → gates) is legible to both personas; achiever_fable at g9_l23 wrote that "assembling everything cold is the point of the whole course." But several specific clarity failures recur:

**Broken multiple-choice steps (both walks, systemic).** Answer options never rendered in at least four lessons: g9_l02 Step 6, g9_l03 Step 6 (average_fable: "I literally could not answer it and had to skip"), g9_l04 Step 5, and g9_l08 Step 5 (achiever_fable: "FOURTH lesson in a row with a busted multiple-choice step"; average_fable: "THIRD time this bug happened"). Students had to guess what the item was testing.

**Identically named, different checklists (average_fable, persistent).** By g9_l08 average_fable counted FOUR different tools all called "the 3 questions" (l01 claim test, l02 strength test, l03 explain test, l08 hinge test), inventing private names to keep them straight; by g9_l15 it was "the FIFTH checklist/tool in the course... I'm the one keeping them all straight in my head." The achiever flagged the same template fatigue at g9_l16 ("about the sixth" 3-question checklist). The course never names or numbers its tools.

**"Which ONE check did your rewrite fix" when the draft fails multiple checks (average_fable, recurring).** g9_l01 Step 8, g9_l02 Step 8, g9_l03 Step 9, g9_l04 Step 10, g9_l07 Step 8, g9_l09 Step 8, g9_l11 Step 7, g9_l16 Step 8. Every occurrence produced genuine confusion ("picking only one felt wrong").

**Checks that run themselves.** Both walks, many lessons: the prompt answers its own diagnostic questions before asking the student to diagnose. achiever_fable tracked this across g9_l02 Step 9, g9_l04 Step 10, g9_l05 Step 8, g9_l06 Step 8, g9_l10 Step 7, g9_l12 Step 9, g9_l13 Step 7, g9_l14 Step 8, g9_l17 Step 7, g9_l18 Step 8, g9_l19 Steps 8-9, g9_l20 Step 7, g9_l21 Step 7, g9_l22 Step 7, g9_l25 Steps 6/8. average_fable independently flagged it at l03, l06, l08, l09, l10, l11, l12, l13, l14, l15, l16. This makes the "check" steps incoherent as assessments: there is nothing left to do.

**Recognition instead of application in Step 4-style MC.** The correct option is the worked example's AFTER sentence verbatim. achiever_fable counted this in l02, l03, l04, l06, l07, l09, l10, l11, l12, l13, l16, l17, l18, l20, l21, l22; average_fable confirmed at l13 Step 4 ("literally the same sentence pair as the Step 3 worked example") and l18 Step 4.

**Instructions contradicting the lesson's own rule (both walks, g9_l13 Step 8).** The prompt told students to use sequence signposts where the real relationship is cause; both students independently overrode the instruction and both left the lesson asking whether they were right to disobey.

**Confusing sequencing near the end.** g9_l25 drops back to revising a two-sentence paragraph immediately after two full essays; achiever_fable: "the course drove me up a mountain and handed me a tricycle." And g9_l15 Steps 2 and 9 ask average_fable to pick a debate side that is never used anywhere in the lesson ("the side-picking felt pointless and confusing").

**Legitimate unanswered content questions.** average_fable carried real questions the course never answers: how to judge reason QUALITY (raised at l01, still open at l09); whether numbers can be copied into a paraphrase without quotation marks (raised at l05, still ~50-80% unsure at l10: "somebody should answer this in a lesson"); what to put in an attributive tag when the source doesn't name its own source (l05); how to tell cause from sequence when both fit (l13, confidence 0.5); borderline slang words (l16, confidence 0.4); whether own-head facts are allowed in warrant repair (l12).

## 3. Do the lessons prepare them for the full compositions?

## Q3: Do the lessons prepare students for the full compositions?

Evidence: composition transcripts exist for achiever_fable only (l18, l23, l24, l26, l27); average_fable's walk provides can_do/confidence corroboration.

**g9_l18 (fresh paragraph + self-check): prepared, over-prepared.** Every component was in place before the lesson: claim (l01), sourced fact (l05/l07), warrant (l09), assembly (l10, l17), transitions (l13). The student wrote in the composition: "the 'write a complete body paragraph' part of today is a rerun of g9_l10 and g9_l17 with a checklist stapled on." Only the observable self-check was new, and it did real work exactly once (achiever_fable caught its own unsourced "as the source notes" fact). SINGLE-MODEL for the transcript; average_fable's l18 flag corroborates the over-preparation (CORROBORATED that skills were in place).

**g9_l23 (full argument essay): prepared for everything it required.** The composition names every part's source lesson (thesis l01/l19/l20, SPO l20, body l09/l10/l17, transitions l13/l21, conclusion l22) and the student executed cold, handling even a counterargument point in the plan by improvisation. Readiness gap: none for the required moves, but the "plan before you draft" content occupied two MC slots and Step 7 regressed to a fill-in SPO grid after l20 had the student write one cold. CORROBORATED (both personas' l23 flags confirm the skills pre-existed).

**g9_l24 (full informational essay): prepared.** "The teach 'clicked' in about five seconds because it's 90% review": l23's structure + controlling idea (l03/l19) + l20's plan. Entered with 0.8 confidence on the lesson's own diagnostic trap. CORROBORATED via both personas' l24 flags.

**g9_l26 (gate informational essay): prepared, but the gate is invalid as a cold test.** All four checklist moves were in place (controlling idea l19, body l10/l17, transitions l13, upshot l22). However the "unseen source" was volcanoes for the third consecutive lesson; achiever_fable: "I could write this essay's skeleton from memory before I even read Step 2," and left asking "Would I pass a genuinely cold gate on a topic I haven't already worked for three lessons in a row?" (self-rated confidence that the lesson needed to exist: 0.2). CORROBORATED (both personas flagged l26 as l24 relabeled).

**g9_l27 (gate argument essay): one genuine readiness gap, the counterclaim.** achiever_fable, in the composition and struggled list: "Counterclaim was never actually taught in any earlier lesson... the one-sentence bullet definition is the only instruction I've ever gotten," and Step 1 falsely claimed "no new teaching." The student scrolled the full memory (claims, stakes, warrants, evidence, transitions, tone, SPO, intros/conclusions, essays, revision) and found no counterclaim lesson, then attempted it on a best guess, self-rated confidence 0.5 (versus 0.8-0.9 on everything else in the same lesson). This is the only skill required by a terminal composition that no lesson taught. SINGLE-MODEL for the gap itself (only achiever_fable's transcript and struggled list document it), but both walks flagged l27 as a redundant second gate.

**Bottom line:** the curriculum over-prepares for the compositions on every taught skill (students arrived at each capstone with all parts at 0.85-0.9 confidence and called the teach steps review), while leaving exactly one required move (counterclaim, l27) untaught and neutering the l26 gate's transfer validity through topic reuse.

## 4. Do the courses prepare them for the tests?

## Q4: Do the lessons prepare students for the tests?

The evidence contains no separate end-of-course test data or MCQ match-rate figures; readiness must be judged from the in-lesson check items and the gate lessons, which function as the tests. No formal match rates can be reported, and this section states only what the walks show.

**Items students could not attempt at all (CORROBORATED, cross-persona, one model): the blank multiple-choice steps.** g9_l02 Step 6, g9_l03 Step 6, g9_l04 Step 5, g9_l08 Step 5. Both walks report the options never appeared; average_fable "literally could not answer and had to skip" three times, achiever_fable hit it four times. These are the only test items in the evidence that were unattemptable, and the cause is a rendering defect, not a skill gap.

**One test item requiring an untaught skill (SINGLE-MODEL, achiever_fable): the counterclaim requirement in the g9_l27 gate.** No lesson in the sequence taught naming and answering a counterclaim; l23 and l26 did not require it. The student attempted it from a one-sentence definition at 0.5 confidence. Missing-skill trace: there is no counterclaim lesson anywhere in l01-l26.

**In-lesson MC items were passed at effectively 100% by both personas, but for the wrong reason.** The recurring pattern (Step 4's correct answer = Step 3's AFTER sentence verbatim; flagged by achiever_fable across ~16 lessons and by average_fable at l13 and l18) means the items measured recognition of a sentence read minutes earlier, not the skill. Distractors that "confess their own wrongness inside the option text" (achiever_fable at l16 Step 6, l19 Step 6, l20 Step 4, l21 Step 5; average_fable at l16 Step 6) further inflate pass rates. So high in-lesson accuracy is weak evidence of test readiness on a genuinely cold item.

**The l26 gate's validity is compromised (CORROBORATED):** the "unseen" volcano source followed two volcano lessons, so the gate partly tested memory of last lesson. Both personas' flags note the structure was memorized in advance.

**Questions the students carried into any future test unanswered (average_fable):** reason quality judgment (confidence 0.4-0.6 from l01 through l09), numbers-in-paraphrase quotation rules (0.5 at l10), cause vs. sequence discrimination (0.5 at l13), borderline formality words (0.4 at l16), side-arguments-as-evidence (0.4 at l06). If a test probes any of these, the average persona is unprepared by their own confidence estimates, and the lessons never closed the gaps despite repeated open questions.
