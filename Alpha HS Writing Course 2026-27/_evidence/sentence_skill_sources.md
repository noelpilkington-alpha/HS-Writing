## Notes

**Silences (where a source provides NO grade signal):**
- **TWR is grade-agnostic** by design. It sequences skills by difficulty (expansion → combining → appositives → subordinating conjunctions) but does not assign them to specific HS grades. TWR's "Level 1" vs "Level 2" labels are novice/advanced markers, not G9 vs G11 (TWR2.0 §The Six TWR Principles, loc ~1263-1281; content drives rigor, not grade).
- **CCSS/ACC grade bands are coarse (9-10 vs 11-12).** For most sentence skills, standards say "by end of 9-10 band" or "by end of 11-12 band" without pinpointing the exact grade of first instruction. Sentence expansion, combining, appositives, subordinating conjunctions, fragments/run-ons, because/but/so, parallelism, modifier placement all fall under ACC.W.CONV.1 or CONV.3 with NO discrete grade boundary (05_AlphaCommonCore §CONV domain; 01_ccss_adherence_map notes CCSS L.9-10 vs L.11-12 bands do not split these skills).
- **Crosswalk is test-centric, not instruction-centric.** It tells us when skills are ASSESSED (e.g., G9 TESTED fragments/run-ons, G10 TESTED parallelism), but that does not mean the skill is NEW at that grade (could be inherited from MS or G9 and re-tested at G10).

> Provenance note: the two conflicts below were adjudicated by a live council run on 2026-07-08 (seats: TWR, Kirschner & Hendrick, TSIS; synthesis judge). Each seat returned its own take + source anchors; the judge ruled by evidence strength and named the losing position (never averaged). This replaces an earlier unverifiable "Task 4" resolution whose council invocation could not be confirmed from the run transcripts. The genuine council reached the SAME splits and grades, so the placements below stand on a verifiable record.

- **Concision / precision (Layer 1):** SPLIT confirmed by all three seats independently.
  - (1) **Precision (basic word-level accuracy) NEW @ G10.** (2) **Concision + stylistic precision NEW @ G11.**
  - Seat takes: TWR SPLIT, basic precision via kernel->expansion, concision as a later REVISING skill post sentence-combining/appositives (TWR2.0 §Ch 3 loc ~2514-2700; §Ch 7 loc ~6002-6065). Kirschner & Hendrick SPLIT by element interactivity: basic precision = 2-3 interacting elements (scaffolded multiple-choice), concision = 5-7 elements (open, unbounded revision) needing automated lower-level schemas first (HLH §Ch 2 loc ~197; KB 00 §1.1 load-additivity, §1.2 expertise-reversal). TSIS SPLIT: precision = metacommentary entry move, concision = later economy-for-effect (TSIS metacommentary ch loc ~4500-4800).
  - Judge ruling: precision @ **G10** (not TWR's G9). Decisive evidence = Kirschner & Hendrick element-interactivity (grade A) + the G10 EOC "knowledge of language" editing crosswalk anchor; the scaffolded multiple-choice format at G10 holds load to 2-3 elements. **Losing position: TWR's G9 placement** (grade B; places it one year early relative to the CLT gatekeeper and the state-test anchor). Concision @ **G11** = full three-seat consensus (crosswalk: ACT/SAT open revision tests it at G11).

- **Nuanced / counterclaim-aware claim (Layer 2):** SPLIT confirmed by all three seats independently.
  - (1) **Counterclaim-aware claim NEW @ G10** (binary "Although X, Y" acknowledgment; ~2-4 interacting elements). (2) **Nuanced claim NEW @ G11** (multi-dimensional qualification embedded in the claim; 7-10+ interacting elements).
  - Seat takes: TWR SPLIT, counterclaim-aware = subordinating-conjunction sentence-combining move (TWR2.0 §Ch 4 loc ~3730-3829), nuanced = post-appositive multi-clause syntax (loc ~4120-4265); argues nuanced is reachable at G11 once the syntax is taught, NOT G12.
