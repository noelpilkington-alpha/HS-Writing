## Notes

**Silences (where a source provides NO grade signal):**
- **TWR is grade-agnostic** by design. It sequences skills by difficulty (expansion → combining → appositives → subordinating conjunctions) but does not assign them to specific HS grades. TWR's "Level 1" vs "Level 2" labels are novice/advanced markers, not G9 vs G11 (TWR2.0 §The Six TWR Principles, loc ~1263-1281; content drives rigor, not grade).
- **CCSS/ACC grade bands are coarse (9-10 vs 11-12).** For most sentence skills, standards say "by end of 9-10 band" or "by end of 11-12 band" without pinpointing the exact grade of first instruction. Sentence expansion, combining, appositives, subordinating conjunctions, fragments/run-ons, because/but/so, parallelism, modifier placement all fall under ACC.W.CONV.1 or CONV.3 with NO discrete grade boundary (05_AlphaCommonCore §CONV domain; 01_ccss_adherence_map notes CCSS L.9-10 vs L.11-12 bands do not split these skills).
- **Crosswalk is test-centric, not instruction-centric.** It tells us when skills are ASSESSED (e.g., G9 TESTED fragments/run-ons, G10 TESTED parallelism), but that does not mean the skill is NEW at that grade (could be inherited from MS or G9 and re-tested at G10).

**Layer 1 CONFLICTS flagged (Task 2 verdict = CONFLICT, requires resolution):**
- **Concision / precision:** Crosswalk tests at G10 (EOC, VT word choice) but also G11 (ACT/SAT "Style, tone, precision, concision"); standards say 9-10 band (Knowledge of Language, ACC.W.CONV.3) but don't pin timing; TWR says revision-phase (not grade-specific). Crosswalk's G10 vs G11 test signals disagree on whether this is NEW at G10 or inherited-and-refined at G11. Requires resolution.

**Layer 2 apparent disagreements (from Task 1, not ruled yet by Task 2):**
- **Sentence combining timing:** TWR treats it as foundational/early (after expansion); Crosswalk shows it TESTED at G9. Standards are silent on timing (no grade-specific boundary). Task 2 ruled NEW @ G9 based on converging crosswalk + TWR signals, but a deeper question remains: is combining NEW at G9 or INHERITED from MS? (Sources suggest G9, but MS inheritance is possible.)
- **Appositives timing:** TWR sequences appositives after combining (intermediate TWR activity); Crosswalk subsumes appositives under G10 "modifiers" without naming them explicitly; Standards are silent. Task 2 ruled NEW @ G10 based on crosswalk + TWR sequence, but signal is indirect (not named explicitly in crosswalk).
- **Parallelism pedagogical foundation:** Crosswalk explicitly names parallelism TESTED at G10 (VT, EOCs); TWR does not name parallelism at all; Standards silent. Task 2 ruled NEW @ G10 per crosswalk, but TWR's silence suggests it may be a "conventions-only" skill without compositional foundation. Not a conflict in grade placement, but a gap in pedagogical approach.
- **Rhetorical syntax and AP sophistication groundwork:** Crosswalk places rhetorical syntax at G11-12 (ACT/SAT/AP); TWR is silent (not in scope); Standards silent (no ACC/CCSS code for rhetorical syntax). Task 2 ruled NEW @ G11 (rhetorical syntax) and NEW @ G12 (AP sophistication), but no source specifies whether they require earlier groundwork or are introduced cold.
- **Genre-specific sentence moves grain mismatch:** Crosswalk ties them to essay modes and tests them at specific grades (e.g., claim at G9-10, synthesis at G11); TWR is silent on sentence-level genre moves (TWR teaches at paragraph/composition level, not sentence syntax); Standards expect the essay skills by grade bands but do not isolate the sentence structures for those skills. Disagreement may not be real (different grains: essay skill vs. sentence syntax), but it's a gap to reconcile (Task 3 will address Layer 2).
