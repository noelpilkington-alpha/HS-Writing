# Sentence-Skill ASSESSMENT Evidence (how the reference tests measure sentence skills, by grade)

**Purpose:** back the progression map's spiral bands (introduce -> develop -> master) with what the reference standardized tests actually document about HOW and AT WHAT COMPLEXITY each sentence-level capability is assessed per grade. Feeds the assessment-evidence columns + the own-words exemplar reconstructions in `Sentence_Progression_G9-12.md`.

**Method:** 5 subagents each mined ONE released-test batch (`ReleasedTests/batch1-5*.md`), extracting only what the file documents, each claim cited to its batch + section. Synthesized here 2026-07-08. No fabrication: every row traces to a batch file, which itself contains no copyrighted item text (format + rubric-trait names + URLs only).

**Copyright posture (HARD):** the reference files contain NO verbatim test items by design. Therefore the exemplar items in the map are OWN-WORDS RECONSTRUCTIONS faithful to each documented format, never real items. Each exemplar cites the official test + the public rubric/format it mirrors.

---

## THE CENTRAL FINDING (reframes the whole ask)

**The reference tests do NOT decompose sentence skills to the granular level.** Across 20+ systems, NOT ONE names appositives, sentence combining, parallelism, subordinating conjunctions, or because/but/so as a discrete tested item. They assess at the **umbrella-capability level**, in two places:

1. **Inside the essay rubric** as a Conventions / Language dimension (the dominant state-EOC pattern: NY Regents, LA LEAP, MD MCAP, SC, FL B.E.S.T., MA MCAS, SBAC full-write). These score "command of grammar/usage/mechanics/sentence structure" holistically, lowest-weight trait (0-2 or 0-3).
2. **As edit-in-context selected-response** items, but only in a subset of systems: STAAR + TN (dedicated revising/editing passages), ACT + SAT (the PRIMARY writing measure in ~26 states at G11), SBAC (a few "Edit/Clarify" items), FL (inside FAST Reading), MA (discrete Language SR items in the item library).

**Implication for the map:** we cannot show "the actual G10 appositive item" because no such labeled item exists anywhere. What we CAN show, faithfully, is the umbrella capability each grade's test measures, the item FORMAT, and an own-words reconstruction at that grade's complexity. The granular skills (appositive, combining, etc.) are OUR pedagogical decomposition of those umbrella capabilities, grounded in TWR, not in the tests' own taxonomy.

## The one EXPLICIT spiral the sources document

The crosswalk (`skills_by_grade_crosswalk.html`, "Sentence structure & boundaries" SR row + "Knowledge of language / style" SR row) is the only source that states a grade-by-grade escalation of sentence capability, and it is TESTED (not laddered) at every grade:

| Cluster | G9 | G10 | G11 | G12 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Sentence structure & boundaries** (ACC.W.CONV.1) | "Fragments, run-ons, combining" (state EOC/editing) | "Sentence formation, modifiers, parallelism" (EOC, FAST, VT) | "Complex sentence revision" (ACT English, SAT) | "Rhetorically effective syntax" (AP) [LADDER] |
| **Knowledge of language / style** (ACC.W.CONV.3) | "Word choice, basic tone" [LADDER, CCSS 9-10] | "Precision, concision, word choice" (EOC, VT) | "Style, tone, precision, concision" (ACT Knowledge of Language, SAT Craft & Structure) | "Rhetorical style control" (AP) [LADDER] |

This is the empirical backbone of the spiral bands. Note G12 is LADDER (AP composition MC + FRQ Row B), not a discrete summative editing test.

## Per-system evidence (what each test documents about sentence assessment)

### G9-G10 state EOCs

- **STAAR English I (G9) & English II (G10)** [batch1 lines 61-63]: dedicated Writing section = 2 revising + 2 editing passages, MC + non-MC. Revising items target "organization, development, coherence, **sentence effectiveness**, and rhetorical/meaning choices"; editing items target "conventions (grammar, usage, punctuation, capitalization, spelling, **sentence boundaries**)." Item shells: MC (4 options), Multiselect, Inline Choice/dropdown, Hot Text, Text Entry, Match Table Grid, Hot Spot. **KEY:** "English I and English II ... structure and rubrics are IDENTICAL ... they differ only in passages/prompts and cut scores" (batch1 lines 13-14). So the G9->G10 escalation is NOT in the item design; it is in text difficulty + cut scores.
- **Ohio ELA II (G10)** [batch1 lines 105-107]: NO dedicated editing passage. Language & Conventions (L.9-10.CSE.1/2, KL.3, VAU.4/5/6) assessed inside passage-based item sets + the essay Conventions dimension. ELA I (G9) shares design.
- **Tennessee English I (G9) & II (G10)** [batch1 lines 162-164]: Editing Task (Subpart 4, 8-16 items): short passage with embedded underlined errors, each a MC item with a **"No change"** option. Sub-skills: (a) conventions (grammar, usage, punctuation, capitalization, **sentence formation/boundaries**, spelling); (b) effectiveness for meaning/style (word choice, clarity, rhetorical/organizational). Same structure G9 = G10 (differ in passages + cut scores).
- **NY Regents (G11)** [batch2 lines 72-84]: NO editing/revising SR items at all (keyword sweep returned zero). Conventions scored ONLY via essay rubric "Control of Conventions" dimension.
- **Louisiana LEAP English I/II (G9/G10)** [batch2 lines 149-162]: NO discrete editing MC. SR items are reading/vocabulary. Conventions = PCR "Written Knowledge & Use of Language Conventions" dimension, 0-3, x1 weight.
- **Maryland MCAP (G10)** [batch2 lines 211-224]: NO editing MC section (sweep found none). Conventions = PCR "Written Conventions" dimension, 0-3.
- **South Carolina EOCEP English 2 (G10)** [batch3 lines 111-117]: 16-20 SR Writing items covering "composition, revision, and language/conventions editing sub-skills." Exact shells COULD_NOT_VERIFY. TDW essay rubric Trait 2 = "punctuation, capitalization, spelling, sentence structure, usage/mechanics" (0-2).
- **Florida B.E.S.T./FAST (G9 & G10)** [batch3 lines 160-173]: B.E.S.T. Writing essay has NO SR items. Editing/conventions assessed in FAST ELA READING via MC, Multiselect, Hot Text, EBSR (2-part), Table Match, Multimedia. Sub-skills = "grammar, conventions, rhetoric, morphology, context/connotation." Essay Language dimension (1-4) = "academic vocabulary, sentence variety, standard-English conventions, tone/voice." **G9->G10 escalation documented:** stimulus words G9 ~1,100-1,900 -> G10 ~1,100-2,000; G10 argument prompts "may draw on American historical speeches/essays" (batch3 lines 148-149). Escalation is in TEXT, not item design.
- **Massachusetts MCAS (G10)** [batch5 lines 71-78]: essay rubric Dimension B "Standard English Conventions" = "(1) **sentence structure (variety, relative to length)**; (2) grammar, usage, mechanics (relative to complexity/length)." PLUS discrete Language SR items (L.PK-12.1/2/3) in formats MC (1pt), MC Two-Part (2pt), Technology-Enhanced (2pt), browsable in the item library by standard (specific skills not cataloged in our file).
- **PA Keystone Literature (G10-11)** [batch5 lines 129-130]: NO editing items (those lived in the retired English Composition Keystone). Reading/literary-analysis only.
- **NJ NJSLA/NJGPA** [batch5 line 182]: conventions inside PCR "Knowledge of Language and Conventions" dimension, not discrete MC.

### G11-G12 advanced tier (the maturity ceiling) [batch4]

- **ACT English (G11)** [batch4 lines 30-42]: passage-based editing/revising; underlined/highlighted portions; most items offer a **NO CHANGE** option; test-taker acts "as a writer who makes decisions to revise and edit a text." Reporting categories: "Conventions of Standard English" (38-43%, incl. **Sentence Structure and Formation**, punctuation, usage), "Production of Writing" (38-43%, topic development, organization, unity, cohesion), "Knowledge of Language" (18-23%, **precision and concision** in word choice, consistency in style/tone). Enhanced format: 50Q/35min.
- **SAT Standard English Conventions + Expression of Ideas (G11)** [batch4 lines 85-95]: discrete items, short passage (25-150 words) or a pair, one MC each; "items testing similar skills are grouped and arranged **easiest-to-hardest**"; **adaptive** (Module 2 difficulty set by Module 1). Domains: "Standard English Conventions" (sentence structure, usage, punctuation), "Expression of Ideas" ("revise texts to improve effectiveness of written expression and **meet rhetorical goals**"), "Craft and Structure" (vocabulary in context, rhetorical evaluation).
- **AP English Language (G12)** [batch4 lines 127-147]: MC section includes "20-22 Writing (**evaluate revisions to stimulus texts**)" of 45 MC. FRQ 6-point analytic rubric; Row B (Evidence & Commentary 0-4): "grammatical/mechanical errors that **interfere with meaning** cap this row at 3"; Row C Sophistication (0-1) implicitly requires syntactic maturity + style as a rhetorical choice.
- **AP English Literature (G12)** [batch4]: same 6-point FRQ rubric; sentence control implicit in Evidence & Commentary + Sophistication.

**Maturity demand (what "mastered" means at the ceiling)** [batch4 synthesis]: G11-G12 assess sentence skills as **purposeful revision decisions in extended, authentic discourse** (ACT "as a writer"; SAT "meet rhetorical goals" + adaptive difficulty; AP tolerance of minor errors but a cap when errors interfere with meaning). Contrast with G10 EOC editing, which isolates sentence CORRECTNESS in shorter contexts. That correctness -> rhetorical-choice shift IS the develop -> master transition.

## What this evidence supports, and what it does NOT

**Supports:**
- A real, TESTED spiral for the two umbrella clusters (sentence structure & boundaries; knowledge of language) from G9 through G12. Combining/expansion/subordination are NOT isolated to G9: they are the G9 entry into a capability re-assessed at rising complexity every year. Precision/concision genuinely escalates G10 (basic) -> G11 (stylistic/rhetorical), matching the council's split.
- The introduce -> develop -> master banding: introduce where the capability first appears, develop at G10 EOC correctness-in-context, master at G11/G12 rhetorical-revision-in-extended-text.

**Does NOT support (be honest in the map):**
- Any claim that a specific test has "an appositive item" or "a parallelism item at G10." The granular skills are OUR decomposition (TWR-grounded) of umbrella capabilities the tests score holistically. The map must label granular-skill exemplars as pedagogical reconstructions, not as items lifted from a test.
- A within-system G9->G10 item-difficulty escalation for STAAR/OH/TN (identical by design; escalation is via text + cut scores). The cross-GRADE escalation is real but is a cross-SYSTEM pattern (G9-10 EOC correctness -> G11 ACT/SAT rhetorical revision), documented by the crosswalk, not by any single test's internal G9-vs-G10 design.
