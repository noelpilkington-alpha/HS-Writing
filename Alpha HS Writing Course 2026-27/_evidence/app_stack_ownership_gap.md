# Alpha App-Stack Ownership + HS Writing Gap Scope

**The reframe (Noel, course-correction):** our job is NOT to build a full sentence-skill progression from scratch. It is a GAP ANALYSIS: map which existing Alpha ELA apps own which standards/skills, then scope HS Writing to cover ONLY the tested writing skills those apps do NOT cover. Some cross-pollination is expected (apps that spiral into HS); focus on the skills students must execute on standardized tests that no current app teaches.

## FINDING 1: The app-stack mapping STOPS at Grade 8

Distinct grade labels in the coverage sheet: Kindergarten, Grade 1-8, "Grades 6-8". **There are NO G9-12 rows.** The existing Alpha ELA app stack is a K-8 stack. That is the structural reason HS Writing is being built: grades 9-12 are uncovered by the app stack.

**Implication:** the "inherited floor" is whatever the K-8 apps leave a student holding at the end of Grade 8. HS Writing owns the G9-12 tested writing skills that (a) no app covers because the stack ends at G8, AND (b) are not merely a spiral-continuation an existing app already handles into HS.

## FINDING 4: What HS Writing SHOULD own (the genuine gap = tested writing skills NO app covers)

Filtering the tested writing capabilities against app ownership, the genuine HS gap is the COMPOSITION + RHETORICAL tier that no K-8 app and no conventions engine (EGUMPP) covers:

| Genuine HS gap (tested, not app-owned) | Why it's a gap | Tested by |
|----------------------------------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Source-based argument/analysis/synthesis ESSAY** (claim, evidence integration, counterclaim, nuance) | AlphaWrite stops at G8 + is general composition, not source-based EOC essay; no app does the STAAR/EOC source-based essay | STAAR EOC, all state EOCs, SBAC, AP |
| **Text-dependent + rhetorical analysis** (device -> effect -> warrant; author's choices) | No app teaches analytical writing about texts at HS level | G10 EOC analysis, AP Lang/Lit, SAT essay |
| **Rhetorical concision / style as a choice** (beyond EGUMPP wordiness drills) | EGUMPP does mechanical wordiness; the rhetorical "meet the goal" tier is not app-owned | ACT KoL, SAT Expression, AP |
| **Nuanced/counterclaim-aware claim, synthesis-across-sources** | Composition-level moves above EGUMPP + above AlphaWrite G8 | STAAR counterclaim, SBAC/AP synthesis |
| **AP sophistication (significance, context, competing perspectives)** | No app; also the two audit gaps N2/N3 | AP FRQ, ACT |
| **The genre-general SENTENCE substrate at HS** | **NOT a gap** - EGUMPP (conventions G3-10) + AlphaWrite (TWR sentence craft G3-8) own it; HS RETRIEVAL-GATES it | (owned) |

**Bottom line for the roster:** HS Writing's sentence-level responsibility is NOT to teach the phrase/clause/parallelism/modifier repertoire (EGUMPP owns that, through the 9-10 band). It is to teach the **genre-specific, rhetorical, and composition-level sentence moves** that turn EGUMPP-correct sentences into test-winning argument/analysis/synthesis writing - and to RETRIEVAL-GATE the EGUMPP/AlphaWrite substrate, routing remediation to those apps on failure.

---

## FINDING 5: Cross-pollination policy (Noel: "there will be some cross-pollination")

Where a skill is app-owned but HS tests demand it AT A HIGHER TIER, HS Writing does NOT re-teach the mechanic; it EXERCISES it inside composition and, where the tested tier exceeds the app's ceiling, teaches only the delta:
- Parallelism/phrases/clauses/modifiers = EGUMPP-owned -> HS gate + apply in composition; teach no new mechanic.
- Wordiness (mechanical) = EGUMPP-owned -> HS gate; but rhetorical concision (style-for-effect) = HS-owned delta.
- The whole conventions strand is scored HOLISTICALLY inside the essay rubric anyway (lowest-weight trait), so HS "teaches" it only by flagging it in revision feedback, not as lessons.
