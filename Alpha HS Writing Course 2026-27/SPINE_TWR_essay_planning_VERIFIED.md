# TWR essay-planning: VERIFIED against the primary text (TWR 2.0 PDF)

Source: "The Writing Revolution 2.0" (Hochman & Wexler), the licensed PDF in the repo root. Verified by reading
the pages directly (not Fable's recall). This SUPERSEDES SPINE_TWR_essay_planning_research.json where they differ.

## Verified facts (with PDF page-index anchors; add book page numbers when citing student-facing)

1. **The essay-planning strategies live in CHAPTER 9: "Moving On to Compositions: The Transition Outline and the
   Multiple-Paragraph Outline"** (PDF idx ~240). NOTE: Fable guessed "Ch. 6 / A Whole Greater Than Its Parts" -
   that is the 1st-edition; in the 2.0 edition we license it is CHAPTER 9. Use Chapter 9.

2. **A composition (essay) is defined (verbatim, idx 241):** "A composition (essay) is a series of paragraphs
   united by a common theme." Characteristics mirror effective paragraphs: Structure, Coherence (transition
   words), Unity (every paragraph supports the thesis), Well-constructed sentences.

3. **TWR provides TWO essay planners (a progression), not one** (idx 242). Fable named only the MPO and MISSED
   the TO:
   - **Transition Outline (TO)** - Appendix L, Exhibit 9.1. "designed for students who are ready to write more
     than one paragraph but not yet ready for the task of writing complete introductions and conclusions." Form:
     TOPIC -> THESIS STATEMENT (one sentence) -> Main Idea + Details rows (numbered 1, 2, ...) -> CONCLUDING
     STATEMENT (one sentence). The intro/conclusion are single sentences (a deliberately easier option).
   - **Multiple-Paragraph Outline (MPO)** - Appendix Q, Exhibit 9.2. The fuller planner. Form: TOPIC -> THESIS
     STATEMENT -> **Introduction** row -> numbered body paragraphs (each a Main Idea + Details block) -> (full
     conclusion). "Some students may be ready to move directly to the more challenging MPO without first creating
     TOs." (idx 242, 244)
   - There is also a **Pre-Transition Outline (PTO)**, Appendix J (idx 245) for the earliest stage.

4. **The MPO/TO use a two-column "Main Idea | Details" structure** (Exhibits 9.1, 9.2, idx 243-244). The exact
   TWR labels are "Main Idea" and "Details" (details in note form: key words/phrases, abbreviations, symbols) -
   NOT "topic sentence + note-form evidence" (Fable's paraphrase; the concept matches, the labels differ).

5. **SPO -> TO/MPO prerequisite skills (verbatim, idx 245):** from ample SPO practice students already have:
   "Write supporting details in note form, using key words and phrases, abbreviations, and symbols" +
   "Organize and categorize details." TWO NEW skills for the essay outline: "Generating a thesis statement that
   summarizes the theme or main idea of an entire essay" + "Generating a concluding statement that rephrases the
   thesis statement." This confirms the SPO is a PREREQUISITE building block, and the essay planner is a distinct
   new tool - exactly the reviewer's point.

6. **Creating the outline is itself the valuable activity** (idx 242): "creating the outline is a powerful
   activity in itself, regardless of whether a student turns it into a draft" - so a plan-only lesson is
   TWR-legitimate.

## What the current lesson (g9_l20_spo_plan_v3_1, id ACC-W910-L-G9-C904-0019) gets wrong
Teaches "plan an essay with a single-paragraph outline: a one-line thesis + THREE ordered body POINTS each
naming its evidence." Wrong because: (a) uses the SPO (single-paragraph tool) for an essay; (b) TWR's essay
planner is the TO/MPO; (c) "body POINTS" flattens the Main Idea + Details two-column structure; (d) no
Introduction/Conclusion rows (the MPO's defining additions); (e) treats the SPO AS the essay planner, violating
the SPO(prereq) -> TO/MPO(new tool) progression.

## The fix (design decision for the rebuild)
Rebuild ACC-W910-L-G9-C904-0019 around the **Multiple-Paragraph Outline (MPO)** as the essay planner (it is the
essay/U4 grain, C.9.04 essay funnel). Honor the progression explicitly: "you already own the SPO; the MPO is the
next tool up - it adds a thesis for the whole essay, an Introduction, ordered body paragraphs (Main Idea +
Details each), and a Conclusion, connected with transitions." Keep TWR's real labels: THESIS STATEMENT,
Introduction, Main Idea, Details, Conclusion, transitions. Grain stays multi_paragraph (plan scales to essay).
Consider whether a sibling lesson should teach the TO as the easier stepping-stone, or whether one MPO lesson
suffices for G9 (open question for Noel / the build).
