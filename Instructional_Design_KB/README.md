# Instructional Design Knowledge Base (HS Writing)

Purpose: make our team proficient in instructional design as it applies to our
grades 9-12 writing courses (mastery-based, self-paced, Alpha School / 2 Hour
Learning model), and give us the means to USE that knowledge when we build
lessons.

Built 2026-07-06 from a fan-out deep-research pass (105 agents, 23 primary/
secondary sources, adversarial 3-vote verification of every headline claim)
plus the on-disk primary-source library and the existing
`Learning Science Synthesis for Writing Brainlift.md`. This KB EXTENDS that
synthesis; it does not replace it.

## How the pieces fit

| File | What it is | Use it when |
|---|---|---|
| `00_Knowledge_Base.md` | The reference. Four layers of ID, each principle evidence-graded and cited with effect sizes. | You want to know what the research actually says and how strong the evidence is. |
| `01_Applied_Toolkit.md` | The runnable part. Design principles turned into decision rules and build-time checklists mapped to our lesson spine. | You are building or reviewing an actual lesson and want a rule to apply. |
| `02_Framework_Audit.md` | Validate-then-extend scorecard for the five frameworks we already use (SRSD, CLT, discrimination-before-production, scaffold fading, mastery/self-paced). | You want to know which of our design choices are backed, which are shaky, and where to go next. |
| `03_Sources.md` | Annotated bibliography with links, quality grade, and what each source supports. | You need to cite something or check a claim at the source. |

## The four layers (what a proficient ID needs to hold)

1. **Learning-science foundations** - cognitive load, worked examples,
   expertise reversal, spacing, retrieval, transfer. The "why" under every
   design move.
2. **Writing-specific pedagogy** - SRSD, the Writing Next ranked menu, Writing
   to Read, sentence combining, the grammar-in-isolation trap. What actually
   moves adolescent writing.
3. **Instructional-design process and models** - Backward Design/UbD, 4C/ID
   whole-task design, Bloom mastery learning / 2-sigma, ADDIE, Gagne. How to
   structure a course.
4. **Assessment and measurement** - feedback that works (Hattie/Timperley,
   Black & Wiliam), rubric types, self-assessment and calibration, mastery
   gating. How to know it worked and drive the next step.

## Evidence grading used throughout

- **A (Strong)**: meta-analysis, systematic review, or WWC "Strong" rating;
  usually multiple converging sources.
- **B (Moderate)**: one solid meta-analysis or primary study, or WWC "Moderate."
- **C (Emerging / Contested / Extrapolated)**: single study, secondary/opinion
  source, or a well-supported finding being applied outside the domain it was
  proven in.

Every principle also carries a **domain-match note** when the evidence comes
from outside writing (much of learning science was proven in math, science,
vocabulary), because applying it to writing is an extrapolation we should flag,
not hide.

## The one-line version

Explicit strategy instruction through a model-practice-reflect cycle (SRSD) is
the best-evidenced lever in adolescent writing. Cognitive load theory and the
expertise-reversal effect justify our scaffold-and-fade design. Spacing and
retrieval make it stick. Feedback (task/process focused, not praise, not grades
alone) and trained self-assessment close the loop. Mastery/self-paced delivery
is strongly backed as a general method but NOT yet proven for writing
specifically, and is actively contested for writing, so we hold it with care.
