# Sources — Annotated Bibliography

Short key → full citation, link, quality grade, and what it supports in this KB.
Quality: **primary** (peer-reviewed study/meta-analysis, WWC guide, or the
foundational text) / **secondary** (reputable summary) / **blog** (practitioner,
useful but weakest). Every headline effect size in `00` was adversarially
verified 3-vote against the primary source (25/25 confirmed) unless flagged.

## Layer 2 — Writing pedagogy (strongest, most directly applicable)

- **[WWC-22]** IES / What Works Clearinghouse (2016, rev. 2019). *Teaching
  Secondary Students to Write Effectively* (Practice Guide #22, grades 6-12).
  Primary. — Rec 1 "Explicitly teach writing strategies using a
  Model-Practice-Reflect cycle" = **STRONG**; Rec 2 integrate reading/writing =
  MODERATE; Rec 3 use assessment to inform instruction = MINIMAL.
  https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/PracticeGuide/22

- **[WritingNext]** Graham, S. & Perin, D. (2007). *Writing Next: Effective
  Strategies to Improve Writing of Adolescents in Middle and High Schools*
  (Carnegie) / *A Meta-Analysis of Writing Instruction for Adolescent Students*,
  J. Educational Psychology 99(3), 445-476. Primary. — The 11-element ranked
  menu; SRSD 1.14 vs 0.62; grammar -0.32. 123 docs / 154 ES, grades 4-12.
  https://jgregorymcverry.com/readings/GrahamPerin2007WritingNext.pdf
  (Ordinal-only summary: https://www.adlit.org/topics/writing/summary-writing-next)

- **[WritingToRead]** Graham, S. & Hebert, M. (2010). *Writing to Read: Evidence
  for How Writing Can Improve Reading* (Carnegie). Primary. — Write-about-text
  improves comprehension (ES 0.40 / 0.51); extended writing strongest; low
  achievers benefit (0.63) ONLY with explicit instruction.
  https://carnegie.org/publications/writing-to-read-evidence-for-how-writing-can-improve-reading/

- **[SRSDonline]** srsdonline.org, *The Science of Writing*. Secondary. — SRSD
  170+ studies, ~3× traditional instruction, WWC strongest rating; 2023
  grades-6-12 meta-analysis; gradual-release stages.
  https://srsdonline.org/science-of-writing/

- **[TWR]** Hochman, J. & Wexler, N. *The Writing Revolution 2.0.* On-disk
  primary (practitioner). — Sentence-level method (because/but/so, expansion,
  appositives) operationalizing sentence combining; syntax before essay.

## Layer 1 — Learning science

- **[Paas-vanMerrienboer2020]** Paas, F. & van Merriënboer, J. (2020). Cognitive
  Load Theory: Methods to Manage Working Memory Load. *Current Directions in
  Psychological Science.* Primary. — Load additivity; worked-example effect;
  methods must match expertise; worked examples become redundant for experts.
  https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/0963721420922183

- **[ExpertiseReversal2025]** Tetzlaff, Simonsmeier, Peters & Brod (2025). *A
  cornerstone of adaptivity: A meta-analysis of the expertise reversal effect.*
  Learning and Instruction. Primary (PRISMA, 176 ES / 60 studies / 5,924
  participants). — Novice d=+0.505, expert d=-0.428; asymmetric; robust; **weaker
  in humanities/language** (the key caveat for writing).
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0959475225000660

- **[Kalyuga1998]** Kalyuga, Chandler & Sweller (1998). Primary. — Optimal format
  shifts with expertise (integrate text for novices → remove it for experts);
  split-attention and redundancy effects.
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0361476X97909516

- **[Latimier2021]** Latimier, Peyre & Ramus (2021). Meta-analysis of spaced
  retrieval. *Educational Psychology Review* (29 studies). Primary. — Spaced vs
  massed g=0.74; expanding vs uniform g=0.034 (n.s.) → don't over-engineer
  intervals. https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10648-020-09572-8

- **[Carpenter2022]** Carpenter, Pan & Butler (2022). The science of effective
  learning with spacing and retrieval practice. *Nature Reviews Psychology.*
  Primary. — Spacing/retrieval durable across ages/domains; retrieval "can"
  promote far transfer; strategies underused because counter-intuitive.
  https://www.nature.com/articles/s44159-022-00089-1

- **[Corral-Carpenter2025]** Corral, D. & Carpenter, S. (2025). Retrieval
  practice and transfer of complex concepts. *Learning and Instruction*, Dec
  2025 (3 experiments). Primary. — Retrieval aids retention AND transfer, but
  transfer is **conditional**: needs multi-round practice + delay (Exp 1 single
  round/immediate = no transfer advantage).
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0959475225001434

- **[LearningStyles2024]** 2024 meta-analysis of learning-styles matching (21
  studies / 101 ES / 1,712 participants). Primary. — Matching g=0.31 but "too
  small and inconsistent to justify"; only 26% showed required crossover; all
  single-session. Multimodal instruction g≈0.70 is the better design.
  https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1428732/full

## Layer 3 — ID process & models (frameworks; low effect-size weight)

- **[Bloom1984]** Bloom, B. (1984). *The 2 Sigma Problem.* Educational
  Researcher 13(6), 4-16. Primary. — Tutoring +2σ; mastery learning +1σ;
  alterable-variables leverage table; the still-open 2-sigma challenge.
  (Caveat: the +2σ figure is widely under-replicated.)
  https://web.mit.edu/5.95/www/readings/bloom-two-sigma.pdf

- **[UbD]** Wiggins, G. & McTighe, J. *Understanding by Design* (ASCD white
  paper, 2014 ref.). Primary (framework; NO effect sizes). — Backward design 3
  stages; alignment; T/M/A coding; Six Facets; rote ≠ transfer.
  https://files.ascd.org/staticfiles/ascd/pdf/siteASCD/publications/ASCD_UBD_whitepaper.pdf

- **[TenSteps / 4cid.org]** van Merriënboer, J. & Kirschner, P. *Ten Steps to
  Complex Learning* (4th ed., 2024) & 4cid.org. Primary (framework). — 4C/ID
  four components (Learning Tasks / Supportive Info / Procedural Info / Part-task
  Practice); whole-task backbone; built-in scaffold fading; part-task only for
  high-automaticity components.
  https://www.taylorfrancis.com/books/mono/10.4324/9781003322481/ten-steps-complex-learning
  · https://www.4cid.org/about/

## Layer 4 — Assessment & feedback

- **[Hattie2007]** Hattie, J. & Timperley, H. (2007). *The Power of Feedback.*
  Review of Educational Research 77(1), 81-112. Primary (12 meta-analyses / 196
  studies / 6,972 ES). — Feedback ES≈0.79 but variable; 3 questions
  (up/back/forward); 4 levels (task/process/self-reg/self); self-level & praise
  ineffective (~0.12); extrinsic rewards negative.
  https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.3102/003465430298487

- **[BlackWiliam1998]** Black, P. & Wiliam, D. (1998). *Inside the Black Box.*
  Phi Delta Kappan 80(2). Primary (~580 studies). — Formative assessment gains
  0.4-0.7; helps low achievers most; grades-alone don't help; self-assessment
  essential; recognize goal / know position / close gap.
  https://kappanonline.org/inside-the-black-box-raising-standards-through-classroom-assessment/

- **[SelfPeerAssessment2022]** Meta-analysis of self- and peer-assessment (626
  ES / 175 studies / 19,383 participants, 2022). Primary. — Self g=0.585, peer
  g=0.606, combined 0.448; online boosts peer not self.
  https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1747938X22000537

- **[SelfAssessmentAccuracy2023]** Meta-analysis of self-assessment accuracy (160
  articles / 29,352 participants, 2023). Primary. — Overestimation bias g=0.206,
  correlation z=0.472; **feedback is the only significant moderator**; **rubrics
  alone n.s.**; calibration trainable; secondary students calibrate better than
  undergrads. https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s10648-023-09819-0

- **[CultOfPedagogy]** Gonzalez, J. *Holistic, Analytic, and Single-Point
  Rubrics.* Blog (2014). — Definitions/trade-offs of the three rubric types;
  Fluckiger (2010) single-point + co-creation + self-assessment raised
  achievement. Weakest source; use for the taxonomy, not effect claims.
  https://www.cultofpedagogy.com/holistic-analytic-single-point-rubrics/

## Contested / critique

- **[MasteryCritique]** Hammond & Barber (2024). *The limits of mastery
  learning* (Phi Delta Kappan) & related. Secondary. — Mastery is a category
  error for writing (generative, not discrete); fixed pacing mislabels slow
  writers; tight standards reward gameable formula. Concedes mastery grading
  reduces bias / removes behavior from grade / drives intrinsic motivation.
  https://kappanonline.org/the-limits-of-mastery-learning/

## Verification & coverage notes (honesty ledger)

- **25/25** headline claims confirmed by 3-vote adversarial verification; 0
  refuted. Three were 2-1 split with cores confirmed (SRSD grade-range
  parenthetical; redundancy journal-attribution; retrieval Exp-1 boundary).
- **Coverage skew:** the workflow's *final* synthesis kept only Layer-1/2
  findings. Layer-3/4 material here (Bloom, 4C/ID, UbD, Hattie, Black & Wiliam,
  rubrics, self-assessment) was recovered from the fetch-phase primary-source
  extractions in the run journal — these are from primary sources but were not
  put through the final 3-vote gate, so treat their exact figures as
  well-sourced-but-single-pass. Grades in `00` already reflect this (mostly B/C).
- **Domain extrapolation:** most Layer-1 findings were proven in math / science /
  vocabulary / research-methods, NOT writing. Every such use is flagged
  **[extrapolated to writing]** in `00`.
- **Aging:** Writing Next (2007) and Writing to Read (2010) are foundational but
  predate AI-assisted self-paced delivery; corroborated by later Graham work.
