## TIER 1 — Structural decisions (reshape Unit 1; Noel's call)

### 1A. Source-binding *(DECIDED — execute in edit pass)*
- **Decision (logged, memory `source-binding-decision`):** evolve the Issue Card into a real source (short passage / paired sources / three perspectives) + a **separately-scored comprehension pre-read** (MVP SPOV 5) + a Setup→Outline source-handling stage. Students paraphrase evidence FROM the source (house-style R.A.P.S.).
- **Why:** the governing MVP brainlift ("where the two documents conflict, this one governs") makes source-binding non-negotiable; the AlphaWrite house style and standards transcript agree. Our "📖 No passages / write from own knowledge" Unit 1 contradicts it. *(LearningSci/House-Docs report §C.)*
- **Reuses prior work:** the verified phones/athletes facts (Beland&Murphy, Birmingham, UNESCO, House v. NCAA, etc.) become the raw material for the authored source(s) — not discarded.
- **Open sub-decision for Noel:** **graduated** source-binding within Unit 1 (single short source early → paired sources by L5) to preserve the cognitive-load on-ramp the old G8→G9 bridge rationale protected — vs. full source-binding from L1. *Recommendation: graduated.*
- **Must come with load-management:** source-binding reintroduces the reading load the bare fact-card avoided → it requires the comprehension pre-read machinery, not just a longer passage.

### 1B. SRSD POW+TREE + "Memorize It" stage *(DECISION PENDING — Noel)*
- **The gap:** our shell (Teach/Model/Support/Independent/Transfer) omits SRSD **Stage 4 "Memorize It"** entirely. The corpus shows memorization-to-automaticity is load-bearing — it frees working memory for composing and is *what enables transfer to no-reference, timed exam conditions* (Ray & Graham 2020 p.6: "they cannot bring physical reminders... when taking the ACT test"). Mission-critical for A2 (ACT) and B-track (timed AP FRQs). *(SRSD report.)*
- **Recommendation (source-grounded, not yet adopted):**
  - **POW** (Pick idea / Organize notes / Write & say more) = the **L5 planning macro-routine**.
  - **TREE** introduced at paragraph level **~L3**, defined for our context as **T**opic(claim)/**R**easons+evidence/**E**xplain(=our implication)/**E**nding. ⚠ Canonical TREE's second E is "Examine" (a self-check), NOT "explain" — so we must adopt the "Explain" variant deliberately and **word it identically everywhere** (HTML, organizer, flashcards, rubric). The "examine" self-check becomes a separate revision step.
  - **Add a "Memorize It" checkpoint** to the shell between Model and Support; criterion = "articulate each step AND its purpose from memory" by L5; mechanics = low-stakes retrieval (flashcards/quiz openers). Memorize the *meaning*, not the letters.
  - **Memorization-gated fade** → feeds Goal 2 directly (see Tier 4).
- **Secondary call for Noel:** also bring in **STOP+DARE** (essay-grain, bakes in counterargument, exam-tested with 10th graders) as the L5 essay-level planner? *(SRSD report §2.)*
- **Decision needed:** adopt as recommended / adopt + STOP+DARE / hold.
