## 1 · What the brainlift actually specifies (verbatim)

**Two skill types** (¶91–93):
- **Through-line skills** — "built in every lesson, grown across the course."
- **Unit skills** — "the one new move each unit installs." (One per unit. Not five.)

**The 8-unit sequence** (¶94–103); English I (G9) = "the passage-based composition floor," frontier = the **Draft stage** where "the explain-the-evidence move lives." The four English I units:

| Unit | Verbatim title + the ONE new move |
|---|---|
| **U1** | **Reading-to-Write Foundations:** "reason rather than restate; read a passage for its idea; refine a response into a controlling idea. **Reasoning is taught before the thesis**, because the reasoning link is the move students actually struggle with." |
| U2 | **Expository Response-to-Text** (primary STAAR mode): a full informational response on the U1 moves, explain-the-evidence taught to standard. |
| U3 | **Argumentative Response-to-Text:** defensible position, line of reasoning, **counterargument (acknowledge, concede, respond)**, evidence integration. "Refuting the counterargument is what separates a 3 from a 2." |
| U4 | **Conventions, Short Responses, and the Gate:** sentence boundaries; sentence-combining; reading SCR; the cold English I gate. |

**The engine** (¶109–116): every lesson = one piece of writing through **Setup → Outline → Draft → Revise → Polish → Submit** (SRSD's six stages, digital). DI sequence = **Teach → Model-with-a-coping-false-start → Guided → Independent → a discrimination beat**, "concentrated on each unit's frontier stage." Scaffold fade Heavy→Medium→Light→None. **STOP+DARE and TREE** are the named planning strategies. **"Discrimination is the gateway to production. You cannot write what you cannot recognize"** (¶82) — "the first ~40% of instruction is discrimination."

## 3 · Proposed re-anchored Unit 1 (the brainlift's actual U1)

**One unit skill, installed across the lessons: the evidence-to-reasoning link ("reason, not restate"), taught BEFORE the thesis.** Three component moves, in the brainlift's order:

| New L | Move (brainlift U1) | What the student does | Copy-resistant because |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L1** | **Read a passage for its idea** | Read the source; identify its central idea; discriminate idea-vs-detail, restate-vs-reason. Mostly **discrimination** (sort/judge/mark in the passage), per the 40%-discrimination SPOV. | The answer is a *selection/judgment* in the passage, not a text to mirror. |
| **L2** | **Reason, not restate** (the frontier move) | Given a claim + evidence from the source, write the **reasoning** (explain HOW the evidence supports the point — the "this shows…" link). Coping-model the dropped-quote → repaired version. Scaffold-fade across new sources. | Reasoning is generative + source-specific; the fade swaps sources so there's no model to copy. |
| **L3** | **Refine a response into a controlling idea** | Turn the position into a tight controlling idea/thesis — AFTER reasoning exists. | — |

(Where "argue vs. persuade" goes: demote to a short **primer/discrimination beat** inside L1 — recognize a checkable reason vs. a feeling appeal — not its own lesson. It's a useful recognition, just not a unit skill.)

**The mnemonic question, resolved:** no writing mnemonic in L1 (it's discrimination). The **paragraph routine** enters at **L2** (when reasoning + a full claim-evidence-reasoning paragraph first exist) and the **essay routine (STOP+DARE / POW)** at the unit's writing/short-response end. R.A.P.S.'s "P = paraphrase + explain why it matters" now lands exactly where reasoning is taught — no longer corrupted.

**This also fixes the copy problem at its root:** an L1 built on discrimination (sort, judge, mark-in-passage) is intrinsically copy-resistant, and the one production move (L2 reasoning) is the one place the scaffold-fade earns its keep.
