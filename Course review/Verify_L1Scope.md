# Adversarial Verification — Unit 1 L1 Scope Claim

**Claim under test** (`Course review/Unit1_Reanchor_Plan.md` §3, §5): Unit 1's first lesson should be **"read a passage for its idea," DISCRIMINATION-LED** (sort/judge/mark-in-passage, minimal production), and **"Arguing vs. Persuading" should be DEMOTED from a full lesson to a short discrimination primer** inside L1.

**Method.** Tried hard to refute, using six sourced docs. Every quote below is labeled with file + line/¶. I separate the two sub-claims:
- **(A)** discrimination-led "read-for-idea" is the right Unit-1 opener; and
- **(B)** demoting argue-vs-persuade to a primer does not discard sourced value.

---

## Verdict

- **Claim A (discrimination-led "read-for-idea" opener): SUPPORTED-WITH-CAVEAT.** The lesson *type* is real and sourced (not invented), and the governing brainlift names "read a passage for its idea" as a literal U1 move. The caveat is the **ratio**: the strongest single quantification the plan leans on ("the 40%-discrimination SPOV") is **not in the governing MVP brainlift** — it is in the *superseded* Complete doc. The MVP supports *discrimination-before-production* qualitatively but **does not license an all-recognition, zero-production L1**; the engine itself bundles every lesson into a Setup→…→Submit production cycle. A pure-sort L1 risks over-rotating on recognition.
- **Claim B (demote argue-vs-persuade to a primer): SUPPORTED-WITH-CAVEAT — with one genuine orphaned-value risk.** The governing brainlift genuinely does **not** list argue-vs-persuade as a U1 unit skill, so demotion is defensible on scope grounds. BUT the L1 dossier's **single strongest finding** (UNC skeptic-reader, "almost verbatim") and the **inform-vs-argue recognition gap** are load-bearing and are *not* automatically rehoused by "read for the idea." Demotion is only safe if that material is explicitly relocated, not deleted. The plan §3 does not say where it goes. **This is the one place the plan is genuinely under-specified, and the refutation lands.**

Neither claim is **CONTRADICTED**. Both are defensible but each carries a real caveat the plan currently glosses.

## Evidence FOR

### A — "read a passage for its idea" is a REAL, sourced lesson type (not invented)

The governing brainlift names it verbatim, twice — as both a through-line skill and a U1 move:

> "Read for the idea | Work out what a source says or argues before writing about it | The tasks are passage-based; comprehension precedes composition"
> — `HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II … (MVP).md` line 183 (Through-line skills table)

> "Unit 1, Reading-to-Write Foundations: reason rather than restate; **read a passage for its idea**; refine a response into a controlling idea. Reasoning is taught before the thesis…"
> — same file, line 195

And the discrimination-led *format* has direct proof-of-existence in real curricula — it is not a learning-science assumption:

> "**LT3 · Highlight-the-evidence (hot-text)** … highlight the span that supports a given claim; selection is the answer … **STAAR Hot Text**; Quill; CommonLit … **yes — proven at scale**"
> — `curriculum_resource_base.md` line 47

> "**STAAR 'Hot Text' item type (TEA)** — student highlights the span that answers 'select the evidence that supports X'; the selection *is* the scored answer. Proof an auto-checkable highlight works at high-stakes scale."
> — `curriculum_resource_base.md` line 23

> "**LT2 · Discrimination (judge strong-vs-weak)** … sort/pick stronger response … get teaching feedback on *why* … **Excelsior auto-graded MCQ**; ATC annotate-then-compare; RWT sorts | yes (MCQ)"
> — `curriculum_resource_base.md` line 46

> "**Achieve the Core / Facing History** — multi-read close-reading + text-dependent-question ladder (literal → vocab → inference → craft → synthesis), every TDQ answerable only from the text."
> — `curriculum_resource_base.md` line 27

So the mark-in-passage / sort-and-judge L1 the plan proposes maps cleanly onto LT3 (hot-text) + LT2 (discrimination) + the TDQ close-reading ladder. **"Read the passage for its idea" is not being invented — it is the brainlift's own words plus three real item types.**

### A — discrimination-before-production is sourced and house-endorsed

> "**7. Discrimination is the gateway to production. You cannot write what you cannot recognize.** … each move is taught with a gated discrimination beat ('which is stronger, and why?') before any production … Sophistication is the result of strong discrimination skills: you cannot produce 'good' if you do not know what 'good' looks like."
> — `HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II … (MVP).md` lines 157–159 (SPOV 7 — the *governing* doc)

> "Comprehension of the solution must precede production. That is to say, the learner must be able to recognise a solution to a particular class of problems before he is himself able to produce the steps leading to it without assistance." (Wood, Bruner & Ross)
> — `LearningSci_and_House_Docs_Mining.md` line 49

> "Discrimination before production. Activity 1.1 (DISC: classify) precedes 1.2 (PROD: rewrite). This matches the book's repeated insistence … 'the component discriminations involved in a cognitive routine should be pretaught before the routine is introduced.' — p.339"
> — `Engelmann_DI_Mining.md` line 162

DI strongly endorses opening on the convergent, decidable recognition move:

> "DI fits CONVERGENT sub-skills, not generative composition … the source SUPPORTS this exactly. Convergent, decidable sub-skills — *argues vs. persuades*, *defensible vs. not*, *evidence + reasoning vs. assertion* … pass both tests … so DI is the right tool. The generative act … should be **released, not scripted**."
> — `Engelmann_DI_Mining.md` line 212

### B — argue-vs-persuade is genuinely NOT a brainlift U1 unit skill

The plan's scope argument holds against the governing doc:

> "**L1 Arguing vs. Persuading** | **Not a brainlift U1 move at all.** U1 is reading-to-write (reason / read-for-idea / controlling-idea). Argue-vs-persuade is a conceptual distinction the brainlift doesn't list as a unit skill."
> — `Unit1_Reanchor_Plan.md` line 34

This is confirmed directly against the brainlift: the U1 line (MVP line 195, quoted above) lists exactly three moves — *reason-not-restate, read-for-idea, controlling-idea* — and argue-vs-persuade is not among them. The course also **deliberately prunes** named conceptual lenses:

> "A lens earns its place only if it is itself a move the assessment scores. Earlier designs tracked six named models; the rest were restatements or are now taught as plain procedures. Pruning is itself an application of SPOV 1 — fewer load-bearing lenses beat a rich vocabulary of frameworks."
> — `HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II … (MVP).md` line 209

Argue-vs-persuade is exactly the kind of conceptual distinction this pruning principle targets: it is **not** itself a scored rubric move (STAAR scores Organization/Development + Conventions; ACT scores Ideas/Development/Organization/Language — none scores "did the student correctly classify a sentence as argument vs. persuasion"). On scope logic, demotion is consistent with the governing doc.

### B — the dossier itself rates argue/persuade as the THINNEST exact match

> "B01 was the 'thinnest exact match'" / "**The inform/explain-vs-argue contrast.** Flagged in the resource base (Part 4) as B01's thinnest match: free lessons teach argue-vs-*persuade* (our chosen axis), **not** inform/explain-vs-argue. *No clean reference lesson exists for the inform-vs-argue axis at G9.*"
> — `Unit1_L1_Source_Dossier.md` line 191

> "**Inform/explain vs. argue contrast (C.9.B01)** — public lessons contrast *argument vs. persuasion* (different axis). Adapt: pair an informational + argumentative text on one topic, label purpose before structure."
> — `curriculum_resource_base.md` line 69

The dossier also concedes the most novel parts of the current L1 are **our-synthesis / new-ground**, not strongly sourced as a *lesson*:

> "**'Write the persuasive version on purpose, then test it on a skeptic'** … *No source has students generate the weak/persuasive version themselves.*"
> — `Unit1_L1_Source_Dossier.md` line 189

So the *production-heavy* parts of the current argue/persuade L1 are the least-sourced parts — which cuts in favor of demoting them, and supports a discrimination-led opener.

## Evidence AGAINST

### Against A — the "40% discrimination" warrant the plan cites is NOT in the governing doc

The plan repeatedly leans on a quantified discrimination ratio:

> "**'Discrimination is the gateway to production. You cannot write what you cannot recognize'** (¶82) — 'the first ~40% of instruction is discrimination.'"
> — `Unit1_Reanchor_Plan.md` line 24

> "Mostly **discrimination** (sort/judge/mark in the passage), per the 40%-discrimination SPOV."
> — `Unit1_Reanchor_Plan.md` line 51

**The "~40%" / "first 40%" figure is not in the governing MVP brainlift.** The MVP's SPOV 7 (lines 157–159) states the principle *qualitatively* ("discrimination is the gateway … gated discrimination beat … before any production") but gives **no percentage**. The 40% number lives only in the **superseded** Complete doc:

> "We spend **40% of instructional time** on 'can you see it?' before asking 'can you do it?' This ordering is non-negotiable and applies to every node."
> — `HS Writing Brainlift - Complete.md` line 319 (SPOV Truth 3)

> "Locked SPOVs: … **Discrimination Before Production — first ~40% of instruction is discrimination** … gateway KCs are predominantly discrimination"
> — `LearningSci_and_House_Docs_Mining.md` line 189 (attributed to "the broader, partly-superseded design")

The MVP brainlift explicitly governs over the Complete doc:

> "Where the two documents conflict, this one governs the MVP."
> — `HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II … (MVP).md` line 3

**Refutation force:** the plan's headline justification for making L1 "mostly discrimination, minimal production" is anchored to a figure from the *superseded* document. Even read charitably (the 40% is a *course-wide* ratio, not a per-lesson mandate), it does **not** say "the first lesson is ~all discrimination." A discrimination-led L1 survives on the qualitative SPOV 7 — but the plan should **drop the "40%-SPOV" citation or re-attribute it**, because as written it cites the wrong doc and over-reads it into a single-lesson production floor.

### Against A — the governing engine bundles production into EVERY lesson

The plan's §5 says "minimal production" L1 and pushes the paragraph routine to L2. But the governing engine is explicit:

> "Every lesson is one piece of writing run through the AlphaWrite cycle: Setup, Outline, Draft, Revise, Polish, Submit. Early reps use the paragraph-writing architecture…"
> — `HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II … (MVP).md` line 231

> "The AI-Tutor direct-instruction sequence: Teach, Model-with-a-coping-false-start, Guided, Independent, and a discrimination beat, concentrated on each unit's frontier stage."
> — same file, line 244

Note the order: the **discrimination beat is the *closer* ("…and a discrimination beat")**, concentrated on the frontier stage — not a stand-alone, production-free opening lesson. And learning science warns explicitly against an all-recognition design with no production bridge:

> "PARTIAL. Unit 1 has a Model phase, but it is coping-modeling … not an explicit completion-task ladder. … add completion-assignment items between Model and Independent."
> — `LearningSci_and_House_Docs_Mining.md` line 23

> "Show a fully-worked model … then use **completion tasks** (give claim+evidence, student supplies reasoning) as the bridge to full independent writing."
> — same file, line 22

**Refutation force (partial):** a *zero-production* L1 is not what the brainlift or the worked-example research prescribes; both want recognition **feeding into** a production/completion bridge. The plan's own §3 actually preserves a small production move ("identify its central idea; discriminate idea-vs-detail, restate-vs-reason"), and §5 keeps production at L2 — so this is a caveat about *labeling/ratio*, not a contradiction. But "minimal production" should mean "a short completion/selection-justification rep," **not** "pure sort." If the build interprets "discrimination-led" as "no writing at all in L1," that would over-rotate.

### Against B — the dossier's STRONGEST finding (UNC skeptic-reader) is tied to argue/persuade and could be orphaned

This is the most serious refutation. The dossier states, unambiguously, that UNC's skeptic-reader is the single best-grounded element of the whole lesson:

> "**The skeptical-reader framing — the core of our lesson (VERBATIM):** think of your reader as 'someone who is perfectly smart but who doesn't necessarily agree with you' … 'do not think of your audience as capable of reading your mind' … generate objections by asking how 'someone who disagrees with you might respond.'"
> — `Unit1_L1_Source_Dossier.md` lines 105–108

> "**TEST (the skeptic test).** *grounding: real-lesson (UNC almost verbatim) — the strongest grounding in the whole lesson.*"
> — `Unit1_L1_Source_Dossier.md` line 155

> "UNC Chapel Hill Writing Center 'Argument' … *This is the single best match for our lesson's core move (the skeptical reader) of all sources reached.*"
> — `Unit1_L1_Source_Dossier.md` line 95

The skeptic test is the operational heart of the current L1, and the dossier rates it our-synthesis-but-real-concept:

> "no source operationalizes it as a one-question gate the student applies to their own sentence in a self-paced tool. … our skeptic-test step is the right instantiation; the grounding is real (UNC) for the *concept*."
> — `Unit1_L1_Source_Dossier.md` line 193

**Refutation force:** if argue-vs-persuade is collapsed to a "short discrimination primer — recognize a checkable reason vs. a feeling appeal" (`Unit1_Reanchor_Plan.md` line 55), the skeptic test ("would this convince someone who disagrees?") does **not** obviously survive in that primer, and it is **not** named anywhere in the brainlift's three U1 moves. The plan §3 gives **no destination** for the UNC material. It either (a) shrinks into the primer (losing its status as the operational self-check), or (b) gets orphaned. Given the dossier calls it the strongest-grounded element of the lesson, demoting without a relocation plan risks discarding the best-sourced piece of content the team has.

### Against B — the inform-vs-argue recognition gap is a standards risk the demotion does NOT close

> "if the standard (W.9-10.1 vs W.9-10.2) requires students to also distinguish *informational* from *argumentative*, that is a *separate* micro-lesson, not something to bolt onto L1. Confirm whether B01 owes the inform-vs-argue contrast…"
> — `Unit1_L1_Source_Dossier.md` line 191

The brainlift's U1→U2→U3 sequence is **expository response (U2) then argumentative response (U3)** — i.e. the course explicitly treats *informational vs. argumentative* as a real distinction students must operate:

> "Unit 2, Expository Response-to-Text (the primary STAAR mode) … Unit 3, Argumentative Response-to-Text…"
> — `HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II … (MVP).md` lines 196–197

**Refutation force (partial):** "read a passage for its idea" is about *comprehension of one source's central idea* — it does **not** inherently teach the inform-vs-argue *purpose* discrimination that the dossier flagged as B01's thinnest-but-real obligation. Demoting argue/persuade to a "checkable reason vs. feeling" primer addresses the *persuade* axis but leaves the *inform-vs-argue* axis unhomed. Since the brainlift's own U1→U2→U3 spine pivots on exactly that informational/argumentative split, the recognition gap is real — though the dossier itself notes this may belong in a separate micro-lesson rather than L1.

## Orphaned-value check — where does the demoted argue/persuade + UNC skeptic material go?

The plan §3 (line 55) relocates argue/persuade to a one-line spot ("demote to a short primer/discrimination beat inside L1 — recognize a checkable reason vs. a feeling appeal") but **does not address** the three sourced sub-elements the dossier surfaced. Honest accounting:

| Sourced element | Strength of grounding | Survives demotion as written? | Where it should go |
|---|---|---|---|
| **UNC skeptic test** ("would this convince someone who disagrees?") | **Strongest in the lesson** — UNC almost verbatim (`Dossier` 155) | **At risk** — not in the brainlift's 3 U1 moves; primer is too thin to carry it | Most natural home is **L2 "reason, not restate"** — the skeptic test ("would a doubter have to deal with this?") *is* the reasoning gate. Re-home it there, where the brainlift's frontier move lives. The plan should say so. |
| **Checkable-reason vs. feeling-appeal** discrimination | real-lesson (UNC+RWT+Purdue), `Dossier` 139–142 | Yes — this *is* the proposed primer | Keep as the L1 primer / discrimination beat. Fine. |
| **Inform-vs-argue (purpose) contrast** | new-ground, no clean source; flagged as B01's thinnest obligation (`Dossier` 191; `resource_base` 69) | **No** — not covered by "read for the idea" | Either a **separate micro-lesson** or fold into the **U1→U2 hinge** (expository vs. argumentative response). Confirm against W.9-10.1/.2. Do not assume "read for idea" covers it. |
| **Misconception warning** "persuasive language isn't banned" (Purdue) | High-value, `Dossier` G1 (line 172) | Unclear — currently lives in the argue/persuade lesson | Carry into the primer or L2 consolidation; cheap one-liner, don't lose it. |

**Bottom line on orphaning:** the *concept* (checkable reason vs. feeling) safely becomes the primer. But the **operationalized skeptic test** and the **inform-vs-argue contrast** are NOT automatically rehoused by "read for the idea," and the plan is silent on them. This is the verified refutation: **demotion is fine only if §3 is amended to explicitly relocate the skeptic test to L2 (reasoning) and decide the inform-vs-argue contrast's home.** As written, the plan risks dropping its best-sourced asset.

## What this means for the re-anchor

1. **Claim A — proceed, but fix the citation and the ratio language.** A discrimination-led "read-for-idea" L1 is genuinely sourced (brainlift line 183/195 + LT2/LT3/Hot-Text). **Two corrections:**
   - **Drop or re-attribute the "40%-discrimination SPOV" citation.** That figure is from the *superseded* Complete doc (line 319), not the governing MVP. The MVP supports discrimination-before-production qualitatively (SPOV 7), without a percentage. Citing it as governing is an over-read.
   - **Do not let "minimal production" become "zero production."** The governing engine runs *every* lesson through Setup→…→Submit (line 231) and the DI sequence ends on a discrimination beat *after* guided/independent practice (line 244). Keep a short completion/selection-justification rep in L1 (recognition → mark-in-passage → one-line "why") rather than a pure sort. This is also what the worked-example/completion research wants (`LearningSci` 22–23).

2. **Claim B — demote, but specify the relocation.** Argue-vs-persuade is correctly *not* a brainlift U1 unit skill (line 195, plus the pruning principle line 209), and the dossier rates it B01's thinnest match. **But amend §3 to state explicitly:**
   - the **UNC skeptic test moves to L2** ("reason, not restate") where it functions as the reasoning gate, not into the thin primer;
   - the **inform-vs-argue contrast** is decided (separate micro-lesson, or folded into the U1→U2 expository/argumentative hinge), checked against W.9-10.1/.2;
   - the **Purdue "persuasive language isn't banned" misconception line** is carried forward, not dropped.

3. **Net.** The scope decision is directionally right and survives an adversarial read — neither claim is contradicted by the sourced base. The two real findings the skeptic surfaced are: (i) the headline 40% warrant is mis-sourced to a superseded doc and should not be read as a per-lesson production floor; and (ii) demotion without an explicit relocation plan risks orphaning the lesson's **best-grounded** element (UNC skeptic test) and an open standards obligation (inform-vs-argue). Both are fixable by editing §3, not by abandoning the re-anchor.
