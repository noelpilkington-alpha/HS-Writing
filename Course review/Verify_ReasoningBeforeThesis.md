# Adversarial Verification — "Reasoning is taught BEFORE the thesis" (Unit 1 re-anchor)

**Claim under test** (from `Unit1_Reanchor_Plan.md` §1, §3): in a Grade-9 source-bound argument unit, the reasoning / "explain how the evidence supports the point" move should be taught **BEFORE** the thesis/position. "Reason, not restate" comes first (new L2); "refine a response into a controlling idea" comes after (new L3). This reverses the current build (position L2 → reasoning L3).

**Governing assertion** (the doc the plan says "governs"): `HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II Source-Bound Argument (MVP)`, Unit 1 line: *"reason rather than restate; read a passage for its idea; refine a response into a controlling idea. **Reasoning is taught before the thesis, because the reasoning link is the move students actually struggle with.**"*

**Method.** I read verbatim: the re-anchor plan, the L2/L3/L4 source dossiers, the curriculum resource base, the TWR verbatim mining report, the SRSD verbatim mining report, the Learning-Science/House-Docs mining report, and the **governing brainlift in full** (read from the readable `.md` twin of the DOCX at `c:/Users/noelp/Writing_Brainlift/HS Writing/HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II Source-Bound Argument (MVP).md`, which is the same text as the cited DOCX — the DOCX itself could not be opened because Bash/Python/pdftotext are environment-blocked this session; flagged for honesty). I tried hard to refute the claim.

---

## Verdict: **SUPPORTED-WITH-CAVEAT**

The claim is supportable, but **only under a specific reading that the plan does not currently make explicit**, and **only if "thesis" means a *refined controlling idea*, not a *working claim*.** Read literally — "teach the reasoning move before the student ever commits to a position" — the claim is **contradicted by every production-grade curriculum in our corpus** (SRSD TREE, TWR's ladder/SPO, and CER all put the claim/topic-sentence/position FIRST). The brainlift's own ordering claim survives, but it survives as a statement about **instructional emphasis and difficulty-sequencing**, not about the **logical order of the moves in a paragraph**. The re-anchor should keep the brainlift's spine but be reworded to remove the literal "no position until after reasoning" reading, which no source supports and which the brainlift itself quietly contradicts elsewhere.

The single most important finding: **the strongest evidence FOR the claim is one sentence in one internal document (the brainlift), and that same document elsewhere lists "Form a controlling idea" / "Topic sentence" FIRST in two separate tables.** The external curricula uniformly contradict the literal reading. So this is an internal design assertion dressed as a settled pedagogical fact.

## Evidence FOR "reasoning before thesis" (verbatim, cited)

1. **The governing brainlift states it outright.** `MVP brainlift`, Knowledge Tree → Unit skills:
   > "Unit 1, Reading-to-Write Foundations: reason rather than restate; read a passage for its idea; refine a response into a controlling idea. **Reasoning is taught before the thesis, because the reasoning link is the move students actually struggle with.**"
   (`HS Writing Brainlift — English I-II Source-Bound Argument (MVP).md`, line 195.) This is the explicit, verbatim warrant for the claim — and it lists the moves in the order *reason → read-for-idea → refine-controlling-idea*, with "reason rather than restate" first.

2. **The brainlift's load-bearing SPOV ranks reasoning ABOVE the thesis.** SPOV 4:
   > "'Explain how the evidence supports the claim' is the highest-leverage move in academic writing — **higher than the thesis**, higher than grammar." … "A thesis is usually present; grammar only gates the bottom. The reasoning link is where the points are actually won and lost, so it is the through-line the whole course is built to install."
   (`MVP brainlift`, lines 145–147.) The *difficulty/leverage* argument for fronting reasoning is real and well-evidenced (STAAR 3-vs-2 lever).

3. **The brainlift says developing the idea — not stating it — is the problem.** Through-line skills table:
   > "Form a controlling idea — State a clear, defensible position about a source's content — **The thesis is rarely the problem; developing it is.**"
   (`MVP brainlift`, line 184.) This supports the *spirit* of the re-anchor: spend instructional weight on the reasoning, not on thesis-stating. (Note: it also undercuts the literal ordering — see AGAINST #4.)

4. **A misconception-source agrees the claim is arrived at, not stated first.** ReadWriteThink "Developing Evidence-Based Arguments" (VERBATIM, read in both L3 and L4 dossiers):
   > "Evidence-based argument builds the case for its claim out of available evidence." … "the writer of the essay likely did not start there. Rather, he or she arrived at the claim as a result of careful reading."
   (`Unit1_L4_Source_Dossier.md`, lines 66–69; `Unit1_L2_Source_Dossier.md`, line 123.) This is the strongest *external* support: a real curriculum says the polished claim is a product of working through the evidence/reasoning, not the starting point. **But note its limit** (see reconciliation): RWT means the *final* claim is refined from evidence — it still has students form a *working* claim and gather evidence under it; it does not teach the reasoning-explanation move before any claim exists.

5. **Odell BEBA literally defers the student's own position.** BEBA Grade-9 unit plan (VERBATIM, full 49-page unit read per the L2 dossier):
   > students "are first expected to understand objectively a complex issue through exploratory inquiry and close reading of information on the topic, then study multiple perspectives on the issue **before they establish their own position.**"
   And Part 1's instruction: student claims should "**not** [focus] on articulating the student's own, still-developing position. Those sorts of claims will come later."
   (`Unit1_L2_Source_Dossier.md`, lines 20–23, 43.) BEBA is the unit our L2 spine "sits on," and it **does defer position** — surface support for "thesis comes later."

## Evidence AGAINST (verbatim, cited) — BEBA, TREE, CER all put the claim/position/topic-sentence FIRST

This is the larger and more credible body of evidence. Read literally, the claim is refuted.

### 1. SRSD TREE — the Topic sentence (≈ the claim/position) is the FIRST letter, before Reasons and Explain
The SRSD argument/opinion mnemonic the brainlift itself names (`MVP brainlift` line 250: "TREE for opinion writing (topic sentence, reasons, explain reasons, ending)") is, verbatim:
> "TREE / T TOPIC Sentence Tell what you believe! / R REASONS - 3 or More … / E ENDING … / E EXAMINE …"
(`SRSD_Mining.md`, line 29, Harris/Graham/Mason 2003.) And the brainlift's own gloss of TREE — **"topic sentence, reasons, explain reasons, ending"** (`MVP brainlift` line 250) — puts **Topic sentence FIRST and Explain THIRD.** The very strategy the course adopts teaches *claim before reasoning-explanation*. This is a direct internal contradiction: the brainlift says "reasoning before thesis" in the Knowledge Tree (line 195) and "topic sentence … reasons … explain reasons" in Named Strategies (line 250).

The SRSD mining report's own mapping confirms the order:
> "Topic sentence ↔ claim (clean match) … the 'implication'/'explanation' move in our spine is exactly the element the papers are *least* consistent about."
(`SRSD_Mining.md`, lines 212–215.) In SRSD, the claim is the stable first element; the explanation/reasoning is the *contested middle*, taught **after** the topic sentence.

### 2. The Writing Revolution — the SPO and every argument thesis put the Topic Sentence / claim FIRST
TWR's Single-Paragraph Outline, verbatim:
> "Solid lines tell students to write complete sentences for the **topic sentence (T.S.)** and the concluding sentence (C.S.). Dotted lines tell students to write notes for supporting details."
The SPO template is **T.S. (first) → details → C.S.** (`TWR_Mining.md`, lines 98–101.) TWR's argument scaffolding sequence also begins with the T.S.: "2. Distinguish a T.S. from details. 3. Identify T.S. and sequence details" (`TWR_Mining.md`, line 106). And TWR's argumentative thesis is built *first*, carrying the position, with the reason packed in via because: "the argument they're making **last**" inside the thesis (`TWR_Mining.md`, line 151). TWR's three-rung ladder (Opinion → Pro/Con → Argumentative) is about *taking and defending a side*; reasoning/evidence sits *under* an already-stated claim, never before it.

### 3. CER — the canonical order is **Claim → Evidence → Reasoning** (claim first)
The course's own resource base and L3 dossier confirm CER teaches Claim first:
> "K20 LEARN — 'Building Arguments With Evidence' (Claim–Evidence–Reasoning)" … Evaluate stage: "develop a five paragraph argumentative essay with **a thesis, claims, evidence, and reasoning.**"
(`Unit1_L3_Source_Dossier.md`, lines 17, 27.) The lesson "treats them as prior knowledge … 'Remind students that in the previous lesson, they learned the terms claim and evidence'" — i.e., claim/evidence are taught *before* reasoning is even reached (`Unit1_L3_Source_Dossier.md`, line 21). The Toulmin model the L3 dossier adopts defines the warrant as "the assumption that **links** the grounds to the claim" (`Unit1_L3_Source_Dossier.md`, lines 47–48) — by definition the warrant/reasoning is *downstream* of a claim that already exists. You cannot have a "this shows the claim" link before there is a claim.

### 4. The brainlift contradicts itself — its own through-line table and engine list the controlling idea/outline FIRST
- Through-line skills table order (`MVP brainlift` lines 183–186): **"Read for the idea" → "Form a controlling idea" → "Explain the evidence"** — i.e., *form the controlling idea BEFORE explain-the-evidence.* This is the **opposite** of line 195's "reasoning before the thesis."
- Engine table (`MVP brainlift` lines 235–238): Setup (read) → **Outline ("controlling idea, evidence slots, 'explain how' sub-prompts")** → Draft (explain how). The *controlling idea is named first in the outline*, with the "explain how" as a sub-prompt under it. Again claim-first.
- Named strategies (`MVP brainlift` line 250): TREE = "**topic sentence, reasons, explain reasons, ending**" — claim first, explain third.

So within the single governing document, the literal "reasoning before thesis" appears **once** (line 195), and **three** other structural passages put the controlling idea / topic sentence first.

### 5. Purdue (read verbatim in L4 dossier) tells students to commit the position EARLY
> "it is key to identify the controversy or debate you are addressing and to **define your position early on in the paper**."
(`Unit1_L2_Source_Dossier.md`, line 93, Purdue OWL.) Direct contradiction of "no position until reasoning is taught."

### 6. The BEBA "support" is weaker than it looks
BEBA defers the *student's own final position* — but it does **not** teach the reasoning-explanation move first. BEBA's Part 2 ("Analyzing Arguments") teaches the *elements together* — "issue, perspective, **position**, premise, evidence, reasoning, chain of reasoning" (`Unit1_L2_Source_Dossier.md`, line 44) — with **position named before evidence and reasoning** in the element list, and the position is then *synthesized from evaluating others' arguments*, not produced after a decontextualized reasoning drill. BEBA's deferral is "read and weigh the sides, then commit," which is **not** the same as "learn to explain evidence before you ever take a position." Our own L2 dossier flags this: BEBA "establishes the position *late and from evidence*" while "our self-paced L2 hands the debatable question on the card and goes straight to staking" (`Unit1_L2_Source_Dossier.md`, line 182) — so BEBA's sequence is *neither* our current build *nor* the re-anchor; it's a third thing.

## The reconciliation — working claim vs. refined thesis (does the nuance hold, or is it post-hoc?)

The plan's §4.4 hints at the reconciliation: *"claim→evidence→implication 'spine' … the brainlift's own word is 'reasoning'."* The honest reconciliation is the **working-claim-vs-refined-thesis distinction**, and the sources DO support it — but the plan does not currently state it, which is the gap.

**The distinction that makes the claim defensible:**
- A **working/provisional claim** is allowed (even required) early — you cannot write a "this evidence shows [X]" reasoning link without an [X] to point at. Every source assumes this: TREE's Topic sentence, CER's Claim, the SPO's T.S., RWT's "working thesis," UNC's "**working thesis** … likely to need adjustment along the way" (`Unit1_L4_Source_Dossier.md`, lines 120–124).
- The **refined, defensible, *specific* controlling idea** (the L4 "Write a Strong Thesis" artifact: arguable + specific, "___ should ___ because ___") is genuinely *refined after* the student has done reasoning work. RWT: "arrived at the claim as a result of careful reading"; UNC: "A thesis is the result of a lengthy thinking process." (`Unit1_L4_Source_Dossier.md`, lines 68, 122.)

**Under this reading the claim is true:** the *thesis-as-polished-controlling-idea* (L4 / the brainlift's "refine a response into a controlling idea") legitimately comes **after** the reasoning move — because refining is downstream of doing the reasoning. This is exactly what the brainlift's through-line note means: *"The thesis is rarely the problem; developing it is"* (line 184). "Developing it" = the reasoning work that refines a rough position into a sharp controlling idea.

**Is it post-hoc?** Partly. The brainlift's line 195 wording ("Reasoning is taught before the thesis") is stronger and more literal than the sources warrant, and reads as "no thesis until after reasoning." The defensible version is narrower: *a working claim is fine early; the **refined controlling idea** is taught after reasoning, and reasoning gets the instructional weight because it is the harder, higher-leverage move.* The reconciliation is real and source-backed, but the plan inherits the brainlift's overstated wording without stating the working-claim caveat — so as written, the re-anchor's L1→L2→L3 sequence (read-for-idea → reason-not-restate → controlling idea) risks teaching students to write a reasoning link before they have *any* claim to link to, which no source does and which is cognitively backwards (you cannot judge "does this evidence support the point?" with no point).

## What this means for the re-anchor (keep / modify the ordering)

**KEEP:**
- **The instructional-emphasis spine.** Fronting and weighting the reasoning/"explain how" move is strongly supported (brainlift SPOV 4; STAAR 3-vs-2 lever; UNC "NONE of your examples should be left unexplained"). Reasoning being *the hard, high-leverage move that gets the most instructional fire* is correct.
- **Refined thesis (L4-style "Write a Strong Thesis") AFTER reasoning.** The *polished, arguable+specific controlling idea* legitimately comes after the reasoning work — RWT and UNC both teach the thesis as a refined product (working-thesis → revise). The plan's "refine a response into a controlling idea = move 3, after reasoning" is sound for the *refined* thesis.
- **Deferring counterargument to Unit 3.** Confirmed by the brainlift (Unit 3) and by Toulmin treating warrant and rebuttal as separate elements (`Unit1_L3_Source_Dossier.md`, line 53).

**MODIFY:**
1. **Allow a *working claim* in L1/L2; do not teach reasoning with no claim present.** Every production curriculum (TREE, CER, SPO) starts from a claim/topic sentence. The reasoning move is "explain how this evidence supports **the point**" — it presupposes a point. The re-anchor should explicitly give students a *provisional* claim (the card already supplies the debatable question and a stake-able side) so the L2 reasoning move has something to attach to. Frame L2 as: *given a claim + evidence, write the reasoning* — which is exactly what the plan's L2 row says ("Given a claim + evidence from the source, write the reasoning"). So the plan's *mechanics* already assume a working claim; only its *headline wording* ("reasoning before the thesis") overstates it. **Reword the headline to: "reasoning is the unit's frontier move and gets taught/weighted first; a working claim is provided, and the refined controlling idea is sharpened after the reasoning exists."**
2. **Surface the working-claim-vs-refined-thesis distinction in the plan and in the lessons,** or you will contradict TREE (which you adopt) and confuse students. The brainlift itself models the conflict (line 195 vs. lines 184/236/250); resolve it in the build by saying explicitly: *working claim early (rough), refined controlling idea late (sharp).*
3. **Do not cite "real curricula teach reasoning before position" — they do not.** If the re-anchor needs external backing, cite the *difficulty/leverage* argument (reasoning is the 3-vs-2 lever, the move students struggle with) and the *refinement* argument (RWT/UNC: the polished thesis is arrived at, not stated first). Do **not** imply BEBA/TREE/CER sequence reasoning ahead of the claim — they sequence the claim/topic-sentence first.

**Bottom line for Noel:** The re-anchor's *structure* (read-for-idea → reasoning-as-frontier → refined controlling idea, full essay leaves Unit 1) is defensible and brainlift-aligned. The *justification as stated* ("reasoning is taught before the thesis") is an internal assertion that the governing brainlift itself contradicts in three other places and that no external curriculum supports under a literal reading. Keep the sequence; reword the rationale to the working-claim/refined-thesis + difficulty-leverage framing, which is what the sources actually support.
