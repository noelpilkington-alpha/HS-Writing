## CLAIM 1 — Remove the full essay from U1; keep a short-response end-cap

### Verdict: SUPPORTED (with caveat)

### Evidence FOR (verbatim, cited)

**The brainlift names "full essays in a foundations unit" as a literal failure mode.** MVP brainlift, Purpose section:
> "Without this brainlift, a curriculum designer or an LLM defaults to one of three failure modes: **assigning full essays and grading the product without teaching the component moves**; treating high school writing as 'longer middle school writing'…" — `MVP.md` line 21

**The brainlift's own U1 explicitly does NOT produce a full essay** — it installs reading-to-write moves, with reasoning before thesis:
> "Unit 1, Reading-to-Write Foundations: reason rather than restate; read a passage for its idea; refine a response into a controlling idea. **Reasoning is taught before the thesis**, because the reasoning link is the move students actually struggle with." — `MVP.md` line 195

**The full ARGUMENT essay is explicitly Unit 3; timed performance is Unit 8** (so it cannot also be U1):
> "Unit 3, Argumentative Response-to-Text … a defensible position, a line of reasoning, a counterargument … and accurate evidence integration." — `MVP.md` line 197
> "Unit 8, Timed Performance and the Gate: plan-to-essay pacing; self-diagnosis of the weakest rubric row under the clock…" — `MVP.md` line 205

**The component-before-product principle is corroborated independently by SRSD and TWR, at the *paragraph* grain:**
- The engine itself sequences paragraph-before-essay: *"Early reps use the paragraph-writing architecture; the core uses the essay-writing architecture."* — `MVP.md` line 231
- SRSD's headline strength is its strategy/component instruction (d = 1.14), and its mnemonics are taught at the paragraph/opinion grain (TREE) before essay grain (STOP+DARE): *"TREE (used in these papers mostly for opinion paragraphs / shorter persuasive pieces)"* vs *"STOP+DARE, which was built for full persuasive/argumentative essays."* — `SRSD_Mining.md` lines 219–220
- TWR teaches the Single-**Paragraph** Outline first; the multi-paragraph plan is a *later* chapter: *"TWR Ch. 6 is the Single-Paragraph Outline; the Multiple-Paragraph Outline (MPO) … is a later TWR chapter and was not in scope."* — `Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 33
- Comprehension/recognition precedes production: *"the learner must be able to recognise a solution … before he is himself able to produce the steps."* (Wood, Bruner & Ross) — `LearningSci_and_House_Docs_Mining.md` line 49

**The MOST decisive evidence (not in the brief): the current authoritative build doc ALREADY does this.** `C1_English_I_II_Lesson_Map.md` is the MVP brainlift's named companion (the brainlift points to "the C1 lesson map … in `HS Writing/ap-frq-mastery-v2/`", `MVP.md` line 345). Its U1 ends not in a full argument essay but in a **short expository response**:
> "1.5 | Essay | Light | **Bridge gate** — full short expository response-to-text, cold-ish: controlling idea + 2 explained-evidence paragraphs + close" — `C1_English_I_II_Lesson_Map.md` line 82

The full argument essay is at 3.5, and the cold full-performance gates are 4.6 / U8:
> "3.5 | Essay | Light | **Full argumentative ECR, cold**…" — `C1` line 101
> "4.6 | Essay | None | **ENGLISH I GATE** — cold passage-based ECR…" — `C1` line 112

So the Plan's "full essay leaves U1, reduced end-cap stays" is not a proposal — it is **reconciling our planning artifacts to a build doc the team already wrote and locked.**

### Evidence AGAINST (the adversarial case — and why it fails)

**A1. "Removing the full essay strands students with no integrative production."** This is the brief's sharpest worry, and it has a real source basis: the L5 dossier shows that *no Unit-1 lesson owns essay structure*:
> "G1 | Essay STRUCTURE / paragraph TRANSITIONS are owned by no Unit-1 lesson … the student is asked to assemble a multi-paragraph essay having only ever practiced single claim→evidence→implication units (L3) and a one-sentence thesis (L4)." — `Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 186 (rated **High** importance)

*Why it fails as a refutation:* this is actually an argument **FOR** the re-anchor, not against it. G1 is the "falls between lessons before the gateway" gap that exists *precisely because* the old build jumped to a full essay (L5) without an owner for connective tissue. The re-anchored U1 keeps a *short* expository end-cap (1.5 = controlling idea + 2 explained-evidence paragraphs + close) whose structural demand is small enough that the C1 map can put the structure-the-response move on its own footing in U2 (2.1 "Structure the response", `C1` line 88). The short end-cap is **enough integrative production** for a foundations unit: SRSD's own gate logic supports a low-stakes, single-sitting "on-demand" form (the L5 dossier's nearest real cousin: *"the closest real cousin is an on-demand / timed prompt … which is single-sitting"* — `Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 209). The integrative *argument* essay isn't stranded; it's relocated to U3 where its component moves (position, line of reasoning, counterargument) are actually taught first.

**A2. "Some sourced lessons DO build to a full essay early."** True, and worth stating: K20 and CommonLit run a whole-essay gateway, and the old A1 L05 was modeled on them.
> "the documented routine is outline first → transfer into the essay" (K20) — `Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 21
> "analyze a mentor text → write a first draft → revision lessons … → revise the unit essay" (CommonLit) — `Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 105

*Why it fails:* every one of those gateways is **multi-day, teacher-paced, and revision-heavy**, the opposite of a self-paced no-teacher MVP foundations unit:
> "Every real gateway we reached (CommonLit, K20, TWR) is multi-day with a revision round; ours is one self-paced pass… No source models a one-round, revision-less gateway." — `Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 209

And the timed-draft + stall-recovery machinery the old L5 bolted on is *the single least-grounded thing in the whole dossier*:
> "This is genuinely the weakest-grounded of the three new moves, and every source confirms it… ATC explicitly states it gives 'no specific guidance about writing under time constraints.'" — `Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 72

So the early-full-essay precedent that exists in real curricula is exactly the teacher-led, multi-session form we *can't* deliver — which is an argument for deferring the full essay, not keeping it in a one-shot self-paced U1.

### Caveat (the one real correction)

The Plan repeatedly says U1 keeps a *"short-response end-cap"* and frames it ambiguously (the Plan's §3 puts the *"essay routine (STOP+DARE / POW)"* "at the unit's writing/short-response end," `Unit1_Reanchor_Plan.md` line 57). But the locked build doc is specific: the U1 end-cap is a **short EXPOSITORY response-to-text** (`C1` line 82), because **expository/analytical-response is the PRIMARY STAAR mode and argumentative has never appeared on a released EI/EII ECR**:
> "the actual STAAR ECR is informational / analytical-response (the primary target …); argumentative (10.C) has not appeared on a released EI/EII ECR." — `C1` lines 65–69; corroborated in `ap-frq-mastery-v2-redesign.md` line 79 ("ALWAYS tagged 10.B informational or 5.B … NEVER 10.C argumentative").

This matters: the Re-Anchor Plan is still written in *argument* vocabulary (argue-vs-persuade, defensible position), inherited from the from-knowledge bridge. If the team adopts the re-anchor, the end-cap should be the **expository** response the C1 map specifies, not an argument short-response. **Recommend the Plan name the end-cap as expository to match C1.**

## CLAIM 2 — Our current U1 = brainlift's U1 + U3 + U8 compressed, in the wrong order

### Verdict: SUPPORTED (the diagnosis is fair)

### Evidence FOR (verbatim, cited)

The mapping the Plan asserts holds up against the old `A1_Lesson_Map.md`:

| Old A1 U1 lesson | Brainlift home | Source |
|---|---|---|
| L01 Expository→Argumentative | (conceptual, not a brainlift unit skill) | `A1_Lesson_Map.md` line 32; brainlift U1 is reading-to-write `MVP.md` 195 |
| L02 Taking a Position | thesis — but brainlift teaches **reasoning before thesis** | `A1_Lesson_Map.md` 45; `MVP.md` 195 |
| L03 Evidence + Reasoning | **brainlift U1 move #1** ("reason rather than restate") | `A1_Lesson_Map.md` 58; `MVP.md` 195 |
| L04 Counterarguments | **brainlift Unit 3** | `A1_Lesson_Map.md` 71; `MVP.md` 197 |
| L05 Full Argument Essay | **brainlift Unit 3 (product) + Unit 8 (timed)** | `A1_Lesson_Map.md` 83; `MVP.md` 197, 205 |

The "reversed order" charge is precise and verbatim-backed: brainlift says *"Reasoning is taught before the thesis"* (`MVP.md` line 195), but the old map puts L02 "Taking a Position" before L03 "Evidence and Reasoning." The corpus-mining doc independently flagged the same reversal as a `⚠ CONTRADICTION`:
> "⚠ Sequencing divergence: the brainlift says teach reasoning before the thesis; our L02 'Taking a Position' precedes L03 'Evidence and Reasoning.'" — `LearningSci_and_House_Docs_Mining.md` line 177

And the old L05 carrying counterargument-adjacent full-essay scope is confirmed; the L5 dossier itself notes counterargument was explicitly excluded from L5 as "a later EI unit" (`Unit1_L5_Source_Dossier.md` line 3), which matches the brainlift sending counterargument to U3.

### Evidence AGAINST (the adversarial case — and why it fails)

**B1. "The 5-lesson build is defensible on its own sourced grounds — it was a deliberate G8→G9 bridge, not a drift."** This is the strongest defense and it has a real basis. The old A1 map's rationale is explicit and sourced:
> "G8 completers know … basic academic argument … They have NEVER analyzed an assigned text… The old v1 lesson map jumped straight to close reading… Students need to solidify argumentative writing skills FIRST — using topics they can argue from knowledge — before pivoting to text-based analytical writing." — `A1_Lesson_Map.md` lines 13–15

So the 5-lesson U1 wasn't random compression; it was a designed argument-from-knowledge on-ramp. *That is a fair point and the Plan should not pretend the old build was thoughtless.*

*Why it fails as a refutation:* the from-knowledge premise the entire defense rests on has been **formally abandoned by the team**, on the explicit authority of the governing brainlift:
> "Decided 2026-06-18: HS Writing Unit 1 (A1) will move toward SOURCE-BINDING… **This overrides the A1 v2 rationale** (recorded as 'argument foundations FIRST, from own knowledge, no assigned texts')… We are choosing the brainlift over the bridge design for Unit 1, **not reconciling them.**" — `source-binding-decision.md` lines 10, 14

And the from-knowledge stimulus was already a head-on contradiction of the brainlift's central bet:
> "⚠ The house style is SOURCE-BOUND… Our Unit 1 is explicitly 'No passages / write from own knowledge.' This is the single biggest divergence." — `LearningSci_and_House_Docs_Mining.md` line 163
> "This is a head-on contradiction of the MVP's central design decision." — `LearningSci_and_House_Docs_Mining.md` line 174

Once source-binding is adopted, an *argument-from-knowledge* bridge unit has no stimulus to bridge from, so its internal order (argue/persuade → position → evidence) loses its rationale and the brainlift's reading-to-write order takes over. The "wrong order, compressed" diagnosis is therefore fair **given the decision the team has already made.** It would be unfair only in a world where the from-knowledge bridge survived — and that world was closed on 2026-06-18.

## CLAIM 3 — The MVP brainlift should OVERRIDE the prior A1 lesson-map structure for U1

### Verdict: SUPPORTED — and the doc-authority check actually strengthens it

### Evidence FOR (verbatim, cited)

**The brainlift states its own authority explicitly** (the exact line the brief asked for):
> "For this scope, it supersedes the broader `HS Writing Brainlift - Complete.md`, which remains the long-range ceiling… **Where the two documents conflict, this one governs the MVP.**" — `MVP.md` line 3

**The named failure mode about full essays** (also requested verbatim):
> "a curriculum designer or an LLM defaults to one of three failure modes: **assigning full essays and grading the product without teaching the component moves**…" — `MVP.md` line 21

**The two skill types the Plan relies on are stated verbatim:**
> "The course teaches two kinds of skill: **through-line skills, practiced in every lesson and grown across the course; and unit skills, the one new move each unit installs.**" — `MVP.md` line 177

**The discrimination-before-production SPOV the Plan's L1 leans on:**
> "Discrimination is the gateway to production. You cannot write what you cannot recognize." — `MVP.md` line 157 (SPOV 7); the ~40% figure is the older docs' (`LearningSci…` line 5), the MVP states the principle but not the percentage.

### Doc-authority check: does the MVP brainlift genuinely govern, or is there unresolved tension?

This is the part the brief flagged CRITICAL, and where I dug hardest to find a contradicting higher authority. Findings:

**1. The MVP genuinely governs over the "Complete" brainlift — and the two DO conflict, exactly as the MVP anticipated.** The Complete doc's lead SPOV is the *opposite* of the MVP's:
> Complete: "**SPOV Truth 1: Advanced Writing is About Choices, Not Formulas** … the fundamental shift is from 'follow the template' to 'choose the template that serves your purpose.'" — `HS Writing Brainlift - Complete.md` lines 309–311
> MVP: "**Templates and formulas reduce cognitive load. Choosing among them is a ceiling skill, not the floor.** … For the novice academic writer this gets the order backwards." — `MVP.md` lines 133–135

The MVP names this reversal and resolves it by fiat of its own scope note. The corpus-mining doc already logged it: *"Ceiling-vs-MVP framing tension … the MVP explicitly reverses this for novices… For our G9–G10 build, the MVP position governs."* — `LearningSci…` line 193. **So the "brainlift governs" claim is not rubber-stamping — it is the correct resolution of a real, documented conflict, and the MVP is the doc that earns the tie-break by its own explicit terms.**

**2. The Complete doc does NOT actually support an early full essay in a *foundations* sense — and cannot outrank the MVP on U1.** Adversarially, the Complete doc *does* put "Full essay production" at G9 (node 1.15 Essay Architecture, `Complete.md` lines 449, 472). If that doc governed, it would contradict Claim 1. **But it explicitly assumes the full-essay foundation was already built in grades 6–8:** node 1.15's prerequisite is *"MPO Mastery (1.10)"* (`Complete.md` line 446), and its bridge summary requires entrants to already *"write organized multi-paragraph essays (MPO mastery)… Construct basic 'They Say / I Say' arguments (1.12) with counterarguments (1.13)"* (`Complete.md` line 416). So even the ceiling doc does not teach the full essay *cold in a foundations unit* — it assumes it as an entry requirement. There is no internal doc anywhere in the set that backs building the full argument essay from scratch inside a G9 reading-to-write foundations unit.

**3. The older `HS Writing Brainlift.docx` / `.html` is the SAME (older) Complete-style design, not a competing authority.** It contains zero MVP language (0 hits for "reason rather than restate / controlling idea / Source-Bound / supersedes") and 14 hits for the Complete-doc markers ("Essay Architecture / Choices, Not Formulas / MPO Mastery / Composition…Mechanics…Discourse"). It is the May-22 ceiling design; the MVP is the June-12 re-aim. No newer-than-MVP authority hides there.

**4. The A1 lesson map does NOT outrank the brainlift — and a NEWER build doc already sides with the brainlift.** The brief asked me to flag if the A1 map is "actually newer/authoritative." It is not. The A1 map self-describes as aligned to the *blueprint v4* and the from-knowledge rationale (`A1_Lesson_Map.md` lines 13–15, 410), which `source-binding-decision.md` (2026-06-18) explicitly *overrides*. More importantly, the MVP brainlift's own named companion build doc — **`C1_English_I_II_Lesson_Map.md` (2026-06-11)** — already encodes the brainlift's U1 verbatim (1.1 "Reason, not restate" first; 1.4 "Controlling idea … thesis-as-refinement"; 1.5 short expository end-cap). So the authority chain is: **MVP brainlift (why) → C1 lesson map (what, newer) → old A1 map (superseded planning artifact).** The Re-Anchor Plan is bringing the *stale A1 planning artifact* into line with documents that already won.

**The one genuinely unresolved tension** (stated honestly, since the brief demanded skepticism): the Re-Anchor Plan, the old A1 map, and even parts of the corpus mining are still written in **argumentative** vocabulary (argue-vs-persuade, "defensible position," "Strong Thesis"), while the governing C1 build doc makes U1's mode **expository/analytical-response** because that is the *only* mode on released STAAR ECRs (`C1` lines 65–69; `ap-frq-mastery-v2-redesign.md` line 79). The Plan re-sequences within an argument frame (its §3 L2 "reason, not restate" is fine, but its L1 "argue vs. persuade primer" and the whole "argument essay" framing are expository-mismatched). **This is not a reason to reject the override — it is a reason the re-anchor must go one step further than the Plan does: re-anchor U1's MODE to expository response-to-text, not just its move-order.** The Plan half-makes this move (it cites "reasoning"/"explain how the evidence supports the claim") but leaves argument-era labels in place.

### Evidence AGAINST (and why it fails)

**C1.** "If our own docs disagree, the 'brainlift governs' claim needs scrutiny." They do disagree (Complete vs MVP; A1 from-knowledge vs MVP source-bound) — but every disagreement is one the MVP scope note pre-empts and resolves, and the disagreements all run the *same direction* (older/ceiling design vs newer/MVP). There is no doc that is both newer than the MVP *and* contradicts its U1. The scrutiny the brief asked for, performed, *confirms* the override rather than undermining it.

## What this means for the re-anchor

1. **Proceed with all three claims.** None could be refuted on the sourced record. The full argument essay leaves U1; a short end-cap stays; the move-order flips to reasoning-before-thesis; the brainlift governs.

2. **Fix the one real error: the end-cap is EXPOSITORY, not "short-response (argument)."** Align the Plan's §3/§5 to `C1` 1.5 — "full short expository response-to-text: controlling idea + 2 explained-evidence paragraphs + close." This also means the Plan's "argue vs. persuade" L1 primer and its "argument essay" framing are mode-mismatched and should be re-pointed to *reason-vs-restate / read-for-idea* on a **source passage**, per source-binding-decision.md.

3. **The Plan under-claims its own support.** It frames the re-anchor as a fresh proposal against the old 5-lesson build. In fact a newer, locked companion build doc (`C1_English_I_II_Lesson_Map.md`) already implements exactly this U1 (5 lessons: 1.1 reason-not-restate → 1.2 read-for-idea → 1.3 cite+explain → 1.4 controlling idea → 1.5 short expository bridge gate). The re-anchor is *reconciliation to an existing decision*, which is a stronger footing than "new proposal." The Plan should cite C1 directly.

4. **Watch the carried gaps, not the order.** The L5 dossier's High-importance gap (G1: essay structure/transitions owned by no lesson) is *solved* by the re-anchor only if U2's "Structure the response" (C1 2.1) genuinely owns connective tissue. Confirm that 2.1 is built before any unit asks for a multi-paragraph product, or G1 simply migrates from old-L5 to new-1.5.

5. **Open question to put to Noel (the Plan's own (c), now sharper):** keep U1 at the C1-locked **5 lessons** (reason / read-for-idea / cite+explain / controlling idea / short expository gate) rather than the Plan's **3-move** compression — the 3-move version drops the comprehension pre-read (1.2) and the cite+explain rep (1.3), both of which are load-bearing source-binding machinery the brainlift requires (SPOV 5 separately-scored pre-read, `MVP.md` line 151). The Plan's 3-move sketch may be *too* lean relative to the doc it claims to anchor to.
