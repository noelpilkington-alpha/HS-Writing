# C1 — High School Writing: English I + II Lesson Map (MVP)

The locked skill sequence for the MVP. ~40 lessons across **English I (G9)** and
**English II (G10)**, each lesson one piece of writing through the AlphaWrite cycle
(Setup → Outline → Draft → Revise → Polish → Submit). Re-aimed from
rhetorical-analysis to **STAAR content-response**, with **passage-based reading
integrated** and an **expository response-to-text** thread. Feeds C2 (standards
crosswalk), C3 (lesson content), C4 (passage bank), C5 (assessment specs).

**Status:** v1 for review. Sequence + gates locked pending review; per-lesson
standards are tagged at strand level (C2 finalizes exact TEKS SEs + CCSS codes).

---

## Design rationale

- **Entry point:** a typical on-grade G8 writer exiting AlphaWrite Read — can write
  expository paragraphs/essays and basic argument *from knowledge*, but has **not**
  written *in response to a source passage*, synthesized multiple sources, or
  analyzed multiple perspectives. The course bridges from what they have to what
  STAAR English I/II + ACT demand.
- **The re-aim (critical):** the planned A1/A2 sequence framed passage work as
  *rhetorical analysis of the author's craft*. STAAR wants a **controlling idea
  about the passage's content/ideas**, supported with **explained textual
  evidence**. Every passage-based lesson here reads *for what the text says/argues*,
  not *what the author is doing*. (Craft analysis returns only downstream, for AP.)
- **Reading is integrated, not assumed.** Every passage-based rep opens with a
  comprehension pre-read scored separately from the writing, so a misread routes to
  re-read support — not mis-attributed to a writing failure (Graham, *Writing to
  Read*; STAAR/ACT are reading-dependent).
- **Reasoning before thesis** (validated by both the data and the AP-experience
  read): students build claim → evidence → **explained reasoning** first; the
  thesis/controlling idea is taught as a *refinement* of what they've assembled.
- **Acquisition order:** expository response-to-text → argumentative response →
  synthesis across sources → multi-perspective (ACT) → timed performance. Each new
  essay type adds one layer onto an automatic spine; scaffold fades within each
  thread (Heavy → Medium → Light → None at the cold gate).
- **The fixed wrapper, built once:** the AI-Tutor DI sequence (Teach →
  Model-with-coping-false-start → Guided → Independent → 15–30s discrimination
  beat), the meaning-before-surface Revise gating, and the rescore-the-weakest-part
  loop at Submit. Only the prompt, stimulus, and scaffold level change rep to rep.

## Legend

- **Arch:** Para = paragraph-writing architecture (shorter reps); Essay = full
  6-stage essay cycle.
- **Rep · Scaffold:** Heavy (fields pre-filled + worked example) → Medium (frames +
  checklist) → Light (checklist only) → None (cold gate).
- **★ Frontier:** the stage carrying the heaviest instruction (the year's hard move).
- **Gate row:** the rubric row(s) the lesson's Revise checklist / Submit scores
  against (STAAR ECR = Trait 1 Org & Development 0–3, Trait 2 Conventions 0–2;
  reading SCR 0–2; writing SCR 0–1; ACT 4-domain 2–12).
- **Generate:** the C3/C4/C5 content the lesson requires.
- **Essay types** (from Build_Scope): **E1** Expository response-to-text · **E2**
  Argumentative response-to-text · **E3** Synthesis essay · **E4** Multi-perspective
  (ACT-style) · **SCR** short constructed responses.

# ENGLISH I (G9) — Passage-Based Composition Floor

*Builds the floor STAAR English I rewards: read a passage, form a controlling idea
about its content, support with explained textual evidence; control sentence
boundaries. Course frontier = **Draft** (the explain-the-evidence move). ~20 lessons.*
*Mode weighting (from 6 released EI/EII forms): the actual STAAR ECR is
**informational / analytical-response (the primary target — TEKS 10.B / 5.B)**;
**argumentative (10.C) has not appeared on a released EI/EII ECR** but is eligible
and ACT-aligned, so Unit 3 keeps it as the **secondary** mode (primary for ACT
readiness, and the eligible-mode hedge).*
*Standards (strand level; C2 finalizes): TEKS EI §110.36(c) — (9) Composition
process, (10)(B) informational + (10)(C) argumentative, (8) author's purpose/craft
[light], (11) inquiry/evidence; CCSS W.9-10.1, .2, .4, .5, .9; RI.9-10.1; L.9-10.1–3.*

# ENGLISH II (G10) — Synthesis, Depth & ACT Readiness

*Adds the orchestration STAAR English II + ACT reward: synthesize across
paired/multiple sources, argue with nuance, analyze multiple perspectives, and
perform under time. Course frontier = **Outline** (the synthesis matrix /
perspective map). Builds on an automatic English I spine. ~20 lessons.*
*Standards (strand level; C2 finalizes): TEKS EII §110.37(c) — (9) Composition,
(10)(C) argumentative, (11)(F) synthesize sources + (11)(G) evaluate
credibility/bias, **(8)(F) diction/syntax → tone** (Polish beat, see below); CCSS
W.9-10.1, .4, .5, .7, .8, .9; RI.9-10.1; L.9-10.1–3. ACT-readiness lessons map to
the ACT Writing 4-domain rubric (bridge to G11).*

> **English II Polish — diction/tone-for-effect beat (decided 2026-06-11, covers
> TEKS EII.8(F)).** The writing course owns author's-craft-for-effect (not the
> Language course). Add to the **Polish** stage of the EII essay reps (esp. 6.x and
> 7.x) a short beat: *choose diction and syntax deliberately for mood/voice/tone in
> service of the argument* — taught as a craft refinement at Polish, after meaning
> is settled. Not a standalone lesson; a recurring Polish-checklist beat with one
> Teach/Model touch in Unit 6. (8)(A) purpose/audience stays a light Setup touch.

## Gates summary

| Gate | Task | Pass bar | Aligns to |
|------|------|----------|-----------|
| **Bridge (1.5)** | short expository response-to-text, cold-ish | Trait 1 rows earned (Thesis + Evidence + Reasoning) | floor check before E1 |
| **English I (4.6)** | cold passage-based ECR (**informational/analytical-response** primary; argumentative eligible) + writing SCR + reading SCR | ECR Trait 1 ≥2 & Trait 2 ≥1; both SCRs earned | **STAAR English I** |
| **English II (8.5)** | cold synthesis ECR (passage-based) + cold ACT-style essay + conventions | synthesis ECR Trait 1 ≥2 & Trait 2 ≥1; ACT essay clears target on ≥3 of 4 domains | **STAAR English II** + **ACT readiness** |

All gates are **single-attempt on reserved passages/prompts** (held out of
instruction). The rescore-the-weakest-part loop runs only on practice reps, never
on a gate — so transfer, not local patching, unlocks completion. Calibration
(self-score vs grader) is surfaced and coached but does **not** gate advancement.

## Audit (against the design rules)

- **One new move per lesson:** yes; consolidation/gate lessons (1.5, 2.4, 3.5, 4.6,
  5.6, 6.4, 7.5, 8.3–8.5) introduce no new move by design.
- **Scaffold fades within each essay-type thread:** Heavy → Medium → Light → None.
- **Reading integrated:** comprehension pre-read scored separately at 1.2, 5.1, and
  every passage-based rep; misread routes to re-read, not to a writing fix.
- **Reasoning before thesis:** 1.1 (reasoning) precedes 1.4 (controlling idea).
- **Re-aim honored:** all passage work reads for **content/ideas**, not author's
  craft; no rhetorical-analysis "what is the author doing" framing.
- **Timing back-loaded:** untimed through U7.3; timing enters U7.4 and is the U8
  variable — the spine is automatic before the clock is added.
- **Every gate maps to a real rubric row:** STAAR ECR 2-trait + SCRs (EI/EII); ACT
  4-domain (readiness).
- **Each lesson has a Generate row** scoping C3/C4/C5.
- **STAAR/CCSS dual-tagged at strand level;** C2 finalizes per-lesson SEs/codes.

## Handoff

- **C2** — produce the per-lesson TEKS English I/II SE + CCSS 9–10 crosswalk from
  the strand tags above; flag any STAAR SE not yet covered by a lesson.
- **C3** — author the per-lesson content named in each Generate cell (Teach beats,
  coping models, gated discrimination items, Outline field prompts, checklists,
  prompts), built English I first.
- **C4** — build the grade-9/10 passage bank: single passages (EI), **paired/
  multi-source sets** (EII synthesis), short SCR passages, ACT-style 3-perspective
  prompts, and **reserved** gate stimuli — all reading-level-appropriate for a
  typical on-grade student (not AP-level).
- **C5** — finalize the assessment specs the dev grader/tests are built from: STAAR
  ECR 2-trait, writing SCR 0–1, reading SCR 0–2, ACT 4-domain — each with anchor
  papers; calibrate the grader against anchors before any gated lesson ships.

## Open dependencies (not curriculum-team decisions)

- **Paired-passage stimulus surface (D1):** E3 + reading SCR need a multi-source
  panel at Setup — confirm the production engine supports it before C3 authoring.
- **Where the gate lives (D3):** in-app vs Alpha test (EI/EII gates).
- **STAAR vs ACT framing of the EII gate:** the synthesis half is STAAR-aligned
  (passage-based); the ACT half is readiness (constructed support) — confirm both
  belong in one gate or split into two.
