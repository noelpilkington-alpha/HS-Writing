## Unit-level design (applies to all 5 lessons)

**The carried mnemonic — FACT, introduced as recall-or-learn refresh** (Amendment 1; robust whether or not SRSD ships in G6-8):
- **F** — Focus: the controlling idea (what the passage explains / your one-sentence answer)
- **A** — Add details: the key facts/evidence from the passage
- **C** — Clarify: explain how each detail supports the focus *(THE frontier move — the C5 evidence-explanation 3→2→1 lever)*
- **T** — Tie up: a conclusion that connects back to the focus

**The 6-step activity flow** (SRSD Activity Specs, instantiated for G9-10): (1) prompt-type discrimination → (2) passage read + **separately-scored** comprehension pre-read (C5/SPOV 5) → (3) FACT-field planning → (4) graded draft (plan visible) → (5) FACT self-check → (6) feedback/revise. Not every lesson runs all six; each lesson owns one frontier move and uses the slice that serves it.

**Scaffold-fade across the unit** (Amendment 1 / Activity Specs 4 phases): 1.1–1.3 Heavy (fields + descriptions + coping model) → 1.4 Medium (labels) → 1.5 Light→None at the gate (no FACT fields; passage + prompt + blank draft). FACT cue card is visible early, **internal by the bridge gate**.

**Grading hooks (C5):** every graded draft → S-INF rubric (Trait 1 Org&Dev 0-3 + Trait 2 Conventions 0-2, ×2 → /10 on the real test; hard-coupling: Trait1=0 forces Trait2=0). The 4 negative exemplars (drops-evidence, missing-conclusion, text-refs-as-transitions, irrelevant-padding) are the discrimination targets. Feedback names the weakest trait + one move-specific next step (Yeager voice).

**Cross-unit retrieval (Amendment 2):** flagged for later units (a U1 expository rep re-surfaces in U3/U5); within U1, 1.5 is the cumulative rep.

## Lesson 1.1 — Reason, not restate · ★ frontier · Heavy

**The one new move:** write the sentence that explains *how* evidence supports a point (FACT's **C**larify), distinguished from restating/summarizing. This is the unit's highest-leverage move (the C5 3-vs-2 lever) and is taught FIRST (Amendment 3: a **working claim** is supplied so the reasoning has something to attach to — reasoning is taught before the *refined* thesis, not before any claim).

**Step flow used:** discrimination (gated) → comprehension micro-check → the C move on a given claim+evidence → self-check. (Planning/full-draft deferred to 1.5.)

**DI block — "Clarify: explain how the evidence supports the point"**
- *Rule:* "Evidence shows *what* the text says. To **Clarify** (the C in FACT) is to say *how* that evidence supports the point — what it means for the focus. Restating just repeats the evidence; clarifying adds why it proves the point." `[real-lesson: warrant defined in Purdue OWL Toulmin Argument ("the assumption that links the grounds to the claim"); UNC Writing Center "Paragraphs" handout ("none of your examples should be left unexplained") — NOT the UNC "Argument" handout.]`
- *Coping model (false-start → repair, per Activity Specs / Rubric B D6):* "Watch me. Working claim: *the passage shows sleep loss hurts teen learning.* I drop the evidence: *‘Teens get less sleep than they need.’* …that just restates the topic — a reader thinks *so what?* I repair it by adding the Clarify: *‘…which means the school day starts before their brains are alert, so the lost sleep directly costs learning time.’* Now it explains how the fact supports the point." `[B: D6 coping ✓]`
- *Example/non-example pair (one dimension varies — restate vs clarify, same evidence):*
  - ✗ restate: "The text says teens are tired in the morning." (repeats the fact)
  - ✓ clarify: "The text says teens are tired in the morning, which shows the early start fights their biology — so the schedule itself, not the student, causes the lost focus."
- *Sameness set (Rubric B D2 — three maximally-different positives under one label, so students don't over-stipulate "clarify = the word *because*"). All three are the SAME move (explain how the fact supports the focus), worded differently and on different facts:*
  - ✓ (linking phrase "which shows"): "Teens average fewer than 8 hours of sleep on school nights, **which shows** that the early schedule is asking them to learn before their bodies are ready."
  - ✓ (no connective — clarify by stating the consequence): "The typical school starts at 8:03 a.m. **For a teenager whose brain isn't alert until later, that means the hardest thinking of the day happens at its worst hour.**"
  - ✓ (clarify framed as significance): "Pediatricians recommend no start before 8:30. **The gap between that advice and the real 8:03 average is the whole problem the passage is built around** — the schedule, not the student, drives the lost focus."
  - *(Counter-anchor for contrast — NOT the move:* "Teens are tired" → restates; no clarify.) `[B: D2 sameness — instantiated, 3 distinct realizations + counter-anchor]`
- *Test (on a NEW point from the passage, not the modeled one — D4 copy-resistance):* "Now you: here's a different fact from the passage. Add the Clarify — explain how it supports the focus."

**Discrimination item (gated, before production — Rubric B D3 / C5 negative exemplar #1):** present 3 sentences responding to the passage; student sorts **restate vs. clarify**. Negative anchors drawn from C5 ("instead of explaining the evidence"). Must pass the sort to reach production.

**Grading:** the C move scored against the C5 evidence-explanation lever (clearly-explained=3 / insufficiently-explained=2 / vague=1). Fail-soft coach: "That restates the fact — add *how* it supports your point (the Clarify move)."

`[Rubric B grade (post-adversarial-fix): D1✓ (Toulmin + UNC "Paragraphs" — source relabeled) · D2✓ (pair + 3 instantiated sameness examples + counter-anchor) · D3✓ (the 3-vs-2 lever itself) · D4✓ (test on new point) · D5✓ (FACT-C) · D6✓ (coping) · D7 pending coach-string voice pass]`

`[PASSAGE: short excerpt, ~120-150w, from C4_U1 topic #1 — "just enough content for one point + evidence" per C4 1.1 spec]`

## Lesson 1.2 — Read for the idea · Setup · Heavy

**The one new move:** comprehension pre-read — identify the passage's controlling idea (what it's saying/explaining), **scored separately from writing** (C5/SPOV 5) so a misread routes to re-read support, not a mis-attributed writing failure. This is FACT's **F** (Focus) sourced *from* the text. Also introduces **genre discrimination** (Activity Specs: "is this prompt asking you to explain (FACT) or argue?") — here, recognizing an informational/explain prompt.

**DI block — "Find the focus before you write"**
- *Rule:* "Before you can explain a text, you have to know what it's saying. The **Focus** is the passage's controlling idea in one sentence — what it mainly explains." `[real-lesson: STAAR Hot Text / close-reading TDQ ladder — read for the idea is a proven item type; curriculum_resource_base LT2/LT3]`
- *Model:* think-aloud locating the controlling idea vs. a supporting detail.
- *Pair:* controlling idea ✓ vs. a true-but-narrow detail ✗ (the dimension: scope — whole-passage vs. one fact).
- *Test:* student states the focus in one sentence + answers 2 text-dependent questions.

**Comprehension pre-read (separately scored — the HS delta vs Activity Specs' MC check):** "In one sentence, what does this passage mainly explain? Then: what evidence does it give?" Scored on a comprehension key (C4 ships the key). A miss here → re-read support, *not* a writing penalty.

**Grading:** comprehension key match (separate score); does NOT enter the writing rubric. `[B: D1✓ D3✓ (Focus = rubric's controlling-idea row) D5✓]`

## Lesson 1.3 — Cite + explain · ★ Draft · Heavy→Medium

**The one new move:** pull a specific piece of text evidence (**A**dd details) AND explain it (**C**larify) — the cite-then-explain pair, the paragraph-level engine of the whole course. Combines 1.1's C move with explicit citation. (This is where R.A.P.S.'s "answer→cite→explain" is *actually* covered — per the SRSD spec's own note that FACT+the detail procedure subsume it.)

**DI block — "Cite, then explain — never a dropped quote"**
- *Rule:* "Add a detail = name a specific fact from the passage. Clarify = explain how it supports your focus. A detail without a clarify is a *dropped quote* — the most common reason a response scores a 2 instead of a 3." `[real-lesson: C5 negative exemplar #1 "drops quotes without explanation"; the cite-then-explain frame]`
- *Coping model:* dropped quote → add the "this shows…" link (Activity Specs §139 / Rubric B D6).
- *Pair:* dropped quote ✗ ("The passage says 1 out of 3 bites of food depends on pollinators.") vs. cited-and-clarified ✓ ("The passage says 1 out of 3 bites of food depends on pollinators, which shows how deeply our everyday meals rely on a process most people never see.").
- *Sameness set (three cite+explain moves — same move, different facts + different linking, so students don't bind it to one phrase):*
  - ✓ "The text reports that more than 75% of flowering plants are pollinated by animals — **a figure that tells us pollinators matter far beyond the food aisle, all the way to wild ecosystems.**"
  - ✓ "Honey-bee pollination added over $19 billion to crops in 2010. **That dollar figure is why the passage frames pollinator health as a farming issue, not just a nature one.**"
  - ✓ "Pollinators support more than 1,200 food crops. **Put next to the single bee on a single flower, that number is the point: the effect is enormous only because it is repeated millions of times.**"
- *Test (new detail from the passage):* cite one detail the models did NOT use + clarify it.

**Discrimination item (gated):** sort "dropped quote vs. explained evidence" (C5 negative exemplar #1).

**Scaffold-fade note:** 1.3 begins Heavy (cite field + clarify field both prompted) → Medium by end (one combined field). `[B: D1✓ D2✓ D3✓ (the 3-vs-2 lever) D4✓ D5✓ D6✓]`

## Lesson 1.4 — Controlling idea from a passage (thesis-as-refinement) · Outline · Medium

**The one new move:** refine a response into a clear controlling idea about the passage's content — **thesis as refinement** of the reasoning already built (Amendment 3: this comes AFTER 1.1's reasoning, exactly per "developing the thesis is the problem, not stating it"). FACT's **F** again, now as a *crafted* controlling idea rather than a located one.

**DI block — "Refine your focus into a controlling idea"**
- *Rule:* "A controlling idea is a Focus you've sharpened: it says what the passage explains AND the specific point you'll develop about it — not a vague topic label." `[grounding: real-lesson (specificity), our-synthesis (the rest). Purdue OWL "Tips and Examples for Writing Thesis Statements" supports SPECIFIC ("a specific topic… avoid a thesis that is too broad") — that page does NOT contain the "arguable/others might dispute" language (per Unit1_L4_Source_Dossier G5). The arguable/refinement idea is sourced separately to RWT "not immediately self-evident" + UNC "Thesis Statements" (working-thesis-as-refinement). Cite each to its real page; do NOT attribute "arguable" to the Tips page.]`
- *Pair:* vague/topic-only ✗ ("This passage is about sleep") vs. controlling idea ✓ ("This passage explains that early start times work against teenage biology, costing measurable learning time").
- *Test (NEW example — D4/D2 conformance):* the student refines a working focus on a passage they have NOT yet worked (the pollinators passage), not the sleep one the pair modeled — so the move transfers rather than copies.

**Scaffold:** Medium — FACT shown as labels only (F/A/C/T, no descriptions); the fade has begun.

**Grading:** controlling-idea row of S-INF (evident→partial→developed). `[B: D1✓ (per-phrase citations corrected — Tips=specific only, arguable→RWT/UNC) · D2 — add a NEW-example test (see fix) · D3✓ D4✓ D5✓]`

## Lesson 1.5 — Bridge gate · Draft · Light→None · COLD

**No new move** — assembles 1.1-1.4 into a **short expository response-to-text**: controlling idea + 2 explained-evidence paragraphs + close. This is the unit's mastery gate (Activity Specs Tier 2/3, scaled to short-ECR).

**Gate conditions (Rubric A A7 / Activity Specs):**
- **No FACT fields, no scaffold, no tutorial** — FACT is now internal (the memorize-to-automaticity payoff).
- **Reserved passage** (C4 RESERVED, never shown in 1.1-1.4) — cold transfer.
- **Single attempt**, no revision loop on the gate (rescore-loop is practice-only).
- **Optional self-check:** "Want to check with FACT before submitting?" (tracked — tests internalization).

**Re-gate policy (clears Scorecard A's A7 CONDITIONAL):** a fail does not lock the student out; per the Alpha progression model, fail → targeted practice reps (with scaffold restored) → **a fresh reserved passage** for the re-attempt (never the same prompt — prevents memorizing the answer vs. the strategy). The gate activity itself does not re-route; it reports pass/fail + diagnostic-which-element-missing to the progression model.

**Grading:** full S-INF (Trait 1 ≥2 AND Trait 2 ≥1 to pass). Diagnostic feedback in FACT language ("strong F and A, but your C was missing on the second detail — you cited evidence without explaining how it supports your focus").
