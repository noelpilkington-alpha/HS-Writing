# C2 — Dual Standards Crosswalk (TEKS/STAAR English I & II + CCSS 9–10)

Per-lesson mapping of the C1 lesson map to **Texas TEKS English I (§110.36) /
English II (§110.37)** and **CCSS 9–10**. Purpose: prove the sequence covers the
STAAR-tested writing standards before C3 content is authored, and flag any
uncovered SE. Feeds C3 (content) and the dev team's test alignment (D3).

**Status:** v1 for review. TEKS SE text verified verbatim against the official TEA
Subchapter C PDF (`tea.texas.gov/.../ch110c-0.pdf`, adopted 2017 / amended 2019).
CCSS codes use the forms already in `Standards/HS_Writing_Standards_Mapping.md`.

## Citation forms

- **TEKS:** shorthand `EI.9(B)(ii)` / `EII.9(B)(ii)` (TEA's STAAR/TEKS form). The
  formal TAC cite adds subsection (c): `§110.36(c)(9)(B)(ii)`. Statement numbers are
  identical across EI/EII: **(4)** comprehension · **(5)** response · **(8)** author's
  purpose & craft · **(9)** writing process · **(10)** genres · **(11)** inquiry/research.
- **CCSS:** `9-10W.x` (writing), `9-10RI.1` (reading-informational, textual
  evidence), `9-10L.1–3` (language/conventions).

| Stmt | Strand | Key SEs this course uses |
|------|--------|--------------------------|

## Released-form mode evidence (what STAAR EI/EII *actually* sets)

Verified against **six released EI/EII answer keys** (2023/2024/2025, official TEA),
which tag each ECR to a TEKS SE:

| Form | ECR TEKS tag | Mode |
|------|-------------|------|
| 2023 EI | 10.B | informational composition |
| 2024 EI | **5.B** | analytical response-to-text |
| 2025 EI | 10.B | informational composition |
| 2023 EII (sampler) | **5.B** | analytical response-to-text |
| 2024 EII | 10.B | informational composition |
| 2025 EII | 10.B | informational composition |

**Three load-bearing facts for targeting:**
1. The actual ECR is always **informational composition (10.B)** or **analytical
   response-to-text (5.B)** — "read the passage, write an explanatory response with a
   controlling idea + explained textual evidence." This is exactly what the C1
   re-aim targets. ✅
2. **Argumentative (10.C) has NOT appeared on any released EI/EII ECR.** It is an
   *eligible* mode (TEA publishes an Argumentative/Opinion rubric for G6–E2) and is
   **ACT-aligned**, so the course keeps it — but it is a **secondary** STAAR target,
   not co-primary. **Weighting correction:** treat informational/analytical-response
   as the primary STAAR ECR target; argumentative serves ACT + the eligible-mode
   hedge. (EI Unit 3 stays, but the EI gate's "informational **or** argumentative"
   should read "informational/analytical-response (primary) or argumentative.")
3. **Two ECR rubrics exist (both 2-trait: Org & Development 0–3 + Conventions 0–2):**
   the **Informational** rubric (scores 10.B and 5.B responses) and the
   **Argumentative/Opinion** rubric (scores 10.C). **C5 must spec both**, with anchor
   papers — and weight the Informational rubric as primary. (URLs:
   `tea.texas.gov/.../tx-staar-informational-rubric-g6-e2.pdf` and
   `.../tx-staar-arg-opinion-rubric-g6-e2.pdf`.)

## Gaps & actions (for C3/C4 and the review)

1. **Conventions sub-SEs are bundled (◐), not each a dedicated move.** EI.9(D)(ii)–
   (vi) are taught/validated in the Polish checklist + the 4.4 revising/editing
   lesson rather than one lesson each. **Acceptable** — STAAR scores conventions
   holistically (Trait 2, error density), not SE-by-SE. **C3 action:** ensure the
   4.x conventions items + Polish checklists collectively touch tense/voice,
   agreement, capitalization, punctuation, spelling — and that **EII conventions
   adds parentheses** (the single EI→EII writing-process delta, 9(D)(v)).

2. **(10)(D) correspondence — RESOLVED, out of scope (⊘).** Investigated against
   official TEA sources (2026-06-11): the redesigned STAAR (2023+) EI/EII ECR sets
   **only informational or argumentative** modes (verbatim, 2023 EI scoring guide;
   2024 EI key = SE 5.B, 2024 EII key = SE 10.B informational). Correspondence
   appears **nowhere** on EI/EII — not the ECR, not the SCRs, not the editing items —
   and **no correspondence rubric exists at any grade**. The "write a letter" prompt
   was a pre-2023 retired-writing-test artifact, eliminated in the redesign. (10)(D)
   is a listed TEKS genre that the test never operationalizes. **No correspondence
   thread will be built.** Confidence: HIGH.

3. **Source evaluation (11)(E)/(G)(i) is light (◐).** Synthesis sources are
   *provided* (not located by the student), so (11)(E) is intentionally thin. But
   **(G)(i) credibility & bias** is STAAR-relevant and currently only implicit in
   5.1. **C3 action:** add an explicit "evaluate each source's credibility/bias/
   stance" beat to 5.1 (it already reads source stance — extend it).

4. **Annotation (5)(E) and citation/plagiarism (11)(H)** are present but implicit.
   **C3 action:** name them as explicit Setup/Draft beats (cheap; the reps already
   do them).

5. **Author's-craft (8) — RESOLVED: add a diction/tone beat to English II Polish.**
   The re-aim moved the course off rhetorical analysis, leaving (8)(F) tone/diction
   light. **Decision (2026-06-11):** the writing course owns it — **add a
   diction/tone-for-effect beat to the English II Polish stage** (not the Language
   course). C3 action: author a Polish-stage beat in the EII essay reps (esp. 6.x,
   7.x) on choosing diction/syntax for mood/voice/tone, mapped to EII.8(F). (8)(A)
   purpose/audience stays a light Setup touch.

## Net

Every **load-bearing** STAAR writing SE — (5)(C) text-evidence-plus-commentary,
(9)(B)(i)(ii) structure + depth, (9)(C) revise, (9)(D)(i) sentence control, (10)(B)
informational, (10)(C) argumentative, (11)(F) synthesis, (4)(H) synthesize texts —
is **covered with a dedicated teaching beat**. The remaining gaps are all either
(a) holistically-scored conventions sub-SEs that don't need per-SE lessons, or (b)
small "make it explicit in C3" items. **Both prior review decisions are now
resolved (2026-06-11):** (10)(D) correspondence is **out of scope** (verified not
assessed on STAAR EI/EII), and (8) diction/tone is **owned by the course** via an
added English II Polish beat. No open standards decisions remain.
